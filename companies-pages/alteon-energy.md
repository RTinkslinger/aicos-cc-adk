## Analysis by dev - 2025-11-21

### Company Snapshot
- Problem: Offshore naval visibility is currently limited, expensive, and labor-intensive
- Solution: Perpetual aerial surveillance using dynamic-soaring airplanes at under $1 per hour

### Product and Technology
- Core concept: airplanes performing dynamic soaring to harvest energy from wind, enabling continuous flight
- Specs: 6m wingspan, 15kg MTOW; propellers act as wind turbines
- Target endurance: ~417 days of continuous flight
- Target operating cost < $1/hr

### Economics
- Monitoring New York's coast every 10 minutes for about $52k/month
- Prior capex example ~$6.2k per airframe (flagged as outdated)
- For the cost of one Arleigh-Burke destroyer, >16,000 aircraft could be flown

### Risks
- Technical: Robust DS autonomy across variable wind fields, reliability, adverse-weather ops
- Operational: Airspace approvals, deconfliction, spectrum, C2, maintenance at scale
- Economics and GTM: Several figures are placeholders; no concrete customers
