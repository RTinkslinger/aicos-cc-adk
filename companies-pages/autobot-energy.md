Autobot Energy Overview

- Company Mission:

- Autobot Energy focuses on providing clean, cheap, and consistent energy through renewable energy sources, aiming to address energy consumption and generation challenges in India.

- The company aims to solve the energy flexibility problem: balancing energy generation during the day with consumption during the night, ensuring 24/7 availability of cheap, clean energy.

- They are focusing on the C&I (Commercial & Industrial) sector, which currently consumes a significant portion of non-green energy.

- Key Insights and Problem Areas:

- Energy Gap in India:

- India’s peak demand is around 250-270 gigawatts, and the country already has 500 GW installed capacity. However, there is a mismatch in generation and consumption as solar power is generated mostly during the day, and storage capacity is not sufficient to handle nighttime demand.

- Challenges in the Grid:

- There is a lack of consumer choice in energy providers, with most energy coming from government-controlled sources, and there’s a growing issue with grid instability as renewable generation increases.

- Energy Price Issues:

- C&I consumers are paying Rs 3-4 per unit for energy, which is significantly higher than the cost of renewable energy, creating a $10 billion savings opportunity if they transition to clean energy sources.

- However, small and medium-sized companies (SMEs) and MSMEs are unaware of the subsidies and potential cost savings and are often not served by renewable energy developers who are focusing on larger C&I clients.

Product and Solution Offering

- Phase 1:

- Energy Consumption Analysis: Autobot Energy uses meters to collect data on energy usage at client facilities (e.g., pharmaceutical and steel plants) and models how much energy can be saved by transitioning to renewable sources.

- Initial Services: Provide advisory services on renewable energy (RE) transitions, identifying the best setup for each facility, and providing savings estimates.

- Phase 2:

- Power Plant Setup: Once energy savings are calculated, Autobot Energy sets up solar, wind, and storage systems for customers, particularly C&I clients, at a minimum of 100 kW capacity.

- Revenue Streams:

- 2% commission on power plant setups.

- Per-unit charge for managing the dispatch of electricity between clients and the grid.

- Battery deployment as another revenue stream, taking higher margins from storage systems.

- Phase 3 (Future Plans):

- Smart Dispatch System: Development of a software platform that will allow factories to optimize energy usage in real-time, including battery management and trading of energy in IEX (Indian Energy Exchange).

- The platform will forecast energy consumption and generation, ensuring that energy is cost-efficient and reliable, automating the process of energy management for factories and businesses.

- Grid-to-Grid Trading:

- Autobot is also looking to enable peer-to-peer energy trading and grid-to-grid exchange to help address energy imbalances and provide a more distributed model for energy generation and consumption.

Market and Customer Strategy

- Target Market:

- Primarily targeting the C&I sector, especially SMEs, which are currently underserved in the renewable energy space.

- They are also working with larger companies and RE developers to aggregate energy demand from smaller facilities to make renewable energy adoption more scalable.

- Challenges in Market:

- Awareness Gap: Many SMEs do not know about government subsidies or how to transition to renewable energy.

- High Costs and Low Awareness: There is a lack of education on the benefits of renewable energy, and initial setup costs (e.g., for solar power) are a barrier for many small companies.

- Customer Acquisition:

- Autobot Energy is using associations (e.g., Manufacturing Associations and Textile Associations) to connect with potential customers.

- They are in talks with pharmaceutical and steel plants in Punjab and other regions to test and deploy their solutions.

Business Model and Financials

- Revenue Model:

- Commission: 2% on the value of the solar/wind power plants set up.

- Per-unit Charges: For managing energy dispatch and trading.

- Battery Deployment: Taking margins on battery installations for storage solutions.

- CapEx and Investment:

- The company has divided its strategy into phases and is looking to raise ₹2 crores for further expansion.

- Funds will primarily go toward software development, customer acquisition, and setting up energy systems for early clients.

Product Development and Timeline

- Technology Development:

- Modeling Software: To calculate energy needs, savings, and the best deployment of renewable resources (solar, wind, storage).

- Smart Dispatch: For real-time energy management, using AI algorithms to optimize energy flow and trading.

- Commercialization Timeline:

- The company is currently focusing on Phase 1 (energy audits and advisory) and aims to scale to Phase 2 (installation and setup of renewable energy systems) by mid-2025.

- Phase 3 (full-scale smart dispatch and grid-to-grid systems) is expected to be rolled out by 2026.

Fundraising and Future Outlook

- They plan to use these funds to set up pilot projects, refine their modeling and dispatch systems, and grow their team.

- Long-term Vision:

- Build a smart energy marketplace with integrated renewable energy sources and battery storage, along with AI-driven optimization for real-time energy management.
