## October ‘25 Investor Update

Quick update as we head into the remaining quarter of the year. We’ve sharpened our positioning and expanded our customer base, working with publicly traded companies — all while zeroing on becoming the best mobile QA solution out there.

Below you’ll find recent progress since YC, learnings, our goals, and our asks :)

Progress since YC:

- In August we had ~$130K ARR, that is now ~$250K ARR with ~$2-2.5M in the pipeline. Working with teams like Fetch Rewards, YC mobile team, [solidcore], and Scribd and many more.

- We've been doing a POC with DoorDash (~1 month in), which we hope to turn into a pilot ($15-20K/month), which when converted to a complete contract will have an ACV of $500K - $1M (estimate).

- In the process of setting up pilots with AAA, 23andMe, Autonation and potentially Pinterest and Rocket Money. Each of these would be $250K-1M in ACV. Exploring if we would fit in heavily as an enterprise solution and maybe switching focus to that. Keeping growth momentum on both ends in the meantime.

- Released an experimental caching feature, boosting agent speed by 5-8x per action (tap, swipe, enter text, etc.).

- Brought on another engineer who we hope to join us full-time in December (extremely talented, amazing person, & wrapping up his last semester of college). Our team is now at 4 people (2 founders, 1 founding engineer, 1 part-time engineer)!

- Cranking on growth: outbound, linkedin presence, case studies, GEO, podcasts, getting creative with it and having fun with it, and relaunching next week (would love your guys' linkedin support on that when it's out!).

Recent Learnings:

- Speed is now a priority–our focus during YC was making our agents reliable, and we’ve crushed that–now it's about making them faster

- A consistent thing we've heard from customers is that "this is the only solution that actually works" -- we've had companies come from competitors complaining about the terrible quality and the extreme shortcomings of their products -- we will keep this lead.

- We have 2 main use cases: For smaller teams, Autosana is a standalone QA Engineer. For the bigger teams, they have a few people using Autosana to 10x their work. We are exploring both and for larger companies we are exploring a seat-based pricing approach.
