## Ref from Kristen Vosmaer, Managing Director at JLL, regarding their ongoing pilot with Boost Robotics.

Meeting Notes

Assessment of Boost’s Founders & Approach

- He rated both Hardik and Hans highly on their software sophistication.

- Standout vs. others: more intuitive software‑first design and nuanced approach to task handling (e.g., precise force detection for delicate fiber optic handling).

- As articulated by him:

Market & Tipping Point

- Believes data centers are not yet at the robotics adoption tipping point seen in logistics but getting closer.

- Key driver will be scale growth in data centers - campuses getting much larger.

- Even in labor‑abundant environments, robots can offer throughput, processing capability, and consistency humans cannot match.

- Comparison with logistics: wide open layouts in warehouses enable easier robotics deployment; data centers have segmented architecture, multiple security layers, and physical access constraints.

Nature of Data Center Tasks

- Many tasks are manipulation‑oriented and bespoke, not easily standardized into software or a single robotic platform.

- Server changes vary in process and complexity; layouts and engineering evolve quickly across builds.

- Frequent generational design changes mean robots must adapt to new environments.

- Human labor introduces higher error risk at scale (turnover, fatigue, night shift issues impacting uptime).

Challenges for Robotics Adoption

- Major hurdle: access control - robots moving between secure rooms/zones in a compliant way.

- Economics: a $50k robot must justify cost vs. flexible human labor who can handle multiple tasks.

- Data halls, security doors, and layout constraints complicate robot navigation.

- Need to solve multi‑task capability – single‑task robots have poor ROI in this environment.

Opportunities & Pilot with Boost

- Boost currently focusing on “white space” tasks - starting simpler before tackling more complex manipulations.

- Boost is working with them on:

- Potential trial environments: crypto mining facility, traditional data centers, and decommissioning scenarios (lower risk).

- For adoption, access control + rounds/readings is the critical first win; would accelerate deployment even if other tasks aren’t ready.

Competitive Landscape & Prior Exploration

- Has explored other robotics solutions: drones, roving “dogs,” security robots, cleaning robots (simpler tasks).

- All competitors face same access/navigation in secure data center challenge.

- Familiar with Bright Machines – sees their focus more on fabrication than install; market seems limited to hyperscalers, with TAM questions.

Adoption Horizon & Economics

- For JLL specifically, robotics impact will be a longer‑term play (couple of years) given labor‑based revenue model.

- Hyperscalers may adopt faster due to OPEX reduction and uptime focus.

- In India context: robotics could improve knowledge transfer and quality control to match global hyperscaler standards, mitigating costly outages.

Success Criteria for Pilot to Convert to Full Contract

- Demonstrated seamless access control navigation.

- Reliable autonomous rounds/readings during staff emergency redeployment.

- Proof that robots can complement rather than replace human teams in near term, while showing clear cost/uptime benefits.
