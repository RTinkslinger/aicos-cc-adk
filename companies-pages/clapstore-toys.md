Detailed Meeting Notes (23.10.25)

Founder Background

- Vedang (22), Psychology major; started Clapstore in 2020 during COVID lockdown while in 11th–12th grade

- First-time founder from a family of educators (mother: govt college lecturer, father: tuition teacher); driven by desire to be first in family to build a business and have autonomy

- Initially aspired to join defense services; COVID shifted focus to entrepreneurship—started with dropshipping via TikTok (US market), scaled to ₹6–7L per month before facing supplier scams

- Transitioned from dropshipping to buying from manufacturers and managing own packaging/dispatch; later evolved into designing and manufacturing products after discovering success with non-screen toys

Business Model & Evolution

- Initial traction from a busy board toy gifted to a neighbor’s kid; child did not use screen for hours, validating “screen-free play” demand

- Pivoted to screen-free, interactive wooden + electronic toys (Busy Boards), iterated 15+ times based on customer feedback

- Manufacturing moved from home-based 10 units/day to semi-integrated setup: woodworks (Mumbai/Gujarat), switches (China/Delhi), assembly + quality contro

- Core focus: educational toys that reduce screen time; iterative product innovation using parent community insight

Consumer Insights

- Parents in Tier 1 are consciously cutting screen-time post-COVID, seeking engaging, durable, learning-focused toys

- Kids engage 30–60 mins per session with Busy Boards; parents notice lower mobile dependency

- Parents value sensory stimulation, safety, sound interaction, and progressive learning themes (e.g., police car version with siren, solar system board with planets)

- Community of 200+ parents co-creates new products; parents rated weekly on engagement + insight; get access to early prototypes and free samples

- Trust-building through radical transparency about product changes, issues, and safety improvements

Traction & Unit Economics

- Monthly revenue: ₹90L → ₹1.01Cr → ₹1.10Cr → ₹1.22Cr (last 4 months)

- Category EBITDA positive for ~4 months; average 2–6%

- Gross Margin: 65%; CM3: 15–17%

- Channel mix: D2C (50%), Amazon (35%), Q-commerce (10%), others (5%)

- ASP: ₹1,200 (website), ₹900 (marketplaces)

- Cost breakdown: 35–45% marketing (Meta omni), 9% commission + logistics, 10% fixed costs

- Fully bootstrapped until ~₹5L; raised ₹1.4Cr (₹1Cr Kishore Biyani, ₹40L WTF Fund)

Supply Chain & Backend

- Wooden parts manufactured in Mumbai + Gujarat; sanded & cut in Pune

- Switches: 4/6 imported from China, 2/6 sourced from Delhi

- Four manufacturing divisions: cutting, sanding, assembly, QC

- 30K+ Busy Boards sold; 15 design updates made based on parent feedback (e.g., LEDs replaced with safer knobs, durability and sound improvements)

Capital & Ownership

- Initial personal investment: ₹4–5L; external raised ₹1.4Cr (₹1Cr from Kishore Biyani, ₹40L from WTF)

- Currently raising ₹10Cr at ₹50Cr valuation; ₹5Cr hard-committed (WTF, Kishore, Raj Shamani, Nikhil etc.)

- Cap table: Founders 70%, Kishore 10%, Raj 2.5%, Nikhil 2.5%

Next Phase Plans

- Target: ₹100Cr revenue in 2–2.5 years; ₹8Cr in FY27

- Vision: 60% India, 40% US in near term; global-first longer term

- North Star: build an Indian Skillmatics-style brand with 20–30 iterative SKUs per category, feedback loop-powered product innovation, high gross margins

- Hero category: Busy Boards; expansion into STEM toys (e.g., DIY electronics cars); all wood + electronics, no plastic

- US expansion: 300-unit pilot shipped → ~60–70 units sold in weeks; capital now for inventory scale + deeper US distribution

Product Pipeline & Innovation

- All toys built through consumer-backward feedback loops (parent community + child observation)

- New categories: STEM-based remote control vehicles (designed for 13+ age group), high-durability sensory boards, phonics boards

- AI-led plush toy prototype (suggested by Kishore Biyani) targeted at Tier 2/3 users—blends devotional/interactive stories and culture (e.g., Dada/Dadi doll telling stories)Pricing ~₹4–5K with optional monthly subscription (~₹500–1,000) for dynamic content; intended as affordable AI toy alternative

Detailed Q&A Shared During Call

- Why start toy brand? Wanted to solve screen-time addiction in kids through active, mindful, screen-free play

- Why US market? Electronics-led toys with cultural nuance missing; China-dominated back-end lacks UX and emotional context

- How are products tested? Community of 200+ parents; systematic feedback loop → product refinements before launch

- What’s the moat? Innovation velocity + high trust + customer-led NPD + culturally relevant content

- Why consider AI? Mentor suggestion + white space in Tier 2/3 for affordable interactive toys

- Main concerns right now? Balancing focus between build-out of core Busy Board line and multiple experimental SKUs

- Business risks? Spreading too thin too early; missing brand clarity; manufacturing consistency during scale

- What defines the brand’s soul? Enabling healthier cognitive and emotional development in kids by reducing passive screen time and encouraging tactile, curiosity-driven play

### One-page summary (what MPI is saying)

This memo argues toys is becoming investable now because a historically unattractive India toys market (small TAM, fragmented categories, China-dominant supply) is being reshaped by regulation + shifting supply chains + proven “India-to-US” go-to-market.

### Why toys was not exciting historically

- Small India TAM: ~$1.5B India market versus $110B global; India is ~1% of global. Domestic scale has been hard (e.g., incumbents stuck around INR 100–300Cr revenue).

- Fragmented category + SKU complexity: Many sub-categories, high SKU count, and constant new product development. “Hero SKU” concentration is limited even for global brands.

- China manufacturing superiority: China supplies ~60%+ of global toy exports and historically ~80% of India’s domestic consumption, with better quality, scale, and ecosystem.
