**Market Landscape**

- Instant Noodles Market (India):

  - Size: $2B+.
  - Dominated by Maggi (Nestlé), Sunfeast Yippee (ITC) etc
  - Noodle Market growing 15% annually, and Korean noodles growing at +40% over the last 3 years

- Korean/International Ramen Sub-Segment:

  - Current size: $200M, growing at ~40% CAGR.
  - Imported SKUs priced at ₹100–120/packet.
  - Indian efficiency brands (local, lower Indianized quality) priced at ₹50–55/packet.
  - Headroom: Indian ramen consumption riding Korean cultural wave (K-pop, K-dramas). Comparable to how Chinese cuisine took 20 years, Korean will be happening in next 3–5 years.

- Category Trends:

  - International cuisines (Mexican, Korean, Japanese) expected to reach ₹4,000–5,000 Cr combined in 5–7 years.
  - Ramen can be start and want to expand to Intl cuisine

**Product & Consumer Insights**

Consumer Insights from the survey at IIMA

- Existing options in market is missing the authentic taste and texture of ramen

- People don’t wanna eat cup noodles because see it as unhealthy, and wanna cook but not v complicated cooking so basic noodle, water, and veggie is the right level.

- Taste Profile:

  - Benchmarked against Nongshim Kimchi (one of the best in Korea)
  - Blind tests with IIMA/IIT students, multiple iteration to get to the right stage
  - once ramen are tried by consumer, our conversion rate is 25%. So, if anyone has ever tried, 1 in 4 will buy us.
  - Flavor notes: mild chilli, tanginess (kimchi), umami (soy sauce + MSG).
  - Avoided “Indian fusion masala” flavoring (taken by ITC/HUL in Korean category with <15% combined share).

- Consumer Segments:

  - Tier-1 youth/urban millennials – early adopters.
  - Tier-2/3 aspirational consumers – following trend via Flipkart/Amazon.
  - Ramen seen as better than Maggi, but less effort than cooking full meal. And consumer want to cook meal bcz they think that’s healthier but also want v low effort

**Manufacturing & Supply Chain**

- Current Setup:

  - Sourcing from local manufacturer in Roorkee (linked via family contacts) – agreed to kind of first manu
  - Current COGS = ₹17.5/packet (25% of MRP which is Rs.70). Includes raw materials + inbound logistics.

- Raw Material Cost Drivers:

  - Oil – biggest area where economies of scale can kick. Economies of scale kick in when buying tanker level. Bulk procurement reduces cost by 10–15% to Rs.13.5 (which is 25% of Rs.50, so if can reduce cost to 13.5, can price it as Rs.50-55, in-line with GT alternatives like maggi etc)
  - Starch – 4x costlier than wheat flour (used in ramen, unlike Maggi) – so Rs.2-3 higher than
  - Vegetables – dehydrated inputs add ~₹3–4/packet.

- Scale Potential:

  - With 10 days/month production utilization → COGS can drop to ₹13/packet.
  - At ₹13 COGS and ₹55 ASP → 75% gross margin potential.

- Capex for Own Plant:

  - Setup cost: ₹2–2.5 Cr (machinery).
  - Excl. land (can lease).
  - Manufacturer-level value capture only 5-10% of MRP; hence vertical Integration not v big upside

**Unit Economics**

- MRP: ₹70.

- ASP (average realized): ₹50–55.

- COGS (current): ₹17.5.

- Gross Margin: ~65% at ASP.

- Scale target: bring COGS down to ₹13 → 75% gross margin.

- Category Benchmark:

  - Maggi: <5% net margin, volume-led.
  - Premium Ramen (ITC/HUL SKUs): ~15–18% margins.
  - Gimi Michi: positioned as premium but accessible (half of imported ramen price), so can get 15-18% net margin at scale

**Distribution & GTM**

  - Quick Commerce:

  - E-Commerce:

    - Offline accounts for ~70% retail consumption today, so acc to Nishank should go there

  - Long-term: build into ₹500–1,000 Cr international cuisine platform.
