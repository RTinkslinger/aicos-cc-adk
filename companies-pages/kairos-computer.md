Here's a summary of the Kairos Computer deck:

**Kairos Computer** is a startup that automates tasks through screen recording. Users can record themselves completing a task once while explaining it, and Kairos will handle it automatically thereafter. No coding or drag-and-drop required - it works like training a co-worker.

**Value Proposition:**

- Reduces time spent on repetitive tasks

- Automates workflows requiring company-specific knowledge and nuance

- Uses video to capture workflow details naturally

- Requires no coding or manual workflow setup

**Current Progress:**

- 4,300 waitlist signups from Twitter

- Prototype developed

- Use cases include invoice processing, candidate screening, and email handling

**Team:** Led by CEO (previously at Sarvam and Delphi) and CTO (previously at Google and Delphi)

**Investment:** Currently raising with commitments from Afore VC FIR ($100k), Ahead VC ($100k), Venky Karnam ($10k), Switch VC ($25k), DeVC ($30k), and angel investors.

### Notion AI Meeting Notes





This meeting featured Manas discussing their journey founding Kairos, an AI agent platform designed to function like a digital employee.

### Background and Journey

Manas developed an early interest in technology, building a Play Store app in summer 2014 and being introduced to machine learning through Andrew NG lectures in 2017. After attending NUS, Manas worked at Delphi (connected through Aryan Sharma of Induced AI) and later joined Sarvam AI through cold outreach.

Manas was fired from Delphi for dedicating time to side projects, which ultimately led to founding Kairos in February 2025. When deciding between Sarvam and working on an AI pendant, Manas chose Sarvam, noting that AI hardware like pendants faced significant challenges compared to established devices like Apple Watch or Meta Rayban glasses.

### Kairos: Vision and Philosophy

Kairos was built on several key observations:

- Model intelligence is no longer the bottleneck; deployment is

- Businesses struggle to effectively implement AI

- AI should function like a "drop-in remote worker"

- AGI requires real-world data

Manas had a realization that video captures user intent better than text, as humans struggle with detailed text prompting. This insight was reinforced by Google's Gemini video mode.

### Product Evolution

The initial Kairos prototype had significant limitations:

- Only allowed screen sharing with no text interface

- Limited 15-minute execution windows

- Confined to workflows of dependent tasks

The new vision is to create an AI agent that:

- Is location-agnostic (accessible via Slack, mobile, email)

- Doesn't require screen sharing for workflow execution

- Acts more human-like, asking for help when stuck

A demo of this concept received viral attention with nearly 6,000 tweets.

### Current Status and Strategy

Kairos currently has:

- Two interns working on the product

- $700K in funding

- A substantial customer waitlist

Despite the trend toward vertical AI agents (specialized for specific industries), Manas is pursuing a horizontal approach due to lacking domain expertise. Manas mentioned that OpenAI is doing $10M contracts with vertical agent companies, with those companies betting they can reach market faster than OpenAI.
