### Company Background

- Founded by IIT Bombay graduate with 20 years of experience in US mortgage banking industry 

- Founder has expertise across business and technology, having built organizations from small operations to 3,000-4,000 person scale 

- Previous experience includes building loan origination systems, mortgage banking functions, and growing VisionNet's business from sub-$3M to $60M 

- Logicality was founded after recognizing opportunity to build scalable institutional business in mortgage industry 

### Problem Statement

- US mortgage market is massive: $15 trillion ecosystem with 6.5 million loans originated annually 

- Despite size, industry is plagued by inefficiencies: 45-day loan closing times with numerous manual iterations 

- Cost of loan production has risen from $8,600 to $12,600 per loan over last 5 years, despite increased technology adoption 

- 45% of production cost ($5,000-$6,000 per loan) is back office operations cost  

- Technology solutions have been point solutions rather than integrated back office approach 

- Underwriting process involves multiple iterations between underwriter, processor, and loan officer tables, adding significant time delays   

### Key Market Differences (US vs India)

- US has active secondary market and servicing market, unlike India where lenders hold loans in portfolio 

- 90% of US loans are sold to secondary market, which then sells servicing rights to servicers 

- This creates more complexity but allows market expansion 

### Solution Overview

- AI-powered platform that automates mortgage workflow processes with modular approach  

- **Data Agent**: Fetches, stacks, and classifies documents; performs missing page and document checks at point of ingestion  

- **Underwriting Agent**: Automates underwriting decisions, generates conditions, provides audit trail; can handle income calculations from multiple sources  

- **QC Agents**: Performs post-underwriting QC, post-closing QC, and investor-specific QC  

- **Queue Agent**: Handles workflow management 

### Value Proposition

- Can reduce underwriter workload from 2 files per day to 8-9 files per day  

- Reduces underwriting conditions from average of 30 down to 4-5 

- Can cut back office operations cost from $6,000 to $2,000-$3,000 per loan 

- Validates conditions at point of file ingestion, eliminating many downstream iterations  

- For investor sales: Determines best investor for file sale and performs QC to meet investor requirements, reducing sale time from 20-45 days to one-fourth  

- Built with complete auditability to meet ReadyMap requirements (implementing April 1st/March 1st)   

### Technology & Integration

- Flexible integration options: API for real-time or batch integration via JSON/XML for overnight processing 

- Can integrate with existing LOS/LMS systems without requiring front-end changes  

- Built on standard underwriting guidelines (Fannie, Freddy, Ginnie Mae, investor guidelines) with ability to add custom conditions  

- Handles both structured and unstructured data, including handwritten documents 

- Designed to work with legacy systems like ICE Mortgage's Encompass (which holds 50% market share)  

### Target Market & Pricing

- Initial focus on mid-market segment (60% of market): lenders processing 1,000-10,000 loans per month 

- These customers spend approximately $6 million per month on operating costs 

- After first year, will approach large banks like BNC and others 

- Modular pricing model: per-file or per-calculation basis depending on modules used 

- Land-and-expand strategy: start with 1-2 modules addressing biggest friction points, then expand 

- Pricing positioned as operating cost (not IT cost), directly tied to 45% of business cost 

- US market has cleaner payment dynamics compared to India, with less price negotiation once value is demonstrated  

### Current Status

- Document processing and data extraction (most complex piece) is solved and demoed to customers 

- Validation engine and calculations engines are built 

- Currently building QC engine and underwriting engine 

- In pilot conversation stage with 3-4 customers
