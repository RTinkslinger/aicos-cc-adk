### Claude Summary (Good Read)

**Company Overview**

AI-first gene editing platform developing hypercompact gene editors as alternatives to CRISPR-Cas9, targeting agriculture, therapeutics, and biomanufacturing.

**The Opportunity**

- CRISPR's Limitations: Large size (~1,300 amino acids) complicates delivery, complex IP landscape ($100M+ licensing), and PAM sequence restrictions limit targeting

- Agriculture Bottleneck: Tissue culture takes 80% of time in gene editing; India has gene-edited crops with 20%+ yield improvement but can't commercialize due to licensing issues

- Market: Gene editing market growing from $9.78B (2024) to $25B (2030), agriculture seed market $150B+

**The Solution**

Building AI protein design platform to engineer Mobile Genetic Elements (MGEs) - naturally occurring editors that are:

- 60-70% smaller than Cas9 (400-500 amino acids vs 1,300)

- Easier to deliver via AAV vectors

- Cleaner IP - less crowded patent landscape

- Challenge: Low efficiency (~20%) which AI will optimize to >50%

**First Product: TnpB Editor**

- Tissue culture-free gene editing for agriculture (cuts time by 80%)

- TAM-flexible targeting (broader genome coverage)

- 8-10 month timeline to validation

**Technology Platform (4 Pillars)**

1. Data Pipeline: Mining public genomic / metagenomic datasets + proprietary wet-lab data

1. AI Model Stack: Protein language models (PLMs) fine-tuned on gene editors

1. In-Silico Screening: Filter millions of candidates to top hundreds

1. High-Throughput Wet Lab: Partner labs now, own semi-automated lab post-seed

**Team - World Class**

- Dr. Kutubuddin Molla (CSO): #1 in plant gene editing in India, pioneered TnpB in agriculture, Senior Scientist at National Rice Research Institute

- Sourab Kulkarni: Ex-Metagenomi (first prime editing hire), Kira Bio, Acrobat Genomics. Patent holder on gene editing systems

- Founder: Lossfunk AI Residency alumnus, AgTech background (Absolute - $500M biotech), built AI tool that outperformed SOTA (Biomni, FutureHouse)

- Advisors: Dr. KC Bansal (former NBPGR Director, architect of India's gene editing policy), Dr. Vijay Chandru (Strand Life Sciences founder), Paras
Chopra (Lossfunk founder, investing $25K)

**Business Model**

Licensing platform + IP assets:

- Agriculture: License to seed companies, ag-biotech (precedent: tens of millions USD upfront + 5-10% sales)

- Therapeutics: License to CAR-T, gene therapy companies focusing on sickle cell, inherited diseases

- Biomanufacturing: Large gene insertion capabilities

**Traction & Validation**

- Backed by NASSCOM, NVIDIA Inception, AWS Activate, Google for Startups, Microsoft for Startups (~$500K compute credits)

- $22K non-dilutive grants from Emergent Ventures & Upsurge

- Dr. Kutub validated founder's AI tool on 5-year research problem - ranked correct answer in top 3

- AI tool ([ai.mandrakebio.com](http://ai.mandrakebio.com/)) already live and functional

**Competitive Edge**

- AI-first approach vs biology-first competitors

- Proof-of-concept: Profluent's OpenCRISPR-1 (AI-designed Cas9) secured partnerships with Revvity & Corteva

- Speed: Moving from years to months through AI-guided design

- Data moat: Custom datasets + rapid iteration loop

**The Ask**

$1M Pre-Seed (8-10 months)

- Deliverable: Experimentally validated TAM-flexible TnpB with >50% efficiency

- Use of funds: Team, compute, consumables

- Next: $2-3M Seed to establish semi-automated wet lab (led by Dr. Kutub)

**Key Risks & Mitigation**

1. Technical: All milestones have precedent (published studies confirm feasibility)

1. IP: AI-generated proteins with >20% sequence difference are patentable (OpenCRISPR precedent)

1. Market / Commercialization: Partnering with Dr. Kutub (established relationships), targeting proven commercial targets

Why Now

- Inflection point: AI can remove trial-and-error from biotech (what took 5-7 years now takes months)

- Sovereign opportunity: India needs these tools for food security

- Platform play: Each editor becomes its own licensable asset across multiple verticals

---

Bottom Line: Mandrake is building the foundational AI platform for next-generation gene editors with cleaner IP, better delivery, and faster development cycles. First application solves an immediate Indian agriculture need while proving the platform for global therapeutics and ag-biotech licensing deals.
