**Current Status of the App**

- Building an AI multi-companion app where users interact with multiple AI personas (friend, spiritual guide, business coach, god persona, etc.).

- Core thesis: Bharat users prefer multiple contextual relationships (for emotion, spirituality, curiosity) over one deep AI friend — reflecting offline social behavior.

- Active characters: 4–5 personas — *Astro, Spiritual, Religious, Friend, Business*; Astro has *God and Astrologer companion*

- God as companion is little tricky and need to make sure it doesn’t show any random stuff

- Key metrics:

  - DAU: ~1,000 WAU: ~3,000
  - D7 retention: 21–22 %; D15 retention: internal north-star
  - Most engagement occurs in-app; WhatsApp and web pilots ongoing to test onboarding funnels.

- Funding: Raised $2.5 M at a $12 M post-money valuation; burning moderately monthly with a lean team.

**Experiments and Learnings**

- Users engage with multiple companions — sharing different life contexts (emotional, career, spiritual).

- Cross-companion introductions drive virality — 60 % CTR when one AI introduces another vs 10 % CTR for cold introductions.

- AI age effect: younger-sounding agents elicit higher imagination and empathy; shown to increase activation.

- Category self-selection: Users gravitate toward categories reflecting real-life emotional routines (friends, spiritual guides, etc.)

- Most of the folks in TG – don’t even know what AI can do; so they start with talking as friend and then they realise that AI can do Astro as; so they dive deep into Astro

- Multi-companion engagement leads to 2.5–3× more weekly sessions than single-companion setups.

**Product Behaviour and Consumer Insights**

- Bharat users mimic real-world communication - preferring 2–3 AI relationships of moderate emotional depth over one confiding bond.

- Astro and spiritual personas drive the strongest engagement; transactional agents (e.g., task or city utilities) fade faster.

- Cultural behaviour: Semi-anonymous chat makes it easier for Tier 2–3 users to express emotions; emotional venting happens without judgment.

- Session depth: Multi-companion users have ~2.5× session frequency vs. single-companion users; emotional satisfaction is distributed across personas.

**Monetization Approach**

- Go-live timeline: Nov–Dec 2025 for text interactions; voice monetization to follow later.

- Initial paid use cases: High-context categories (Astro/spiritual) likely to monetize first, with users willing to pay for personalized experiences.

- Planned monetization layers:

  - Text-based microtransactions or subscriptions.
  - Premium category upgrades (e.g., *Astro+*, *Business Mentor+*).
  - Commerce and affiliate integrations later for brand tie-ins.

- Philosophy: Treat monetization as a feature built alongside engagement. Early introduction of paid tiers helps test elasticity and filters serious users.

**Detailed Discussion on AI companion space**

- Founders debated whether users form stronger bonds with one AI or multiple AIs.

  - Data by Mello favors multiple companions, as users open up differently with each persona.
  - Emotional expression is context-dependent — e.g., sharing career anxiety with one and relationship stress with another.
  - Single deep relationships create higher D30 retention but fewer DAU; multi-companion networks increase overall engagement and total minutes spent.

- On how this drives retention and monetization, multi-companion users exhibit 2.5–3× longer session times and higher LTV.

  - Retention loops strengthen because users revisit for different moods and needs.
  - Monetization experiments will begin in Astro and Spiritual categories, where users already perceive guidance as a value service.

- On differentiation from other AI companion apps, the moat comes from local emotional context and inter-companion graph.

  - Western AI apps (e.g., Replika) rely on one romantic partner model; this product mimics India’s multi-social dynamic.
  - The AI referral loop (60 % CTR) mimics organic friend introductions, enhancing discovery and retention.
  - Companions also speak in local tone, humor, and mixed Hindi-English — creating strong cultural resonance.

- On long-term defensibility once voice agents commoditize, the moat lies in context memory and social graph.

  - Each user forms a personal AI ecosystem — leaving means losing an emotionally familiar network.
  - Companions remember events, cross-reference stories, and talk about each other, deepening perceived intelligence and loyalty.

- On Bharat user depth vs. U.S. benchmarks, founders note India is currently ~50 % of U.S. engagement depth but rising fast.

  - With increasing comfort in digital expression, usage time is expected to converge with global norms within 12–18 months.

- On company vision, the goal is to build a Bharat-native AI social ecosystem.

  - The product aims to replicate India’s plural social world — one where people engage with 4–6 emotional personas daily for friendship, spirituality, and guidance.
  - Long-term, these relationships will form the emotional and conversational layer of the Indian internet, serving as a foundation for later monetization through services, commerce, and content.
