### AI notes





### Product and Market

- The company's AI platform targets pre-construction management, specifically focusing on the scoping and costing phase (second of four pre-construction phases) 

- The platform automates three key workflows for pre-construction managers:

  - Scope sheets: analyzing thousands of pages of documentation 
  - Bid leveling: comparing subcontractor bids (typically 2-3 weeks of work) 
  - Price benchmarking: historical and market price comparisons 

- The product positions as an operating system sitting on top of point solutions rather than competing with them  

- Main competitor is spreadsheets, not other construction tech tools 

### Business Metrics & Growth

- Current customer base: 6 customers 

- Revenue growth: ~100K ARR to ~400K ARR in approximately 1.5 months 

- Current pricing: Seat-based model ($60-65K ACV), limiting customers to 3 users initially  

- Future pricing strategy: Moving toward percentage revenue model like Procore and Autodesk 

- Sales cycle: 3-4 weeks average in mid-market (fastest deal was 2 days)  

- Deployment timeline: 4-6 weeks average 

### Go-to-Market Strategy

- Initial outreach: LinkedIn cold outreach, now adding emails 

- Next phase: Adding SDRs and implementing cold calling 

- Target market focus: Lower mid-market and mid-mid market  

- Enterprise approach: Opportunistic engagement with 4 enterprise prospects, with one verbal commitment secured 

- Value proposition: Increasing pre-construction manager productivity from 3-4 projects to 6-8 projects in parallel 

### Future Growth & Vision

- Market potential: Estimated path to $30-35 million revenue within 2-3 years just within mid-market 

- Long-term strategy: Focus on lower mid-market for next 2 years, then shift to upper mid-market and enterprise 

- Distribution evolution: Introduce value-added resellers for lower mid-market segment 

- Positioning: Aiming to be the central AI infrastructure platform for construction industry, similar to Procore's position in SaaS 

### Investor Discussion

- Investment timing: "Right now is the time where we would feel like money could be really helpful" 

- VC partnership value-add discussed:

  - Focus on "product to business journey" 
  - Support with go-to-market strategy 
  - Recruitment assistance both in US and India  
  - Network of 70+ founder operators as resources 

- VC thesis on construction tech: Interest in founders with industry experience disrupting "sleepy industries" with outdated software
