## From Anurag 2025-03-19 

Rohit reached out as they are again in the market. Also heard that they have a working prototype in place now, hence did a BRC (avg.) and met briefly

<u>Overall:</u> Continues to be a Pass – both on founder and market/revenue risk

- Avg. BRC on overall capabilities (Felt very corporatish), but good technical know-how

- Founder is scientist profile, not thinking of revenue and commercialization is still a year off.

- No revenue pipeline even for the Industrial diesel genset replacement - which is the first use case they have decided to go after

- Didn’t see founder compounding

- Progress they have made in the last 7 – 8 months is the technology risk is now taken out to a large extent and protype of the micro gas turbine has been tested and certified

- No real L-1 team, but have made good use of IISC faculties and facilities

<u>BRC on Rohit and Arjun from </u>[Sandip S.](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sandipsonar/)<u> – Capability lead at Rolls Royce</u>

<u>Context:</u> Sandip was the program/BD counterpart for Rohit at Rolls Royce, Arjun reported into Rohit, so interacted with him as well

<u>Overall:</u> Positive on technical capabilities and ability to deliver for both, but lukewarm on commercial and business skills + flexibility on requirements

<u>What did you like about working with Rohit and Arjun?</u>

- Very thorough in their work – Rohit esp. put in a lot of emphasis on quality

- Can trust Rohit and team to deliver high quality output (although not necessarily always on time)

- Very clear in their communications and highlighting challenges early to all stakeholders

<u>What are their Areas of improvement?</u>

- Rohit needs to understand business and commercial side of things better

- Best tech solution may not always be the right answer in different situations

- Arjun gets very technical in conversations. Difficult to explain tradeoffs

- Project management not Rohit’s strongest suit esp. financial aspects

<u>Thoughts on Nabhdrishti</u>

- Good that they are doing it – have the technical skills

- Prob. They are solving is not new – Gas turbine tech is old, miniatization is more novel, but multiple companies incl. RR and GE have commercial models for that already

- The biggest challenge for them will be to find customers – all aerospace and defence companies and large O&G will prefer working with the RRs and GEs of the world.

<u>Rate them as compared to others in Rolls Royce</u>

- Don’t know their actual rating, but Technically both will be in top quartile

- Business acumen wise have seen better teams
