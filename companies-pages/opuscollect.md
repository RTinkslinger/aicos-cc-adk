## Analysis by dev - 2026-01-08
  ### Company Snapshot
  Problem & solution
OpusCollect positions itself as an AI-first full-stack collections platform aimed at moving from pre-delinquency to hard-bucket collections, addressing a market with fragmented pan-India field operations and little scale in field-based collections. Market notes emphasize a gap in pan-India field collectors and a shift toward AI-enabled, data-driven recovery processes for banks and NBFCs.

Ideal customer profile and pricing
Direct clients include Unity Bank, Jana Bank, Avanti Finance, BikeBazaar, Branch, with additional through-partner channels (Fusion, ESAF, Cashe). Total assets under management ~₹900 crore; monthly collections ~₹6 crore. Pricing signals:
- Take rates reported as 10% regular, 15% SMA, 20% for 90+ DP buckets.
- Revenue drivers indicate 60–70% commission from collections, 15–18% automated legal services, 5–8% AI-driven collections, 2–8% data enrichment; for >90DPD, commissions can be 25–35% of amount collected.
- For Kissht, a go-live target of ₹1 crore per month billing is noted.
[Inference] Pricing appears to mix performance-based commissions with verticalized service fees; no formal external pricing sheet disclosed.

Status of product & traction highlight
Live, multi-channel field operations with AI/automation components:
- In-house bot calling, route optimization, automated case allocation, and digital legal workflow.
- Android field-collection app; real-time field tracking; 64k digital legal notices issued; 6000+ pins covered pan-India; 600+ field agents; 120 call-centre staff; 5 tech leads; 12 central team.
- NBFCs/Banks partnered; portfolio ~₹758 crore; average monthly resolution for unsecured pool (180+ DPDP) ~3%; monthly revenue from current clients ~₹0.9 lakh; margins ~30% (180+ DPDP), lower for early buckets; 38% of customers engaged digitally.

  - 5-6 lakh collection agencies operate only at regional/city level (max 35-40 people)
- Target segments: MFI and fintech lenders
  - Digital PL market ₹1.2 lakh crores, none have field teams
  - MFI crisis created opportunity: Unity Bank customers unvisited for 1 year due to BC partner failures

- Team structure: 600 field agents across 13 states in 5 months
  - Variable compensation model: minimum ₹1 lakh collection for ₹20k salary

  - Additional through partner: Fusion, ESAF, Cashe
  - Kissht will go live from Jan once non-compete ends; 1 cr per month billing expected
  - Total assets under management: ₹900 crores
- Monthly collections: ₹6 crores
  - Take rates: 10% regular, 15% SMA, 20% NPA
  - Net margin: 75-80% after field costs
  - EBITDA: 28-30% after all expenses
**AI & Technology Integration**
- Bot calling partnership with WordWorks
  - Pre-9am calling (8-9am slot): 66-70k calls processed hourly
  - Legal notice automation: bot calls, SMS notices, visit scheduling
  - Context-aware follow-ups: PTP reminders, left message tracking
- Field automation initiatives
  - Route optimization within 5km radius
  - Automated case allocation based on geography and agent performance
  - Data science approach to improve 2% recovery rates in late buckets
**Expansion Plans & Capital**
