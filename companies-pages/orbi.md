**What it is**
- **Orbi** is building **AI companions for kids** focused on *play, learning, and social connection*.
- Core pitch: move kids from passive consumers to **creators**, by letting them co-create stories and “worlds” with an AI character.

**Origin story / product evolution**
- Started as a **reading tutor chatbot**.
- A kid (“Bruce”) used it as a **dragon companion**, which pushed the team toward a more character-driven companion + world-building product.
- Next step described: not just chat, but building **personality**, **social worlds**, and **sharing with friends**.

**Product features (as presented)**
- Pick a companion and build an ongoing relationship that “evolves” with interaction.
- Daily “rituals” (eating, sleeping) intended to create a sense of life.
- **Generative interactions** + **Voice AI** to create and evolve worlds by speaking.
- Social layer: kids invite friends into shared worlds.

**Market / monetization framing**
- TAM framed around “digital-native kids” globally and ARPU analogs from UGC/games.
- Monetization: **in-game microtransactions** and **subscriptions**.
- Growth loop framing: acquisition through trust channels (homeschooling networks, parent influencers, gaming creators), leading to network effects and paid conversion.

**Traction claims (not audited)**
- “Kids skip Roblox to spend hours on Orbi.”
- Metrics shown:
  - **M1 retention: 60%** (best cohorts: **80%**)
  - **Average session time: 26 mins** (claimed 2.5x Roblox)
  - **Max weekly time: 6 hours**
  - **30% WoW growth**
  - **50% referral rate**
  - **1,110+ invite-only beta users**

**Team**
- 3 co-founders: **Raj (CEO)**, **Virok (CPO)**, **Ritik (CTO)**.
- Positioning: engineers who have built gaming/consumer products to multi-million-dollar businesses, with prior experience across growth, product, and building for kids at scale.

**Why now (their framing)**
- Multi-modal AI enabling interactive world generation.
- Increased AI adoption in schools.
- Regulatory tailwinds (ESA vouchers) increasing parent-directed spending power in the US.

**Fundraise / vision**
- Raising **$2M**.
- Long-term vision in phases:
  1. digital foundation (virtual worlds with companion)
  1. companion across apps/media (e.g., games + video)
  1. across offline experiences (toys / physical objects / wearables)

    ### Previous Product and Pivot
    - Before building Hobby, the team built an AI tutor with human-in-the-loop for homeschooling kids in the US 
    - Scaled to $80K ARR in 3 months with 98% retention over 6 months 
    - Decided to pivot away for two key reasons:  
    - The experience working with US homeschooling kids led to spending time playing Roblox and games like Adopt Me and Grow Your Garden with children, which provided deep insights into kids' behaviors around creation and companionship 
    - These insights formed the foundation for Hobby 
    ### Product Overview and Features
    - Hobby is an AI-powered creative platform where kids can create companions (pets), characters, and interactive adventures  
    - Example use case: A girl created a fox veterinarian and simulated conversations between the fox and her pet companion; the fox told the pet to have soup, so she created soup with ingredients the pet liked, put the pet to sleep, and played a cozy movie for it 
    - The platform provides a "playground for imagination to become tangible" - similar to how kids play with toys in their heads, but now it's visual and interactive 
    - Kids can create ongoing adventures and narratives with their companions 
    - Social features are core to the experience - kids compare their characters and comics with others, print out creations, paste them in scrapbooks, and compare houses, dragons, and pet companions 
    - Companions have emotional responses - if kids don't care for their pet daily, it gets sad, teaching social-emotional learning and empathy 
    - Kids can choose from various companion types including dragons, hamsters, monkeys, and unicorns 
    ### Most Requested Features and Roadmap
    - Creating custom companions is the #1 requested feature - kids want to design unique companions like "ice snakes" and "rodents" with custom voices and personalities 
    - Multiplayer co-raising of companions with friends  
    - 3D interactive world creation similar to Roblox where kids can walk around with their companions and interact with villains they've created 
    - Game creation tools with templates that kids can customize to create their own mini-games with objectives like treasure hunts and custom game mechanics  
    ### Target Market and User Insights
    - Initially targeted ages 6-12, but found 8-13 works best because kids in this age range are more creative and can better articulate their thoughts 
    - The 8-14 age group cares more about social validation and doesn't want to engage in isolation, unlike younger kids 
    - Currently have 1,100 users in private beta 
    - User acquisition started with a micro school where the team observed which types of kids engaged most, then grew through referrals from micro school founders, educators, and parents 
    - Retention is notably high - kids who were expected to get bored after 10 days have been creating the same adventures for 4 months straight due to investment in characters they've created 
    - The platform prompts kids to create backstories for heroes and villains, which drives deeper engagement 
    ### Standout User Behaviors
    - One child is so particular about their creations that a WhatsApp group was created with their mom to share images so they can show friends 
    - During Christmas, this same child compiled their comic creations into a printed book and gave it as a holiday gift to cousins and friends - described as their proudest moment of the year 
    - Kids exhibit competitive behavior - one child told their cousin that "this pet isn't going to get impressed so easily" and gave tips on how to get their companion to like them  
    - One child is now requesting the ability to enter the 3D planets they've created and physically fight the villains (crabs) they've designed 
    ### Vision
    - The core vision is "personalizing a Club Penguin-like platform for every kid"
