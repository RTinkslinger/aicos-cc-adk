## Note from founder 2026-03-09T16:43:00.000+05:30 
**Pactle**
At Pactle, we’re building an **AI-native revenue engine for manufacturers, distributors, and wholesalers**.
If you look at this space today, most companies either use expensive, clunky ERPs or run their entire business on Excel and local IT-built custom software. Even after spending crores on ERP implementations, adoption usually breaks down at the sales and ops level - the people who are actually supposed to use the system every day don’t use it because it’s slow, complicated, and painful.
What ends up happening is that sales teams continue working on spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and internal tools, while the ERP becomes a **glorified accounting system used mainly by senior management**. There’s very little real-time visibility, and none of the leakage actually gets fixed. Talk to any manufacturer or distributor and you’ll hear the same thing.
In manufacturing and distribution, sales reps spend **~70% of their time on repetitive admin work** - creating quotes, proforma invoices, commercial invoices, and manually updating Tally. At the same time, data is completely scattered across marketplaces like IndiaMART, Justdial, TradeIndia, emails, spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and different internal teams.
This is how the process usually works today:
- Product Enquiries come in via marketplaces, email, or WhatsApp
- They are manually forwarded to sales
- A sales rep spends **1–1.5 hours per quote** finding the right specs in the price master, calculating prices, and putting everything into Excel
- Once the PO comes in, the same data is re-entered into proforma invoices, order confirmations, and work orders
- Documents are shared with the plant for approval and then entered again into Tally
- Inventory is tracked manually
- There is no single source of truth, and this process repeats hundreds of times every month.
Because data is so fragmented, companies lose customers and revenue at every stage:
- Got the RFQ but forgot to send the quote
- Sent the quote but didn’t follow up and lost the PO
- Got the PO but delayed or missed the PI
- Sent the PI but forgot to update inventory
There’s a lot of leakage across the system.
---
### **Our solution**
Pactle is an **AI-native revenue engine** that replaces this entire mess.
- Captures RFQs from any channel - email, WhatsApp, marketplaces like IndiaMart etc
- Auto-maps SKUs, prices, and specs from the price master
- Instantly generates quotes, POs, proforma invoices, order confirmations, and work orders
- Automatically follows up at every stage so nothing falls through the cracks
- Syncs with Tally automatically
- Tracks the order end-to-end - inventory, invoicing, and payment collection
The goal is simple: **remove manual work, reduce leakage, and give teams one system of record instead of 10 disconnected tools**.
---
### **Current stage**
- The thesis is validated. I’ve spoken to **~700 manufacturers and distributors** and continue to talk to users daily. This has given us a very clear understanding of the problem and buying behavior.
- We’ve raised **~$500k** so far from SPC and gradCapital.
- The product is live and doing **~$25k in ARR**.
- We’re live with our first customer and three companies are under implementation.
- Customers include **Stellaris Speciality**, one of the largest MIG/TIG electrode manufacturers in the country.
- We currently have **79 companies in the pipeline** that we are about to close.
### **Path forward**
**1. India First**
Immediate focus: dominate the Indian MSME manufacturing and distribution market. Target 10,000 companies onboarded over the next 24 months. Build Pactle as the deeply embedded operating system that Indian MSMEs run their business on.
**2. Geographic Expansion**
The Middle East and Southeast Asia feel like the most natural extensions.
We already have a couple of companies in the pipeline, like TransACNR (a bus air-conditioner manufacturer that started in India, scaled well, and later expanded to the Middle East). I’ve also spoken with hundreds of traders and manufacturers across the region, including cold-calling ~50 businesses in Saudi Arabia to better understand how these companies operate on the ground.
What stood out clearly is that the Middle East has a much higher density of traders and wholesalers than manufacturers, which actually works in our favor since transaction volume and repeat workflows are significantly higher in trading businesses.
Operationally, these companies function very similarly to Indian MSMEs:
- Heavy dependence on Excel
- ERPs adopted at the management level but largely ignored by sales teams
- Cheap labor imported from India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh handling manual ops
- Significant data leakage and no real system of record
What’s interesting is that, unlike India, there are no dominant demand-aggregation platforms like IndiaMART or Justdial. Most businesses are highly relationship-driven.
**3. Credit & Working Capital Enablement**
Our near-term goal is to onboard ~10,000 companies over the next 24 months. At that scale, Pactle will have structured and detailed information about every company across each part of their value chain - how many leads they buy, what is inbound, what are repeat customers, how many quotes, how many invoices, and payments collected.
While we’re not building the product with lending as the primary objective today, many of the core workflows naturally enable underwriting over time.
For example, we’re rolling out payment gateway enabled invoices, which lets us track actual cash realization, not just intent.
When combined with quote-to-invoice conversion and customer data, this starts creating a reliable credit profile for MSMEs that are otherwise invisible to traditional lenders.
Long term, I see SaaS as the distribution layer and data as the underwriting moat with lending and financial services can become a meaningful revenue engine on top.
That said, my immediate focus remains on dominating the Indian market. I’m confident that by building a deeply embedded operating system for Indian MSMEs first, these expansion paths will unlock naturally and at scale.



## Unit economics 2026-03-09T16:43:00.000+05:30 

### **Technology & Unit Economics**
If you look at software companies serving manufacturing, they solve the problem by throwing people at it. Not just in sales - even in product implementation.
- They send 3 - 4 engineers per customer.
- They do heavy customization.
- Implementation takes 3 - 4 months.
- There are even separate implementation partners whose whole job is gathering requirements and customizing the system.
Few things happen:
1. It takes a lot of time and effort per customer without knowing whether company-wide adoption will actually happen.
1. It becomes very human-heavy.
1. Unit economics don’t make sense unless you charge very high implementation fees.
1. Scaling becomes extremely slow.
This is the reason why, apart from Tally, there isn’t one standard system across Indian MSME manufacturing and trading. Economically, customization-heavy ERP models don’t work for this segment.
We are taking a different approach, and tech plays a huge role in it.
Instead of customizing everything for 3 months, we are generalizing the core workflow:
RFQ → Quote → PI → WO → Invoice → Collection
And making it configurable.
Our goal is to onboard a customer in 30 minutes - not 3 months.
To enable that, we are building an internal admin configuration layer that our future sales and customer success teams can use right after a contract is signed.
They will be able to:
- Assign roles (admin, super admin, sales, accounts, etc.)
- Control visibility
- Customize quote fields, invoice fields, and calculations
- Add/remove fields like freight, discount logic, GST structure, etc.
- Modify branding and terms
- Toggle integrations like Tally sync or WhatsApp
**For example**, one customer might have:
SKU, Qty, Unit Price, Discount, Final Amount.
Another might want:
SKU, Qty, Unit Price, Discount, Freight (after discount), GST, Final Amount.
Our team should be able to configure all of this without engineering involvement.
This small structural change is what allows us to scale without increasing engineering or implementation costs linearly.
---
### **Current Unit Economics**
Today we charge ₹35,000 per month per customer.
On average, a customer creates 500 - 600 quotations per month.
AI cost including document parsing + SKU matching + quote/document generation is ~₹6.5 per document.
At 600 documents, that’s ~₹3,900 per month in AI cost.
Including infra and hosting, total variable cost per customer is roughly ₹6,000 - 7,000 per month.
So at current usage levels, gross margin is ~75 - 80%.
AI costs scale with document volume, not revenue and over time, as workflows stabilize and models get cheaper, that cost will reduce further.
The bigger risk in this market is not AI cost. It’s services creep. That’s why we’re investing heavily in configurability instead of customization.
---
### **Where AI Adds the Most Value**
Automation is important, but our main goal is visibility and revenue leakage reduction.
Right now, most manufacturers don’t have end-to-end visibility.
Leads come from IndiaMART, email, WhatsApp.
Quotes are made in Excel.
POs are tracked manually.
Invoices go into Tally.
Follow-ups are inconsistent.
We give them one system from lead → quote → order → invoice → payment.
That itself is a massive unlock because senior management finally sees what’s happening.
In the coming months, AI becomes the intelligence layer:
- If a sales rep doesn’t create assigned quotes → alert management.
- If a customer has sent 3 RFQs worth > ₹10 lakhs in the last 3 months → trigger follow-up.
- If deals are stuck beyond X days → escalate.
- If margin drops below threshold → notify.
The goal is to move from “workflow automation” to “revenue control layer.”
Instead of just generating documents faster, we tell founders where they are leaking revenue.
That’s where AI adds the most value.
---
### **GTM**
Manufacturing GTM is very different from SaaS selling to tech companies.
Here’s what we’ve experimented with:
- I started with LinkedIn outbound, but most manufacturers are not active there.
- Then we tried sales-heavy email outbound. That didn’t work either - they don’t reply to salesy emails.
- Then I ran a campaign where I told manufacturers I wanted to hear their story and write a blog about how they built their company. If it performed well, I’d feature them in Times Square. It helped us get a foot in the door, but the sales cycle became unnecessarily long, so we stopped.
**The breakthrough came when we built lists from IndiaMART and directly tried to speak to MDs and owners.**
That changed everything.
Out of 100 MDs we spoke to, we started getting ~20 demos.
**Current bottleneck:**
Most of them prefer in-person meetings instead of online demos.
When I meet them in person, we usually close within 1–2 days.
So yes, today GTM is founder-led and relationship-heavy.
Outbound is working really well for us right now, but I’m very aware that this alone doesn’t scale infinitely.
Apart from outbound, here are the key things we are building to make GTM non-linear:
1. **Branding-led growth**
Every quote, PI, and invoice generated through Pactle will say “Created using Pactle” or “Sent via Pactle.”
Manufacturers sell to distributors. Distributors are also our ICP. So every time a distributor receives a document, they see Pactle.
This is similar to how DocuSign built distribution through signatures, or how Superhuman builds visibility inside email workflows.
We want Pactle to sit inside the transaction layer so every commercial document becomes distribution.
1. **If we can crack distribution via IndiaMART / marketplaces**
We are actively thinking about how we can integrate or partner with platforms like IndiaMART, TradeIndia, OFBusiness, etc.
If Pactle becomes the default RFQ → Quote automation layer for sellers on these marketplaces, then distribution becomes embedded.
Instead of convincing each manufacturer individually, we sit inside their lead source itself.
That’s where GTM becomes structurally different.
1. **Configurable product reduces sales friction**
A big reason manufacturing SaaS doesn’t scale is heavy implementation and customization.
By building a configurable system instead of a customized one, we reduce onboarding time dramatically.
Faster onboarding → shorter sales cycle → easier for non-founder sales reps to close.
Over time, this reduces dependency on me personally being involved in every deal.
---
Outbound will continue to be a strong channel because it’s working very well right now. I haven’t seen this kind of curiosity for a product before.
