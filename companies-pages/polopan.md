**What They’re Building Now (Product Summary)**
- Product positioning
  - “Outfit app” (moodboard-like experience but with purchasable items).
  - Swipe-based discovery of complete outfits (not just single SKUs).
  - Personalization driven by “taste graph” + image-to-vector embedding.
  - Style controls: user can “replace” an item within an outfit with alternatives (e.g., change color/variant/similar style).
  - Wishlist / “love tab” for saved outfits.
  - PDP layer: price, size, details shown post-tap.
  - Alerts: size-back-in-stock notifications; price alerts planned/mentioned.
  - Link-out commerce: user clicks “shop” → redirected to marketplace/brand site; Polopan earns commission regardless of final purchase destination.
- Core claim
  - Building “taste identity” and “fashion graph” of users (trend + taste intelligence).
  - “Style intelligence layer on top of existing marketplaces.”

**Consumer Insights (Key Learnings From Discovery)**
- Marketplace overwhelm is driving behavior shift
  - Younger users find Myntra “overwhelming” and not resonating; discovery increasingly happening on Instagram + D2C/Shopify brands.
  - Founder’s customer discussions suggested only ~30–40% of recent purchases come from marketplaces; rest from Shopify / brand sites / D2C (e.g., athleisure, Zara, H&M, Uniqlo search behavior mentioned).
- Tier-2 vs Tier-1 difference (their view)
  - Tier-1: more style-aware; better offline options; less need for “styling help.”
  - Tier-2: fewer offline options + higher need for “putting outfits together”; hence stronger PMF.
  - Their bet: Tier-2 is “budget conscious,” not brand-purist; cares about rating/quality/price more than brand label.
- Why chat-based fashion assistants struggled (their view, competitor learning)
  - Chat interface assumes user knows what they want; often they don’t (“vague canvas” like occasion/event).
  - Discovery is driven by past behavior signals; similar to social feed curation.

**Unit Economics / Monetization Model**
- Current monetization
  - Current take rate: ~8% (commission).
- Medium-term monetization path (their logic)
  - Increase take rate by shifting mix towards direct Shopify brand partnerships (vs only marketplace affiliate).
  - Marketplace model: commission stays ~8–10% for large flagship brands.
  - Shopify plugin model: “plug-and-play” onboarding → faster scale of brands without manual onboarding.
- Shopify brands economics (explicit numbers)
  - Brands currently pay ~30% “success” (commission) on Myntra.
  - Polopan offers ~20% “success” fee for sending traffic to brand’s own Shopify site.
  - Rationale: brand is happy because 20% < 30% and traffic lands on their own site.
- Catalog strategy
  - Aim to identify ~250–300 brands that complete the catalog “from all angles.”
  - View: top ~20% brands will drive most revenue (Pareto framing).
  - Flagship brands (e.g., Uniqlo) act as “showstoppers” / traction drivers even at lower commission.

**Tech Differentiation / Moat (Per founder)**
- Core tech claim
  - Built “neuro-symbolic” model/engine to recommend full outfits.
  - Claimed benefits:
    - ~100x cheaper than token/API-dependent AI approaches.
    - Runs on CPU (cost advantage critical for Tier-2 scaling).
    - Predictable output due to symbolic relationships (“100/100 accurate” claimed in terms of consistency/predictability).
- Taste graph
  - Image-swipes → convert items into vectors → map user as a point in taste space → recommend nearby styles.
  - Cold start approach:
    - Uses age-group aggregated preferences (summation vector for what top/bottom/footwear are liked by that age group).
    - First set is “slightly randomized” because the space is large; personalization tightens after swipes.
- Data asset / network effects framing
  - As users engage, they build “taste graphs” making the system stronger; analogy to social media feeds.
