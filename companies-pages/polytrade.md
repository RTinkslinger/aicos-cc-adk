**About**
[Polytrade](https://polytrade.finance/) is a trade finance co bridging DeFi (decentralised finance) and TradFI (traditional finance) using smart contracts and tokenomics. They bring safe and insurance backed assets (trade invoices) to the crypto world, providing real world borrowers access to low interest and swift financing for working capital, by tapping in to crypto liquidity.

**Thesis**
- Over 100B+ of stable-coin supply in crypto world looking for stable real world yields
- DeFi yields on stable coins are low and currently limited to over-collateralised (LTV 60-70%) products
- Smart contracts and crypto = unique opportunity to unlock stable yields for lenders in US, EU across borders to SMB where APY can be higher
- Polytrade is building a protocol for invoice discounting, factoring with distribution partners downstream for lending and investor GTM which is both direct and with partners
- On investor side cos like Vauld, Flint, Pillow etc would deploy on polytrade ++ distribution partners like Drip, Volofin, Tradewin, Incomlend etc would work on capital deployment end
- Polytrade smart contract would become the middleware for crypto rails + smart contracting
- Polytrade smart contract + [oracles](https://ethereum.org/en/developers/docs/oracles/) would assure investors of risk mitigation by using technology to assess each transaction on criteria such as buyer creditworthiness, seller’s performance risk and specific risks to each deal

What's Goldfinch *(refer to protocol design below for details)*
- Undercollateralized lending marketplace working on the principle of trust through consensus - protocol participants assess credit worthiness
- Eventual vision is to be the open source VISA for lending - be the underlying network on top of which lending businesses are built
Why now?
- Needed key infra pieces which are now in place: global ramps, stablecoins in multiple currencies, crypto native fx hedging instruments
- Globalization of capital markets - much more VC money flowing into emerging markets equity, debt will follow suit
- Global stablecoin market cap now ~$200B
Value prop
- **Supply side:** Higher + bear market proof APY on stablecoin holdings
- **Demand side:** Under/zero-collateral borrowing without good traditional credit score
- Wealth compounding incentive for both borrower and lender via rewards in protocol's native token, GFI
GTM: Wholesale lending in emerging markets
- @Avnish Bajaj, this is one difference with Polytrade - they're going after the trade finance market eliminating the fx risk and costs altogether
- Mohash is going after the same market as Goldfinch
Scale
- TVL: $112M, AUM (Active loans): $82.6M
- 35k+ KYC'd investors, 5k+ LPs and backers
- 200k+ end borrowers across 18 countries
- Twitter: 47k, Discord: 44k
Protocol design and role of community
- Structured as a full DIY open marketplace for both borrowers and lenders orchestrated with GFI tokens
- @Avnish Bajaj, this is the key difference with Mohash and Polytrade
- Both are structured as asset managers on the investors end, protocol participants don't underwrite end borrowers
- On distribution side too, borrowers don’t engage with the protocol directly but via own/third party centralized entities
- Mohash only underwrites traditional lenders and fintechs while Polytrade goes one step further with underwriting end borrowers themselves
- 4 main actors in the protocol
- Borrowers (fintechs initially) - propose borrower pools with a term sheet
- Use GFI to pay for audit
- Auditors (9 randomly selected for each pool verification weighted by the amount of GFI staked) - verify legitimacy of borrowers and prevent borrower-backer collusion
- Earn staking yields via the fees paid by borrowers
- Backers (active investors, on + off-chain risk assessors, first loss takers) - Form the junior tranche of the lending pool and earn APY b/w 9 and 15%
- Backers can stake GFI to signify consensus and increase the leverage ratio of the pool + also acts as additional collateral
- They earn GFI rewards for good underwriting and low defaults
- Liquidity Providers (passive investors) - Invest agnostically and parametrically across all pools - this forms the senior tranche and is added to lending pools via a mathematical leverage model, current APY ~5%
- The model depends on the number of backers, amount in junior tranche and distribution of funds across individual backers
- 20% of the interest earned by the senior pool is reallocated to the backers, rewarding the higher risk
- Interest and repayments distribution is trustless according to set rules and parameters
- 10% of all repayments are kept as reserves which the GFI token holders will manage via decentralized governance - can be used as grants, FLDG etc.
- Current reserves $450k+
Contra-thesis
- Full DIY approach - need to attract traditional risk analysts as backers, robustness and NPAs untested, need huge pain points for the borrower to go through all the obstacles (gas costs, poor ui)
- Lots of complex moving parts to achieve under/zero collateralization and extremely tough to orchestrate and the founders have said "introducing tokens made running the protocol 10x harder"
- Unlocking newer forms of collateral (e.g. digital assets) might be the key innovation needed to solve but with lesser complexity
- Solving cold start and sustaining liquidity - this is where the importance of community and incentivizing early adopters comes in, also the invested capital is locked for 1y to sustain liquidity
- Solving trust - lenders are used to dealing with humans via relationships and also might not come back to the protocol to borrow after a single failure
