### Product Overview & Market Positioning
- AI security platform solving enterprise visibility and compliance challenges
- Current focus: enterprises with 300+ AI workloads struggling with inventory and governance
- Started with visibility, expanding to red teaming and runtime security
- Zero-configuration testing approach differentiates from competitors requiring heavy setup
- Core value proposition
- “I don’t even know what’s going on” - primary enterprise pain point
- 24 unauthorized AI applications discovered in first deployment at NBD bank
- 350+ AI workloads across internal and external use cases at enterprise clients
### Technical Architecture & Differentiation
- AI Asset Graph (patented) using Abstract Syntax Trees and Code Property Graphs
- Processes 100 repositories in 5-10 minutes vs 30-35 minutes for competitors
- Creates comprehensive mapping of AI assets and relationships
- Automatically updates as code changes
- Two-product approach
- Lens: visibility, compliance reporting, sensitive data detection ($80K average)
- Shield: business logic testing, runtime security ($100-150K additional)
- Technical moat concerns
- Graph-based relationship mapping is unique
- Fast AST/CPG processing differentiates from competitors like Protect AI
- Zero-config testing reduces customer friction significantly
### Customer Base & Pipeline
- Strong enterprise logos across regions
- NBD (350+ AI workloads), Emirates, Airtel
- Agoda replacing Noma Security after 9-month deployment
- Current metrics
- 15-20% month-on-month pipeline growth
- $80K average deal size for Lens, $180-230K combined
- 60% pipeline from existing connections, 40% from outreach
- Customer use cases
- Regulatory compliance (ISO 27001, GDPR) reporting requirements
- Sensitive data processing identification and cataloging
- Business logic vulnerability testing beyond standard OWASP Top 10
### Go-to-Market Strategy
- Three-pronged approach for next 6-12 months
- High-touch enterprise deals (current focus)
- Self-serviceable SMB product (Lens only, $40-50K deals)
- Channel partnerships (Guide Point, Cyber Night distributor for EMEA)
- Geographic expansion timeline
- US: primary focus with enterprise pipeline
- EMEA: partner-led distribution model
- ANZ: 12-month horizon, working with government policy contributors
### Competitive Landscape Assessment
- Visibility space still has 6-12 month window before saturation
- Red teaming and guardrails markets already crowded
- Major players acquired (Robust Intelligence to Snowflake)
- Remaining independent players like Encrypt in India
- New entrants like Portkeys in guardrails space
- Strategic advantage: entering through less contested visibility wedge
