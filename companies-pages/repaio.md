    ### Context
    Business discussion between Rahul from DeVC and Ishita from Westbridge covering Teractive (DeVC portfolio company) competitive positioning and in-depth analysis of the collections/fintech space, particularly focused on Credgenics (Westbridge portfolio company).  
    ### Women's Athleisure Market Insights
    - **Customer perspective on brands**: Lululemon is preferred for quality and longevity - leggings from 5 years ago still look brand new  
    - **Market gaps in India**: Women's formal and office wear market is underdeveloped compared to Singapore/Western markets due to limited depth and diversity in acceptable styles   
    - **Competitive set for evaluation**: Teractive vs Kava should include Bliss Club (₹350 crore scale) and Uniqlo in competitive analysis   
    - **Quality positioning**: GoColors is not in consideration set - not remotely close in quality to Lululemon/premium brands 
    ### Credgenics Business Model Evolution
    **Revenue Mix Shift**  
    - 2023 at $15M ARR: 70% SaaS / 30% Services 
    - Current at $25M ARR: 40% SaaS / 60% Services (completely flipped)
    - Services (mediation) business has surpassed SaaS revenue
    - Customer appetite for technology implementation is "super low" 
    **Why Services Dominate**  
    - Collections processes are owned by separate teams divided by portfolio type and DPD bucket
    - Technology implementation requires buy-in from every team, which is "next to impossible"
    - Customers now expect Credgenics to be better at actual collections, not just software
    - Easier to sell services than get technology adopted
    ### Pricing & Business Model
    **Outcome-Based Pricing** 
    - All fulfillment revenue is outcome-based (no payment per SMS/call)
    - Minimum guarantee may exist as retainer, but real money comes from performance
    - Need significant data and intelligence to succeed with this model
    - Credgenics operates at 60% gross margin, but customers complain they are "too expensive" 
    **Market Structure** 
    - 2-3 vendors per NBFC/Bank for risk mitigation and competitive pricing
    - Always operates as cost-plus model, not true SaaS margins
    - Vendors are frequently evaluated and rotated based on performance 
    ### Technology & AI Reality Check
    **Limited AI Applications**   
    - "There's absolutely no AI in this" - need for tech is very low in this slow, boring industry
    - Most financial institutions have internal analytics teams with predictive models  
    - Even struggling lenders like SMFG have in-house predictive analytics capabilities
    - Credgenics has "no clear answer to why AI" - customers just want rule engines repackaged as AI 
    **Why Tech Doesn't Scale**   
    - Lenders have more data than tech vendors - get monthly reports on borrower behavior across loans
    - Digital connectivity/contactability is only 10-15% outside urban areas 
    - Urban sector is not the biggest problem - retail/microfinance portfolios in areas with poor network access are
    - Even with predictive models, regulations limit interventions before actual default 
    ### TAM Assessment
    - SaaS TAM: ~$200M
    - Services TAM: ~$1B 
    - Team always knew larger play was services, but didn't realize appetite would be this limited 
    - Credgenics hasn't been able to expand beyond retail unsecured lending book 
    ### Field Collections Operational Challenges
    **Gig Model Won't Work**  
    - Field collections requires skip tracing, which needs strong variable compensation and full employment
    - Gig workers only handle easiest buckets (bucket 1) to hit monthly targets
    - Same collectors work with multiple lenders - they know which customers are defaulting across lenders 
    - Local market is small: "same people collecting, same people borrowing and defaulting" 
    **Scaling Limitations**  
    - Most agencies unable to scale beyond couple of states
    - Requires very tight process management without relying on professional management
    - Ops-heavy business is "very difficult to scale beyond a point"
    ### Bull Case for Credgenics
    **PSU Market Entry** 
    - Slowly getting into public sector banks could create "large unlock"
    - Arise acquisition provided PSU contracts for cross-selling mediation business  
    **Tech Initiatives**  
    - Exclusive partnership with PhonePe: defaulters using PhonePe UPI see notifications prompting payment when opening app
    - Shows high intent since user is opening payment app
    - App can suggest partial EMI payment based on account balance
    **Legal Notices as Wedge**   
    - Legal notice business is "easiest foot in the door" and core competitive advantage
    - Process: bank provides customer list, Credgenics drafts letters, gets them signed by local advocates, and posts them 
    - Very basic business but effective wedge product
    ### Acquisition Strategy
    **No Tech Acquisitions Likely**   
    - Scanned market for 4-5 years, haven't found interesting opportunities
    - Evaluated Gnani AI but concluded "we could just build it" 
    - "There's just nothing interesting that's not easy to build" 
    **Arise Acquisition Rationale**   
    - Primary: PSU contracts and customer relationships for cross-selling
    - Secondary: Inorganic growth as organic growth is slowing 
    - Enabled entry into field collections with existing network
    **ARC Business Consideration**  
    - Credgenics considering investing in distressed pools through ARC
    - Westbridge IC pushback: "we don't want to put our capital at risk" - should focus on tech, not age-old capital markets business 
    - Question raised: "have we hit a ceiling on TAM?" 
    ### Broader Fininfra Challenges
    **Fund Performance**  
    - DeVC Fund 1 invested in ~10 fintech companies
    - Only 1 fininfra company likely to break out: Saidrila (RTA) - propped up as third competitor due to broker ecosystem needs
    - On distribution side: Ambak (balance sheet play)
    - Result: 3-4% of fund in zombie outcomes
    **Key Insight**  
    - "The only interesting play is lending" - requires building balance sheet
    - Distribution plays are bad idea, manufacturing plays are better
    - Insurance: Managing General Agent construct could be interesting with right reinsurance partnerships
    ### Competitive Landscape
    **Key Players Mentioned**
    - Credgenics (market leader)
    - Spocto (number 2 player) 
    - DPD Zero 
    - Credit Nirvana (going through challenges)  
    - Cred Resolve (ex-Wheels Eye team) 
    - Repayo (ex-MoneyView team) 
    **Early Mover Advantage** 
    - Credgenics broke out due to being early - was novel at the time
    - Many customers learned from Credgenics, then built collections internally or switched vendors
