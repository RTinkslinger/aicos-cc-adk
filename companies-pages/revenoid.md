## BRC from Soumitra 2025-07-03 provided by @Aakash 

So I have known Rabi since 2021 when he was building Evabot. He was also part of Morpheus in the early days, and I know a few folks from there so got feedback on him there too.
Rabi's main spike is **product**. Evabot's product experience was also fantastic, and they were serving large enterprise customers there (it was an intelligent corporate gifting assistant, so again in the sales enablement space).
Within product, my observation is that the team's expertise is around **data** - ingesting structured + unstructured, 1st party and third party, making sense of it, web scraping from diverse external sources & internal systems of record, and bringing it all together. This was a core part of Evabot too, and this is what they are leveraging in Revenoid as well.
The entire team is the **exact founding team of Evabot**, they are very scrappy & iterative from what I have observed. Leveraging the latest in LLMs/ other AI tech in their features isn't a big risk in my head. They won't "invent" breakthrough things but will deliver high ROI features for large enterprises in sales. It's a practical, street-smart type of approach to product building.
My bet on him is as a **repeat founder + chip on shoulder (Evabot wasn't an outcome) + proven ability to raise institutional capital in the US (he raised $13Mn from Comcast and Blumberg in Evabot) + ability to iteratively build large enterprise-grade products in the US**.
**His gap is large ACV GTM**. He has learned & improved on it from Evabot + is now aggressively surrounding himself with GTM advisors who can help him crack large logos.
Every time I have met him, he has only talked about customers & more customers, how to get more of them, how to increase ACVs, how to get even faster GTM etc. He seems to be less enamored with LLMs as a core tech or creating deep AI-based differentiation. He is more of a believer in building enterprise products that solve real pain points, leveraging complex data pipelines, shipping more compelling features regularly etc.
PS: he is also thinking big this time and seems to have more confidence. I mentioned that cross-border funds could be interested in the next round, and he immediately said I don't want to raise from them. I will target only raising from Tier 1 Valley VCs this time
