## Analysis by dev - 2025-11-01
### Company Snapshot
Problem & solution
Counter-drone defense focused on hardkill capability; aims to deploy a full-stack system (radar detection + autonomous interceptors) to neutralize small drones, including swarms, at unit and field levels.

Ideal customer profile and pricing
Beach-head: Indian defense units and Special Forces; LOI from Special Forces for portable hardkill system (~INR 5 crore per unit) with 18-month development; inbound interest from European/American system integrators.
Pricing elements cited: full detection system < $200k; interceptor unit ~< $2,000 each at scale; one unit price cited ~INR 5 crore (Special Forces LOI).

Status of product & traction highlight
Ongoing paid pilot with a listed Indian defense company; PO around $25k for one interceptor + accessories (Feb 2025) leading to army trials (Mar 2025); LOI from Special Forces; inbound international partner interest (Dedrone, APS, etc.); roadmap with Q4 2025 state estimation, 2026 trials, and 2027 full system readiness.

### (2) Product and Technology
Product stage
Prototype/early field trials; ongoing end-user pilot; system-level MVP progression with end-user integration.

Core functionality & tech stack
Radars for small-drone detection; in-house active radar development; patch antennas; RF electronics; onboard ML for detection/tracking/guidance; UAV control system built from scratch (not using Pixhawk/ArduPilot); HIL simulation lifecycle; edge ML pipeline (Alon edge); autonomous interceptors (vajR long-range and chakR short-range); GCS integration.

Unique IP
In-house radar design, RF electronics, patch antenna characterization; bespoke autopilot/control stack; ML-based guidance development; HIL test bench; no explicit patents stated.

Key differentiators vs substitutes
End-to-end hardkill system with integrated radar, C2, and interceptors; in-house control systems and radar with a focus on compact, SWaP-conscious hardware; aim to outperform passive jammers and RF-based softkill approaches; target 4 km hardkill range with compact radars; direct system-level end-user integration rather than subcontracting to primes.

Implementation complexity / switching costs
High technical risk (full-stack build from radar to interceptor); emphasis on system-level trials with end users to mitigate mismatch between user needs and a vendor’s capabilities; risk around radar supply chain and government procurement cycles.

### **Meeting Notes**
**Founder Background- **[**Karan Goyal**](https://www.linkedin.com/in/karan-goyal-6b1099188/)
- Origin & early exposure: Grew up in Samana (near Patiala cantonment), surrounded by tanks and artillery; developed early fascination with defense.
- Family context: Comes from a business family (yarn mill background), saw entrepreneurship at home.
- Education: IIT Delhi graduate, Class of 2022.
- Member of the Formula Student race car team for 4 years — hands-on hardware building, teamwork, and exposure to startup culture.
- Inspired by seniors who started Baaz Bikes and Vecmocon, both now beyond Series B.
- Influences:
- Studied US defense-tech ecosystem — particularly Anduril (Palmer Luckey, ex-Oculus, and Palantir alumni).
- Read *Kill Chain* by Anduril’s co-founder; internalized concept that modern warfare revolves around building and disrupting the “kill chain.”
- Concluded that robotics will disrupt defense the most, creating room for a new “Lockheed Martin.”
**Company Formation & Team**
- Founded: 2022–23 after noticing drone-based drug smuggling on the India–Pakistan border.
- Initial setup: Funded by founder’s father; started in a 2BHK apartment in Gurgaon converted into a workshop.
- Co-founders: 5 IIT Delhi graduates.
- Initially balanced jobs with building; all now full-time.
- Team split:
- 2 on business, investment, manufacturing, finance.
- 3 on technical verticals: AI on edge (computer vision), radar development, and interceptor engineering.
**Technology & Product**
- Focus: Counter-drone defense systems with hard-kill capability (beyond just RF jamming).
- Key tech challenges:
1. Radar that can track objects <30cm.
1. Interception of fast, small UAVs in 3D space.
- Current capability: Can reliably intercept hovering drones; algorithms evolving for higher-speed engagements.
- Vision: A “Mini Iron Dome” specifically for drones — radar + interceptor drones with autonomy (hangar launch/reload, onboard AI, net/kinetic defeat).
- Differentiation: Most Indian solutions today = RF jammers or integrators of imported systems. Sharang Shakti is attempting indigenous radar + interception IP.
**Business Progress & Strategy**
- Pre-seed round (early 2024): $600K from AUM Ventures, Venture Highway, micro VCs.
- Customer interactions:
- ZEN Technologies (inbound pilot request) → team realized being a sub-vendor/integrator isn’t scalable.
- Pivoted to working directly with end customers (defense agencies).
- Key win: $6M Letter of Intent from Special Frontier Forces (SFF).
- Connection originated via a colonel studying at IIT Delhi, later posted to SFF (ex-NSG commando).
- SFF interest: portable counter-drone systems for unit-level deployment (contrast to heavy 30-crore border systems).
**Fundraising & Plans**
- Current raise: $6M round.
- $3M for System-level development (deploy MVP with Special Forces in ~18 months) and the other $3 Mn for Radar R&D (longer-term IP creation).
- Future engagements: In discussions with DDPD (Department of Defence Production & Development) and evaluating inbound interest from army units.
- Market segmentation: Focused on special forces and tactical brigades, where drones are increasingly used (inspired by Russia–Ukraine FPV drone use).
