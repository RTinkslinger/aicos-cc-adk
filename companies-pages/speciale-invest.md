    ### Summit Overview
    - The Speciale Invest Summit 2025 showcased innovative deep tech startups and featured keynote addresses on global economic trends and opportunities for India. 
    - The summit highlighted Speciale Invest's unique focus on deep tech startups and their understanding of the challenges and timelines specific to deep tech ecosystems. 
    - Dr. Somnath, former chairman of ISTROS, was featured as one of the keynote speakers.  
    ### Deep Tech Startup Showcase
    - **InBound**: Developing a "lifting body" spacecraft to provide easy, low-cost access to zero gravity environments for research and commercial applications.   
    - **KineLab**: Pioneering quantum physics-based cybersecurity solutions to replace the traditional detection-response approach with proactive protection.   
    - **AI Drug Discovery**: Accelerating pharmaceutical development by using AI to learn collectively from previous research mistakes and streamline the discovery process.   
    - **Processor Technology**: Creating integrated solutions that combine different types of processors (CPUs, GPUs, custom) to support emerging applications. 
    - **Satellite Positioning**: Developing a constellation of low-orbit satellites to provide precise, secure positioning for critical operations like drones and defense that can't be jammed or spoofed.   
    - **ZKM Batteries**: Creating long-duration energy storage solutions using metallic zinc to address renewable energy's seasonal storage challenges, critical for achieving global net-zero targets.    
    - **Darshan Labs**: Focusing on simple but transformative innovations in materials science to support the sustainability revolution.  
    - **WinMan**: Building intelligent defense drones that can operate autonomously in contested environments with electronic countermeasures, while being 50% smaller and 40% lighter than existing technology.   
    ### Global Economic Trends
    - **US Position**: Currently represents 4% of world population but 27% of global GDP and 50% of global market cap with significant foreign liabilities ($62T) and assets ($35T).   
    - **US Advantages**: Benefits from global talent attraction, substantial R&D funding ($240B annually from government), and foreign students (including $20B from Indian students).   
    - **US Challenges**: Current policies may undermine US economic advantages through dollar depreciation (down 9%), tariffs, immigration restrictions, and potential inflation.   
    - **China's Strategy**: Implemented phased economic development focusing first on agricultural productivity and later on manufacturing with strategic partnerships. 
    - **Global Markets**: US dominates global market capitalization ($62T of $124T total), followed by China ($11.7T), Japan ($6.3T), and India ($5.3T).  
    ### Opportunities for India
    - Current global shifts present a significant opportunity for India to attract talent and capital.  
    - Potential to attract $100B from the $27T of risk capital currently in the US as global economic dynamics shift. 
    - Need to improve ease of doing business, as current regulations remain excessively bureaucratic (e.g., BIS licensing requirements).

    - Opportunity to attract Indian talent currently on expiring H-1B visas in the US (~135,000 people) and those in other countries.
