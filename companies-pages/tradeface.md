Problem:
- Earlier Kiraanas were family businesses, children/wife used to sit in
- Now it is increasingly become single owner business, so difficult for them to move out.
- Today: 4-5% Margins made, will not be profitable incl. the Udaan model
- Demand from retailers is not the problem, doing it profitability is the challenge.
- The cost is high because there are incumbents trying to create parallel assets.
- When you try to substitute them, your costs increase vis a vis the players doing that very specialized things
Solution:
- They realized that 3 stakeholders: Wholesalers, transporters and retailers
- Wholesaler has the largest share, then transporters and finally retailers.
- Wholesalers had offline discovery, Transporters didn’t get full capacity and Retailers just want predictable consistent and cost effective service
- Recently, experimented with B2B SaaS product to enable Sellers to run own ecommerce (being piloted) with 1 seller, is built using existing tech
- ONDC as a lever to scale up ops
Model:
- An asset-light model digitally connecting existing seller – buyer – logistics can offer ecommerce profitably. We own Ops and experience, minus the assets/costs.
- Min. fee from sellers per orders(25 – 30Rs.) They get multiple income stream from same order as retailers get from multiple sellers.
- Buyer gets guaranteed delivery, but they are charged a delivery fees.
