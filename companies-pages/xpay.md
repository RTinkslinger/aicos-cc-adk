    ### Company Overview & Business Model
    - Xpay is a cross-border payments company using a Merchant of Record (MOR) model to help Indian digital services companies collect international payments 
    - Currently achieving 90%+ success rates compared to 70-75% for competitors like Razorpay  
    - Company is already profitable with approximately $1M in revenue 
    - Core value proposition: Higher success rates by avoiding mandatory 3DS (two-factor authentication) which causes significant friction for international customers  
    ### Vertical Focus & Market Approach
    - Started with EdTech (only supporting AfterPay and Klarna initially), then moved to digital consumer services (AstroTalk wanted Apple Pay)  
    - Now focusing on Travel sector where players like PayGlocal are paying attention and Air India has custom requirements  
    - Merchants currently paying 12% for card EMI product, with Xpay earning approximately 3% risk-free  
    - Overall merchant cost is approximately 6% all-in (including forex) compared to 9-11% for PayPal  
    ### Founder Assessment
    **Strengths identified:**
    - High agency and execution focus - numerous examples of scrappy sales tactics (standing outside office, cold calling)  
    - Strong customer obsession and sales ability through frank conversations  
    - Profitable growth with minimal capital and small team 
    - Pattern matches with early Razorpay founders in some aspects 
    - Takes time to think through answers when doesn't know something, rather than being glib  
    **Concerns raised:**
    - Not the typical risk-first fintech founder archetype that the firm usually backs  
    - Limited depth in regulatory and compliance thinking compared to typical fintech founders 
    - Appears more short-term focused rather than strategic long-term thinking  
    - Questions about coachability - described as "cocky" by some partners 
    - Limited time tracking these founders (only 2.5 weeks) 
    ### Traction & Customer Metrics
    - Strong customer wins: AstroTalk, Apps for Bharat, working with Swiggy, Shadi, Xflow  
    - Typically capturing 75% of customer volume after ramping up over 3-4 months 
    - Customers report urgency and significant value, with some saying "losing money every day" without switching 
    - Only losing deals when customers recently integrated with another provider or lack bandwidth 
    ### Market & Competitive Landscape
    - Global MOR market showing trend toward vertical-specific solutions (SaaS-specific, AI-specific, etc.)  
    - Comparable companies mentioned: Xsola (gaming-focused), Paddle, Lemon Squeezy  
    - Competitors in India: Razorpay, BigLocal, Road of Payments all have lower success rates  
    - Risk of market capping out discussed - historical MOR companies have faced this challenge  
    ### Product Differentiation
    - First to bring credit card installments for international cards in India 
    - Card EMI product with 2.8-3% risk-free take rate through partnership with US-based provider  
    - No risk taken on chargebacks for EMI product - all risk sits with partner  
    - Planning partnerships with local PSPs in each market (Cashfree, Xflow, EaseBuzz in India) 
    ### Key Risks & Concerns Discussed
    - Regulatory complexity across geographies and potential PSCB licensing conflicts  
    - Fraud and chargeback risk management (currently passing all chargeback risk to merchants) 
    - Thin value proposition if only solving for success rates - needs vertical-specific workflows  
    - Large Indian payment players (Razorpay, PhonePe, Pine Labs) cannot acquire due to PSCB regulations  
    - Potential for commoditization as merchants scale and seek lower-cost alternatives  
    ### Pricing & Deal Structure Discussion
    - Company seeking $10M round but partners skeptical this is firm 
    - Pricing discussion centered on 30-35 post-money, with some suggesting 40-45 
    - Multiple partners emphasized check size should be limited given unknowns - suggesting 3-4M per firm  
    - Preferred structure: Split check with high-quality co-investor at reasonable pricing  
    - Valuation approach: Some partners pricing off 15x net revenue one year forward

    ### Strategic Considerations
