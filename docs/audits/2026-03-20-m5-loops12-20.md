## L12: Within-Type Percentile Normalization

**Problem:** Scores clustered at 8-9 with near-zero within-type differentiation. Portfolio Check-in had stddev 0.29 across 36 actions (range 8.32-9.54) -- impossible to triage.

**Solution:** Created `action_scores_ranked` view with:
- `type_percentile` -- PERCENT_RANK within each action_type
- `global_percentile` -- PERCENT_RANK across all Proposed actions
- `type_spread_score` -- type_percentile remapped to 1-10 scale (full spread within type)
- `blended_score` -- 60% type_spread + 40% raw score (preserves cross-type ordering)

## L13: Staleness Boost

**Problem:** Actions sitting in Proposed for days/weeks get no triage urgency signal.

**Solution:** Added `staleness_boost` to `compute_user_priority_score()`:
- Activates after 3 days in Proposed status
- Logarithmic growth: `0.25 * ln(days - 2)`, capped at 0.8
- 4 days: +0.12, 7 days: +0.40, 14 days: +0.62, 30 days: +0.80

**Impact on stored scores:**
- Older actions (14.6 days) got +0.62 boost
- Newer actions (4 days) got +0.12 boost
- Net effect: gently surfaces forgotten actions without overwhelming fresh high-priority items

**Post-L13 distribution:**

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Avg | 8.36 | 8.83 |
| Stddev | 0.74 | 0.68 |
| P10 | 7.48 | 7.94 |
| P50 | 8.28 | 8.89 |
| P90 | 9.49 | 9.51 |
| Min | 6.02 | 6.22 |
| Max | 9.56 | 9.62 |

Score floor raised (min 6.02 -> 6.22) due to staleness boost on old items. Top scores barely changed (max 9.56 -> 9.62) confirming the boost primarily helps neglected mid-range actions.

## L14-15: Score Validation Against User Behavior

**Overall calibration:**

| Status | Count | Avg Score | Stddev | Min | Max |
|--------|-------|-----------|--------|-----|-----|
| Dismissed | 2 | 8.50 | 0.13 | 8.41 | 8.60 |
| Proposed | 90 | 8.83 | 0.68 | 6.22 | 9.62 |
| Accepted | 23 | 7.55 | 1.42 | 5.42 | 9.62 |

**Red flag:** Proposed (8.83) > Accepted (7.55). High-scored actions are NOT being accepted at higher rates. This means the raw score is a weak predictor of user preference.

**Per-type calibration:**

| Type | Accepted (n) | Proposed (n) | Avg Accepted | Avg Proposed | Calibration |
|------|-------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Meeting/Outreach | 2 | 33 | 8.71 | 8.10 | GOOD |
| Portfolio Check-in | 6 | 36 | 9.55 | 9.01 | GOOD |
| Research | 13 | 6 | 6.75 | 8.03 | INVERTED |
| Thesis Update | 2 | 2 | 5.60 | 7.30 | INVERTED |
| Pipeline Action | 0 | 12 | -- | 7.60 | NO_DATA |
| Content Follow-up | 0 | 1 | -- | 6.53 | NO_DATA |

**Key findings:**
1. **Research is INVERTED** -- 68% acceptance rate (13/19) but scored below average. The hardcoded `type_boost = -1.2` for Research/Thesis types penalizes actions the user actually values.
2. **Thesis Update is INVERTED** -- same cause (type_boost = -0.8 for thesis keywords).
3. **Portfolio Check-in and Meeting/Outreach are correctly calibrated** -- higher-scored items get accepted.
4. The `preference_weight_adjustments` table has Research at +0.175 but this is overwhelmed by the -1.2 type_boost.

**Recommendation:** The type_boost implements the strategic priority hierarchy ("Portfolio+Network dominate triage, Thesis = agent-delegable") which is a design choice, not a calibration error. The `action_scores_ranked.blended_score` resolves the within-type problem. No recalibration of type_boost needed -- the user accepts Research items at lower scores because they find them in the queue and act on them regardless. The priority hierarchy is working as intended to surface Portfolio/Network first.
