1. **Created `relationship_pairs` table** with canonical ordering (person_a_id < person_b_id):

## L50-51: Interaction Pattern Detection

### Problem
- No automated detection of deal acceleration, relationship cooling, negotiation signals, or escalation patterns
- Patterns hidden in raw interaction data, not surfaced as alerts

### Solution
**Created `detect_interaction_patterns()`** -- returns JSONB with 5 pattern types:

1. **DEAL_ACCELERATION** -- 2+ interactions in 30 days with same company, with deal signals. Severity: LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH based on count.
2. **RELATIONSHIP_COOLING** -- relationship pairs with 2+ prior interactions and last contact >21 days ago. Severity by days silent.
3. **NEGOTIATION_SIGNAL** -- interactions with financial terms (valuation, term sheet, ARR, funding, round, etc.) in deal_signals.
4. **OBLIGATION_ESCALATION** -- people with 2+ overdue/escalated obligations. Severity: MEDIUM/HIGH/CRITICAL.
5. **HIGH_FREQUENCY_CONTACT** -- people with 2+ interactions in 14 days.

## L52-53: Communication Intelligence Score

### Problem
- No per-person composite score for how "managed" a relationship is
- No way to identify poorly-managed high-priority contacts

### Solution
1. **`person_communication_profile(person_id)`** -- comprehensive JSONB with 6-factor composite score (0-10):
   - Interaction volume (0-2.0): more interactions = better managed
   - Recency (0-2.0): recent contact = better managed
   - Channel diversity (0-1.5): multiple channels = better reach
   - Signal richness (0-1.5): richer signals = better intelligence
   - Obligation health (0-1.5): fewer I-owe = well managed
   - Trend bonus (0-1.5): warming > stable > cooling

   Tiers: EXCELLENT (8+), GOOD (6+), FAIR (4+), POOR (2+), UNMANAGED (<2)

   Also returns: interaction details, obligation balance (I owe vs they owe), trend detection (14d vs prior 14d), relationship strength from graph.

2. **`communication_profiles` view** -- all people with interactions, ranked by score, with tier/trend/obligation breakdown.
