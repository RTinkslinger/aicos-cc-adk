    ### Meeting Overview
    - Discussion focused on various startups across different technology sectors and their investment potential  
    - Analysis of companies including Piatrika Biosystems, Alteon Energy, LightSpeed Photonics, Pixxel, and Armory
    - Framework shared for categorizing startups based on technical challenges and go-to-market readiness 
    ### Piatrika Biosystems
    - Building cloud-based Platform-As-A-Service (PAAS) for agri-genomic discoveries and plant breeding
    - Company focused on selling seeds rather than technology 
    - Raised a $1.2 million seed round in 2022 (originally targeted $2 million)  
    - Very early stage with basic testing but not at commercial scale yet 
    ### Alteon Energy
    - Working on high-altitude drone technology 
    - Concerns raised about technological feasibility and customer validation 
    - Facing significant engineering challenges rather than theoretical physics limitations   
    - Issues identified:
      - Energy requirements exceed expectations 
      - Motor lifespan limitations due to vibration, load cycles and stress 
      - Marine environment challenges including humidity, salt, and electronics degradation 
      - Founder capability questions regarding understanding the true engineering challenges 
    ### LightSpeed Photonics
    - Developing chip-to-chip communication using lasers 
    - Solving data bottleneck issues in semiconductor chips 
    - Technology involves miniaturized lasers that convert optical to electrical data 
    - Categorized as a "hard engineering challenge" company  
    - Founded by highly qualified team with relevant experience and patents 
    ### Pixxel
    - Satellite/imaging technology company in the investor's portfolio 
    - Currently servicing $50 million worth of contracts 
    - Expected to close $18 million in revenue this calendar year
    - Founded in 2019 and reached significant revenue within 6 years 
    - Differentiated by creating better imaging capabilities than previously available 
    ### Armory
    - Defense technology company with ₹94 crore in orders from Navy and ₹10 crore from Army 
    - Technically qualified for another ₹25 crore order
    - Developing RF jammer technology 
    - Founded by IIT Bombay aerospace engineer with bachelor's and master's degrees 
    - Competitive advantage through India-made products and customer relationships  
    ### Investment Framework
    - Four categories of startups discussed:  
      1. Science challenges (quantum computing, rocket launches) - difficult to fund with limited fund size/life
      1. Hard engineering challenges (like LightSpeed Photonics) - backed when right team is present
      1. Hardware execution companies (like Pixxel and Armory) - faster market entry with competitive moat
      1. Accessible technology companies (AI, drones, EVs) - using off-the-shelf technology
