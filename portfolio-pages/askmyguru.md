**Overall View: Numbers are not encouraging**

- GTV growth is conversion-led rather than depth-led, with ATV and frequency more/less flat.

- Chat adoption is strong, but declining sessions and time spent indicate high intent without habit.

- CAC efficiency has improved, but payback signals remain weak.

*Note: All current numbers are based on Nov-25 .*



**Engagement**

- Logged-in MAU ~1.7L, growing ~30%+ on a 5M CAGR basis, but DAU/MAU at ~8% is declining sequentially (vs. Jun’25 at 28%).

- Chat conversion is strong at ~85–90% of MAU; however, average sessions per month are ~3 and average daily time spent is ~5.8 minutes, both declining, indicating high intent but weak habit formation.





**Conversion & Monetisation**

- Depositor penetration at ~7–8% of MAU and is almost flat vs. the last 3M (nearly doubled vs. last month due to a dip in the prior month).

- GTV has grown ~2x in the last 3M to ~Rs.10L but is driven by higher conversion (8-9%) rather than higher ATV or frequency, both of which remain flat over L3M.



**Retention and CAC Payback**

- D7 retention is at 35% and D30 is at 25%.

- CAC is at Rs.172 and has trended down vs. the T3M average of Rs.274.

- Even after the reduction in CAC, CAC payback does not look great, with D7 and M0 ROAS at ~50% in Nov’25 and in the range of 25–30% over T3M.



**P&L & financial trajectory**

- Monthly Net GMV is ~Rs.9.5L and up 2x over L3M.

- CM1 turned positive at +38% in Nov’25, as AI costs scaled more slowly than revenue.

- CM2 stands at -211% due to high performance marketing costs (250% as a % of GMV).

- EBITDA-level monthly burn is ~Rs.80L.



**Projections (super aggressive)**

- Net monthly GMV is projected to reach Rs.50L in the next 3M and ~Rs.20Cr by Dec-26.

- CM1 is projected at 66% in the next 3M and 94% by Dec-26, driven by slower growth in AI costs relative to sales ramp-up.

- EBITDA burn is projected at Rs.1.15Cr in the next 3M (driven by marketing spend and other fixed costs) and to be flat by Dec-26 (with CM1 offset by marketing spend).



- **Assumptions**

- CAC is assumed to stabilise at Rs.200 (vs. Rs.172 today and a T3M average of Rs.275).

- Retention is assumed to improve from 25% today to 28% in the next 3M and then stabilise at 33%.

- ATV is assumed to increase from Rs.70 today (flat over L3M) to Rs.100 in the next 3M and stabilise at Rs.120.

## MIS & deck from founder 2026-01-14T09:18:00.000+05:30 







### Deck



**Company / product:** AskMyGuru positions itself as an “AI Astrology Companion” and a broader “human life problem-solution platform” grounded in Bharat’s Vedic wisdom, with a longer-term ambition to become an AI Vedic Shastra “companion app” that helps users seek/learn/practice multiple shastras (starting with Vedic astrology / Vedanga).



**Vision / mission:** Build the world’s “first and most accessible AI Vedic Shastra Companion App,” using Vedic Astrology as the entry point and then supporting users with relevant shastras + “personalised lifestyle companionship.”



**Founding team:** Krishna Mohan Vedula (CEO & Co-Founder; founder of Striker; ex-VP MPL) and Vivek Vishwanath Sadamate (Co-Founder; founding member MPL). Deck also highlights a core team across Product/Design, AI/Tech, Data Science/ML, and Content.



**Market framing:** They cite a “large global TAM” with “>1Bn users” and claim “only astrology” is a **$58.56B** market growing **10.4% CAGR**, with a “resurgence of faith” among young Indians and reference to existing vertical players across astrology, puja/devotion, and bhakti.



**Industry insights they’re anchoring on:**

- **Chat beats call:** 90% of users prefer chat; privacy/anonymity are key reasons.

- **Acquisition:** >80% via Facebook; tier 2/3/4 spend time on Meta; “CAC is lower for a new platform.”

- **Demand mix:** Relationship advice is the top query; then marriage/career/children; health last.

- **Expert behavior:** Users prefer experts but don’t stick to one expert after getting an opinion.

- **Monetization dynamics:** Human “resolutions” have ₹250–₹1000 ticket sizes; high ticket sizes can hurt payer retention.

- **International:** US & Australia contribute >30% of revenue for incumbents; NRIs prefer India-based platforms.



**Problem statement / gaps in current astrology platforms:**

- **One-time payer economics:** Human-guru constraints force dependence on constant new users.

- **Rising CACs:** More “astrology-only” players entering.

- **“AI astrology gap”:** Lack of subject-matter depth causes training gaps → inaccurate resolutions.

- **No “platform play”:** No single platform closes the loop (identify problem → remedies → execute → daily Vedic lifestyle) across astrology + puja + bhakti.



**Their “why now / why win” (how they claim they disrupt):**

- **AI Guru companions:** Lower subscription rates, higher chat volume, more accurate resolutions, and “guru <> user affinity.”

- **Subscription / repeat payer:** “Unlimited free chats” (Astro/Ayurveda/Gita), astrology reports, content (soon).

- **Lower CAC via platform strategy:** They claim ~70% of acquisitions come via non-astro categories (notably reports).

- **Hyper-paced AI-first execution:** Build categories, features, and content faster.



**Product roadmap:**

- **Phase 1 (short term):** Convert one-time payers into repeat payers by building AI astrology companions optimized for astrological accuracy and long-form conversations.

- **Phase 2 (long term):** “Vedic super app” aimed at payer retention—Astrology + Ayurveda + Gita, reports, additional shastras (Vastu, palmistry, Agama Shastra), puja/chadhava, and content (e.g., Upanishads), geared toward a Vedic lifestyle.



**How the “AI companions” are described (key differentiators):**

- **Computation for astrological accuracy:** Deep mathematical work on charts/dashas “at breakneck speed” per query.

- **Many personas:** “100+ AI Guruavataars” trained with different personalities/emotional storytelling to match “Guru <> Seeker.”

- **24x7 + long context:** Always available with context storage for long-form conversations.

- **Faster category rollout:** New Vedic categories can be launched quickly (Upanishads/Gita/Ayurveda).



**“Vedic super app” loop (their long-term product thesis):** Identify the problem via astrology → connect to personalized Vedic remedies → execute remedies via gamified rituals/habits (examples include Brahma Muhurta reminders, guru coins for jaap, suggested rituals/mantras).



**Current product offering (as shown in-app):** AI Companions, Astrology Reports, Free Daily Reads, Free Kundali.



**Coming soon features (as shown):** Audio content, multi-profile, kundli match-making, Vastu Shastra.



**Go-to-market / growth channels:** Multi-channel plan: Meta & Google Ads, “SEO optimization for AI search engines,” WhatsApp marketing, and product referral/social loops.



**Traction / metrics (since launch June 14, 2025; Google Play Open Beta):** **9L+ downloads**, **7Cr+ questions asked**, **100+ AI Guruavataars**, **$250K+ ARR**.



**Unit economics & engagement claims (high-level):** They show charts for CPI vs depositor conversion trend, ROAS vs time with a ₹51 subscription, CPD (monthly cost per depositor), engagement trends (all users vs payers), MAU growth, payer conversion %, revenue growth, GMV/CM1, and M1 retention.



**Payer behavior stats called out explicitly:** **43%** of payments from repeat depositors (incl. subscribers); payers spend **22 min/day** and **77 min/month**; avg chats increased **5 → 26** for subscribers; subscription cancellations dropped **60% → 44%** in 4 weeks; report-category payers have week-1 ROAS ~**65%**; reports are **26%** of overall deposit amount.



**2026 plan / targets:** Q1–Q2: **>$1M ARR**, launch iOS + desktop, and launch in USA+Canada; Q3–Q4: **>$8M ARR**.



**Appendix / worldview framing:** Includes a “Vedic Shastra knowledge tree” map and examples of life problems by age cohort mapped to specific shastras (e.g., Jyotish, Gita, Arthashastra, Yoga Sutras, Ayurveda, Upanishads, Manusmriti, Mahabharata).



**Parent entity framing:** AskMyGuru is under “Bharat Ek Khoj,” whose stated purpose is to discover/preserve/share Bharat’s cultural heritage using modern tech.
