**Founder-Meeting Prep – Atica Global**

### **1. Company Overview**

- **What they do (plain English).** Atica is an outsourced, tech-enabled commercial engine for hotels. It combines a proprietary lead-generation/CRM platform that scrapes local business demand with human “remote sales managers,” digital-marketing specialists and revenue-management analysts. The pitch: deliver up to **40 % YoY revenue lift** for mid-scale U.S. hotels without adding on-property head-count.

- **Pain point & urgency.** Most limited-service and select-service hotels either (a) leave group/corporate sales to a brand’s central team or (b) rely on a single on-property DOS. That under-indexes mid-week occupancy and ADR. Revenue-management talent is scarce/expensive; digital channels (OTAs, Google Hotel Ads, social) change weekly. Atica promises a one-stop plug-in to fill that capability gap.

- **Uniqueness / edge.** The blend of **software + managed service** is unusual in a space dominated by pure SaaS (Duetto, IDeaS) or pure agencies. If their lead-scoring dataset and offshore labor arbitrage both scale, they could keep CAC low and gross margins >60 %.

- **Why better than status quo.** Compared with hiring a revenue manager (~$90-120 k/yr) and agency support, Atica advertises month-to-month pricing and no cap-ex integration; everything runs remotely through their CRM and OTA dashboards.

---

### **2. Market Analysis**

| Slice | 2024 Market Size | 2024-34 CAGR | Notes | Sources |

| Hotel revenue-management systems | $1.2 B | 12 % | Projected to hit $3.4 B by 2033 |  |

| Wider hotel management software | $4.3 B | 7.6 % | $8.8 B by 2034 |  |

*Dynamics.* Fragmented today, but capital is consolidating around winners (e.g., Lighthouse/OTA Insight became a unicorn after a **$370 M KKR-led round, $2.4 B valuation**).    Many independents still run spreadsheets, so green-field remains, but once a chain standardizes on a Duetto/IDeaS-type RMS, switching costs rise.

*Macro tailwinds.*

- Post-COVID labor shortages push owners to outsource back-office roles.

- Google is phasing out free hotel booking links, forcing sharper paid-channel strategy.

- Generative-AI tooling lowers the bar to build “me-too” sales-automation apps – raises noise level.

*Winner-take-most?* Likely **“barbell.”** Global chains anchor to a few enterprise RMS vendors; independent & small-brand hotels could be served by dozens of boutique players. Atica’s wedge is U.S. select-service properties (Marriott Courtyard, Hampton, Wyndham, Choice).

### **4. Business Model & Early Financials**

- **Revenue streams.** Monthly retainer (no annual contract) plus %-of-upswing or small RevPAR-linked success fee. Website highlights “no annual contract” messaging.

- **Pricing / unit economics.** If a 120-room hotel pays $3-4 k/mo and Atica services one manager : 8-10 hotels from India, gross margin plausibly 60-65 % once mature. Need exact pay-roll assumptions.

- **Traction.** Client logos include Marriott, Hilton, IHG flags via franchisees (not corporate), but numbers undisclosed.

- **Funding.** March 2023 seed round, size undisclosed, **led by Titan Capital plus DLF family office & Byju’s tech execs**.

- **Revenue guess.** Growjo’s **$54 M** estimate is very likely web-scraped noise; 100-150 paying hotels at $40-50 k ARR would suggest $4-7 M run-rate—still attractive at seed. Flag for confirmation.

- **Runway.** Unknown. If they raised < $3 M and run a 70-person India team, cash-burn could be $150-200 k/mo → < 18 months runway unless profitable.

---

### **5. Competitive Landscape**

| Type | Example | Funding / Valuation | Strength vs. Atica | Weakness |

| Enterprise RMS | Duetto | $143 M raised, acquired by PE in 2024 | Deep pricing science, chain integrations | Pure SaaS, no outbound-sales component |

| All-in-one BI/RMS | Lighthouse (ex-OTA Insight) | $370 M, $2.4 B val. | Massive data network, AI bench | Focus on pricing/intel, not lead-gen |

| Mid-market RMS | Pace, Atomize, RoomPriceGenie | $5-35 M funding | UI simplicity, price | Limited services layer |

| Agencies/outsourcers | Aimbridge’s central sales desk, independent revenue-management consultants | Low cap-ex | Human relationship depth | No proprietary tech |

*Defensibility.* Atica’s moat must come from (a) proprietary SMB-contact database for local accounts, and (b) process IP around remote execution. Both could be replicated by a well-funded competitor within 12-18 months; they need network effects (data fly-wheel) or deep integrations (brand CRS, PMS) to lock in.

---

### **6. Risks & Red Flags**

1. **Service-heavy model.** Scaling people faster than software compresses margin; may cap valuation multiple.

2. **Tech differentiation unclear.** Lead-scraper & email sequences are commodity. Without IP around demand forecasting or pricing AI, switching costs are low.

3. **Competitive intensity.** Unicorn-level capital entering space; Lighthouse & Duetto are out-spending on R&D and partnerships.

4. **Cyclic exposure.** A U.S. recession cuts corporate transient demand first—exactly Atica’s target segment. Retention in a down-cycle untested.

5. **Transparency gaps.** Conflicting employee counts and no disclosed ARR; need hard KPIs (hotels onboarded, net-retention, gross margin).

6. **Platform dependency.** Reliant on Google/OTAs APIs; policy changes (e.g., Expedia removing email address from booking confirmations) could erode their outbound data.

---

### **7. Founder Questions to Stress-Test**

1. **Unit economics:** What is fully-loaded gross margin per hotel after 24 months, and how does it trend as you automate more workflows?

2. **Retention & NRR:** 2024 churn rate, reasons for churn, and net revenue retention across cohorts.

3. **Tech moat:** What proprietary datasets or ML models are impossible for Lighthouse/Duetto to replicate in < 12 months?

4. **Scalability:** How many hotels can one remote manager handle today vs. target, and what tooling enables that step-change?

5. **Customer mix risk:** What % of revenue comes from the top 5 hotel owners? How diversified is the book across brands & geographies?

6. **Capital plan:** How much runway after the seed? What milestones (ARR, product) trigger the next raise and at what target multiple?

7. **Exit vision:** Do you see Atica becoming a pure-play SaaS platform over time, or will the services component always be core? How does that influence eventual acquisition vs. IPO?

---

### **Bottom Line**

Atica is tackling a genuine pain point with a sensible tech-plus-service wedge, and early reference customers in mid-scale U.S. hotels de-risk GTM. The opportunity could be **$1-2 B TAM** if they capture the long-tail of 30 k U.S. limited-service hotels. However, lack of clear product moat and heavy services footprint mean this is more likely a **$200-300 M exit** than a fund-returning unicorn unless they can pivot to majority-SaaS and prove >120 % NRR. Push hard on unit economics, true automation depth, and churn data before leaning in.
