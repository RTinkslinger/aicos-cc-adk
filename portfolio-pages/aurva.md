### **1. Company Overview**

- **What they do – in plain English**

Aurva builds an AI-driven data-security platform that sits at the **runtime layer** (containers, VMs, on-prem DBs) and watches how sensitive data actually *moves* inside production systems. It continuously discovers data stores, classifies sensitive fields, maps flows, and enforces policies such as blocking, tokenisation or deletion.

- **Pain point & urgency**

India’s forthcoming DPDP Rules and mounting global frameworks (GDPR, PDPL, SOC 2, ISO 27001) are forcing engineering and security teams to prove, not just claim, that data is handled correctly. Current “scan-and-report” DSPM tools flag exposures *after* the fact; Aurva promises **real-time lineage and prevention**, cutting audit fatigue and breach liability.

- **Why it’s hard to copy**

Aurva’s sensor uses eBPF to tap kernel-level network and process events without sidecars or restarts, giving near-zero-overhead visibility that competitors typically achieve only via proxies or heavyweight agents.

- **Status & traction (from internal notes)**

*~$400 k ARR* (April 2025) across design-partner logos such as Jupiter, Razorpay and early POCs at Axis, Ujjivan. Avg ACV ≈ $50 k; cloud-only today, on-prem SKU slated for July 2025 (expected to lift ACV).

---

### **2. Market Analysis**

| Metric | India focus | Global relevance |

| Size (2024) | $5.34 B data-protection spend | $20 B+ DSPM/DLP/DAM combined |

| Growth | 18.3 % CAGR to $27.8 B by 2033 | AI-induced data-risk uptick every geography |

Sources:

**Dynamics:**

- DPDP enforcement (Rules released Jan 3 2025; grace period expected ≤ 12 months) acts as a **regulatory forcing function**—particularly for BFSI, fintech and large consumer apps.

- Market is **fragmented** now; likely to consolidate once compliance deadlines hit because buyers prefer a single runtime + reporting layer.

### **5. Competitive Landscape**

| Player | Edge vs Aurva | Gap Aurva must close |

| Securiti.ai – “PrivacyOps” ($50 M Series B, 2024) | Mature consent + DSAR workflows; big-logo references | Heavier deploy, weaker runtime visibility |

**Moat reality:** Aurva must translate eBPF runtime insight into *stickiness* (automated prevention, high-fidelity alerts) before incumbents add similar telemetry.

---

### **6. Risks & Red Flags**

1. **GTM execution** – Long sales cycles (6-12 mo) with ACV still sub-$100 k; founder doing most deals.

2. **Market timing** – If DPDP Rule enforcement slips, near-term urgency in India could wane.

3. **Feature breadth vs focus** – Multiple “random partnerships” and experiments (per Z47 feedback) risk diluting roadmap clarity.

4. **Competition with well-funded platforms** – A Palo Alto or Varonis could replicate runtime flow mapping within 12-18 mo given resources.

5. **On-prem SKU delivery** – Tech lift + support complexity; slips would hurt BFSI pipeline credibility.

---

### **7. Questions to Stress-Test the Founders**

1. **ACV expansion:** How will you double ACV in 12 months—bundled modules, seat-based upsells, or on-prem premium?

2. **DPDP contingency:** If enforcement is delayed, what non-regulatory pain pushes prospects to buy now?

3. **On-prem roadmap:** What specific milestones ensure GA by **July 2025**? How will you handle version drift & support SLAs?

4. **Channel vs direct:** What is the concrete partner-sourced pipeline today (value, stage), and how do you avoid channel conflict?

5. **Tech moat longevity:** What prevents eBPF approaches from becoming table stakes once open-source probes mature?

6. **International GTM:** Which geography comes *after* India, why, and who will lead it?

7. **Cash plan:** Assuming no pre-emptive Series A, when do you hit cash-flow breakeven or raise again? What triggers that decision?

---

**Bottom line:**

Aurva is a **credible DPDP-timed bet** with differentiated runtime tech and founders who understand both infra-scale and privacy pain. To become a fund-returner, they must (a) prove on-prem delivery, (b) professionalise GTM to lift ACVs, and (c) stay ahead of deep-pocketed competitors racing into runtime data security.
