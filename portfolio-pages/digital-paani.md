## Quaterly update sent on 2024-10-09 

Hello from DigitalPaani! It’s been a regrettably long time since our last update. Sincere apologies for this lag - there’s been a LOT going on. I was also unwell for a while in the middle and then made the mistake of waiting for some big bang announcements which were on the brink of closure before writing the next update. From now on, we’ll just make sure to stick to a regular cadence irrespective of everything else going on.

That said, here’s what we’ve been upto! This will be a long-ish update to catch up :)

**Highlights**

**Overall gut check: **This is a very fuzzy metric, but I read somewhere recently that no metric is more predictive of how a startup is doing than the gut feeling of the founders. While my gut feeling has been through a lot of ups and downs in the last 6 months, I’m currently feeling GREAT about our future. Here’s why:

- **People and Processes: **We’ve built a strong team (35 people now). A few months ago, we onboarded a senior leader as VP Growth. Under him, we then began building a BD + sales team which now comprises 4 people all showing great promise. Similarly, we elevated our star performer in the technology team and built the rest of the team under him, and our velocity has gone up substantially. At this point, each team has clear performance metrics, review dashboards, and accountability structures, and the team overall has clear people operations processes (values, hiring and onboarding process, performance eval, and so on)

- **Business Model:** We’ve sharpened our strategy substantially. This is how we sell now:

- **Building sector (commercial and residential building):** DigitalPaani-enabled operations contracts in geographical clusters; we take the O&M at an equivalent cost to a manual services provider but provide strong SLAs, energy savings, and much improved results. We’ve productized the offering to a point where we can meet a customer and give them a precise 1-pager proposal on the spot. Overall, I would say our strategy is crystal clear and largely proven here, and now we’re doubling down on sales and execution.

- **Industrial segment: We’ve broken up our work into sharp offerings clearly linked to value drivers (compliance/ water recyclability, energy saving, and chemical saving) which has streamlined the entire sales process. We’re doing on ground sales in certain industrial clusters for target segments with these (paper, pharmaceuticals, and automobiles) and have been able to cut short a lot of the steps which were elongating the sales cycle.**

- **Strategic deals to other water players typically catering to government contracts: We’re focused on cracking these deals now at the leadership level as all of them are multi-crore in nature.**

**With this overall context, here’s where we’ve reached:**

- **Sales: **This FY, we’re expecting to at least triple our revenue (last year closed at ~2.2CR). The start of the year was a bit slow, but with all this work on the sales process, there’s a lot happening now. Some worth highlighting are:

- Paperwork for our first multi-crore deal where the 1 year revenue is almost equivalent to our annual revenue last year is closing in the next week! We’re also at very late stages with 2 other large enterprise customers which have been challenging and hard fought for :)

- A mid-sized deal for a Lodha township which hopefully is the start of a larger partnership with them

- We got our first airport contract, doubled down on Goa as a geography with 4 contracts now, and expanded existing accounts (Leela, Amazon, Britannia). Overall, we’ve crossed 50 facilities.

- **Product: Besides up-levelling our software (happy to give a demo for more clarity), we are working on 2 proprietary hardware-software-computer vision products for the industrial segment that we’re going to patent. We think that in about 9 months time, our software offerings will far surpass even global competitors. On multiple feature sets, we’re there.**

- **Market presence: **This year, we’ve worked on establishing a strong brand. Post our Shark Tank episode, we’ve seen a big boom in inbounds + developed champions for long term collaborations in organisations like IOCL. We also won the Cartier Women’s Entrepreneur Award for Asia which was a sizable grant.

**Asks**

- We’re hiring for a few crucial roles, particularly a Frontend Lead (~4-6 years of exp; SDE3 level; in person in Gurgaon), and a Service Delivery Head (technical services/ operations experiences across multiple assets; strong communications/client management skillset along with internal ops and org building skillset). If someone comes to mind, let us know.

- Any warm intros to water EPC players, paper and pulp mills, automobiles, hotels, developers, or colleges/ schools will be very helpful.

- Low interest debt finance; for the building sector, we’re selling on a recurring fee only and amortising the IoT assets required.

- Connections to technology companies that have cracked the industrial sales process as a startup. Looking to gain more insights and further finetune.

In our next update, I’ll share more on revenue projections and fundraising plans; the deals that are closing now will have a significant impact on those, so we’re reworking a bit on those.

Thank you!

Mansi

## April to June Update (2025)

Dear Divyanshi,

Hello from DigitalPaani! We’re excited to share what we’ve been upto this last quarter and what we’re focusing on now. We’d be especially grateful for support on our asks and request you to treat these updates as strictly confidential.

**Recap of what we did**

·  **Sales: **This was our best Q1 ever by a long shot! Historically, we’ve found Q1s to be the slowest -  last year, we closed only 1 order in Q1. This year though, we closed 7 orders if the first few days of July are included, with an order value crossing Rs 3CR. This included 2 repeat orders and our first ever sale to a large water company, which is a GTM we’ve been working on for some time.

·  **Product: **Our product and technology is progressing ahead on full steam. We worked this last quarter on making our system AI first, and rolled out the first set of features in this vein such as providing better diagnosis of any process issue’s root cause. We also rolled out our first few specialized computer vision models detecting specific process issues such as poor biological health. We’ll be applying for patents for these.

·  **Other fun things: **We got to present our work at the World Economic Forum summer Davos as one of the only speakers on water.

·  **Other key numbers:**

o Team size: 40

o MRR: Contracted around 25L; currently billing around 19L per month

o Runway: 12-14 months, but subject to change right now with the closures expected (time to start the next fundraise!)

**What are we focusing on?**

This year is going to be one of transformation for us. Key glimpses:

·  **GTM focus:**

o We’re focusing on 3 core strategies:

§ Delhi NCR and Maharashtra cities: Tech-enabled full stack operations sales in buildings. This model is gaining significant momentum, with 4/7 facilities closed under this model

§ Going deep in a single geography to crack industrial sales (we’ve chosen the Dahej region). This model is showing strong early promise, but needs dedicated focus to generate ultimate results.

§ Collaborating with water companies from the ‘new project’ stage. We’ve cracked our first deal and have many larger ones in the pipeline.

·  **AI everywhere and deep focus on operational efficiency:**

o Besides product, one of our big quarterly priorities is tightening internal operations by using AI to make things more efficient across the board as well as using Jira across the entire team.

·  **Fundraising:**

o We will be beginning our fundraise process in August.

**Asks**

·  Connections and warm leads to begin conversations for fundraising, including HNIs and family offices besides VCs

·  Low interest project finance/ debt capital for financing IoT assets backed by long term contracts

·  Help connecting to:

o Industrial customers in Gujarat belt who are water or process intensive, say paper, chemical, pharma, textiles

o Technical consultants who have significant influence with municipal corporations

o Large infrastructure companies with water operations like NCC, Voltas, SusBio, UEM, REIPL, Aquatech, Tata Projects, Shapoorji Pallonji, Hindustan Construction Company etc

Thank you so much!
