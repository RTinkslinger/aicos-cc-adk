Shreyansh, CEO of Finsire, detailed the company's shift from a multi-product approach to focusing exclusively on becoming a secured credit marketplace. The discussion covered their business model, partnerships, revenue streams, and fundraising plans.

Business Pivot

- De-prioritized the data business (wealth monitor and analytics products)

- These were burn-heavy and service-oriented without margin expansion

- Focusing entirely on secured credit business

- Discovered better economics as distributors scaled

- Less need to increase costs as they add more distributors

Business Model

- Finsire acts as a Technology Service Provider (TSP) for banks offering Loan Against Securities (LAS) and Loan Against Mutual Funds (LAMF)

- Creates a two-sided marketplace connecting banks (supply) with distributors (demand)

- Deeply integrates with banks' systems including core banking, loan management, and decision engines

- Handles pledging, de-pledging, collateral management, and risk policies

Competitive Differentiation

- Unlike competitors (Yenmo, Volt Money), Finsire is on the "manufacturing" side

- Exclusive TSP partnerships with banks (banks prefer single TSP due to reconciliation challenges)

- Product advantages over NBFC offerings:

- Lower interest rates (banks have lower cost of capital)

- Instant credit line creation

- Better margin call policies

- Lower minimum loan amounts (₹10,000 vs ₹25,000)

Partnerships

- Exclusive LAS partnerships with 4 banks with ₹2,000 crore mandate per bank

- Distribution partners include:

- MobiKwik (already live)

- PhonePe (launching in 2-3 months)

- Groww (signed contract for loans ≤₹20L per RBI regulations)

Revenue Model

- One-time implementation fees

- Annual contracts (₹12L to ₹30L from banks)

- Interest spread (currently getting 200 bps - sell to customer at 10.5% vs 8.5% cost)

- Split with distributors (70/30 for large players, 50/50 for smaller ones)

- Transaction fees (1% of loan amount)

Financial Progress

- Raised ₹10.5 crore, with ₹5 crore still in the bank

- Achievements:

- Built exclusive LAS partnerships with 4 banks

- Won mandates for both ETB (existing-to-bank) and NTB (new-to-bank) customers

- Outbid competitors like M2P, Nucleus, and Digio

- Pathway to ₹80 crore ARR over 4 years without launching additional products

Future Plans

- Expanding product suite to include:

- Mutual Fund backed card (secured Simpl equivalent)

- Secured POS payments

- Considering NBFC opportunity in 15-16 month outlook

- Potential co-lending partnerships with existing bank partners
