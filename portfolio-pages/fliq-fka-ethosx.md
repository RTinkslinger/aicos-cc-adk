### **APPENDIX : Product Context**

1. [Operps](https://operps.kanalabs.io/) is our first product which we launched last year on the BNB & ARB mainnets (in partnership with another startup Kanalabs). We provided the mathematical design and the blockchain infrastructure and Kanalabs team built the UI and launched it on their website. It is basically an on-chain tokenized risk management tool which acts somewhat like a perpetual option for BTC,ETH,SOL & DOGE and has cash flows every 15-minute on ARB (BNB version has been decommissioned). Now this product is completely independent of Kanalabs and is on our website.

2. OTC Options - This is an RFQ based OTC Options trading platform which is connected to an LCH style, completely on-chain clearing house for post-trade operations. This product was actually built first and is in Prototype stage for a while. This needs significant market-maker support before launch. We intend to establish market maker relationships starting with Operps and then over time, onboard them for this product as well. Hence it becomes even more important to get market makers for Operps.

- Liquidation Protection - This is a product built on top of the above platform and provides 100 % automated liquidation protection for users of DeFi lending protocols. This is also in the prototype stage.

### Where we are now

Since launching real-money trading in September, we’ve crossed:

- [**$10.5M+ total volume**](https://dune.com/kamyth/fliq-dashboard/2dd3d598-84c7-4ce1-b1bf-3f2b383f1977)

- **$6M+ traded in November alone** (up from **$2.7M in October** – >100% month-on-month growth)

- **5,000+ traders** in total, with **~500 trading actively right now**

- **100+ creators** running markets and markets in localised languages spanning form Bengali to Naija

- **2 live partners** ([Stan ](https://stan.fliq.one/#/)&[ DGbet](https://dgbet.fliq.one/)), with a few more in the pipeline

We’re also seeing **inbound demand from exchanges, social apps, and other platforms** that want to plug into Fliq’s prediction markets rather than building their own.

### How we actually found our audience

Most of our traders today are **Web2 users** in **Nigeria and Bangladesh** - they don’t care about “DeFi,” they care about calling things right.

- **Nigeria**: football, crypto, and local entertainment

- **Bangladesh**: iGaming and cricket

We didn’t plan this top-down. Our **creators pulled us there**.

Creators started onboarding their own communities - friends, Telegram groups, betting circles - and that’s how we discovered both our **geos** and our **topic clusters**. That’s now where we’re doubling down: turning these creator-led pockets into deep, repeatable verticals.

In the process, we’ve learned a lot about **how creators onboard users** and **how those users actually trade**.

### What a consumer product needs in emerging markets

Building for these users looks very different from building for US/EU crypto natives. A few things are non-negotiable:

- **Mobile-first**: most users only ever see Fliq on a mid-range Android phone.

- **Works on low network**: 3G-level connections, packet loss, and intermittent coverage are normal - the app still has to feel fast.

- **Ultra-simple UX**: large buttons, minimal steps, no jargon; users will not fight the product.

- **Low-ticket, high-frequency**: the product must feel comfortable and fun at **$1-$5** trade sizes, not just $100+.

A lot of our product work has been around **speed, simplicity, and trust** for exactly this environment.

### Experiment 1: Making the very first trade stupidly easy

A big unlock was admitting that **most of our users have never touched crypto or USDT**. Even with Google login, “fund your wallet with USDT” is a hard stop.

So we shipped a very simple, almost gimmicky first interaction:

- A **first-time user gets $0.10 of trading credit**

- They can’t withdraw this credit - **only profits are withdrawable**

- The flow is a **gamified YES/NO market** so they can experience the mechanics once without friction

This does a few things for us:

- Lets a pure Web2 user understand **how prediction markets work** in one click

- Reduces drop-off at the “funding” step

- Gets them to that first “I was right” moment as fast as possible

We’re already seeing this shorten the time from **landing → first trade**, especially in low-trust, low-education environments around crypto.

### Experiment 2: Turning creators into their own market makers

On the liquidity side, our long-term goal is **end-to-end infrastructure where creators can run their own markets and their own liquidity**, with Fliq handling the plumbing.

We already provide initial liquidity on key markets, but we don’t want to be the only liquidity provider. So we’ve been iterating on **“liquidity credits”**:

- Creators can **purchase liquidity credits** to seed their own markets

- Those credits plug into **our market-making system**, which then provides depth on their behalf

- To make this more accessible, we **reduced the default credit size from $10 to $5**

This sounds small, but it changes who can play: instead of only a handful of large creators seeding markets, **a much wider set of mid-tail creators can now spin up and own their markets**.

Over time, this is how we get from “Fliq-owned liquidity” to **thousands of creator-owned micro-exchanges** across regions and topics.

We’re still early, but November was the first month where the flywheel felt real:

creators → local communities → local topics → product changes → better onboarding → more creators.

We’re continuing to raise to scale liquidity, deepen these Nigeria/Bangladesh beachheads, and replicate the model across more Africa + Asia corridors. If you’d like to dig into the numbers or talk about how this could compound with the right capital and partners, we’d be very happy to chat.
