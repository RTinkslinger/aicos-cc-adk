Dear investors,



Sharing updates for the month



1. We don’t have major traction updates this month, and hence I wanted to share a few thoughts on how we are looking at new competition and technology.

2. **We saw our first direct competitor for the studio rendering product **[https://x.com/bilawalsidhu/status/1803096170769567787](https://x.com/bilawalsidhu/status/1803096170769567787). This validates our novel approach towards using rough 3D assets to render images and videos

1. The launch of [Luma DreamMachine](https://lumalabs.ai/dream-machine) and [Runway’s Gen-3](https://runwayml.com/ai-tools/gen-3-alpha/)  have further validated and sharpened our vision for the future - consistency and control with new modalities are key for powerful generative AI applications. This foreshadows that low-level content creation will become commoditized with text prompts, while **the top 10% creators (content that most of us consume) will seek tools that give them more control to create novelty**.

2. Video generation will become table-stakes for all creative tools companies in this space. There are several approaches here

3. **Text prompts: **easy-to-use but hard to control 

4. **Image prompts with keyframes:** better but still doesn’t allow the user to control cameras and ensure subject consistency for longer durations.

5. **3D “prompts”: **3D has the highest-degree of camera and subject control. However, 3D assets and scenes are hard to create. A 4D generative model conditioned on rough 3D scenes and assets can offer the best of both worlds, **and we are well-positioned to build this foundation model, a new type of rendering engine that will change how video games, TV and films are made**.

6. Of course we will need a lot more resources to execute this vision and compete effectively. Hence, over the next few months, we will focus our efforts on stabilizing our studio product and building a v0 of the renderer that can validate our thesis and make the case for scaling it up.

7. Product

8. We are working towards a timeline/animation feature in the 3D studio that are stepping stones towards video

9. The studio will get a massive UI update in July

10. We are testing a new product called, tentatively called *Bash,* that will allow our power users to generate 3D assets from parts and then assemble them into a single 3D asset. This allow more detail/resolution when creating 3D assets with image prompts. We plan to add more-detailed textures/materials as well.

## Fondant Investor Update - August 2024



Dear investors,



TL;DR: We're freezing current Studio development and focusing on automating creation of animatable 3d characters.

### **Longer version**



Over the past two months, we've spoken with users, customers, and investors to (in)validate our thesis on leveraging generative AI for gaming, VFX, and animation.

Some of those conversations have led to a strategic change in direction, where we are freezing our current Studio product, and narrowing our focus on workflows where we can add value immediately.



Key insights:

1. Commercially available AI 3D asset-generation tools  haven’t gained wide acceptance in production *yet. *Why?

2. Clean topology and geometry — essential for UV mapping, rigging and animation — remains unsolved. Unlike image and video generation, this problem is hard to tackle due to the lack of a publicly available high-quality large-scale 3D dataset

3. 3D artists want a greater degree of control over the content they generate for their users, which end-to-end models lack

4. Users are using generative AI in 3D content creation in unexpected ways. For instance:

5. creative directors at Netflix gaming are using their own ComfyUI workflows to retexture characters with fixed topology and UV maps

6. Roblox is using custom pipelines to automatically animate user-created assets with a fixed rig

7. WetaFX is training their models on per-show data for stylization at render time



As I had mentioned in the last update, we strongly believe that end-to-end video generation models won’t inherently solve game engines. We believe the current class of image/video diffusion models will become “neural shaders” that will be part of a rendering pipeline conditioned on scenes with high-quality geometry, materials, animation, lighting, physics. 



Solving for the latter (primitives) is key to unlocking value for the former (shading, stylization).



**To demonstrate immediate value, we have decided to narrow our focus on a specific part of the pipeline which is crucial for narratives and storytelling - automating character creation.**



We are making a differentiated bet and leveraging generative AI in a novel way that we haven't seen in existing products. We are currently iterating on the initial version with users and will share specific details as soon as its ready for launch.
