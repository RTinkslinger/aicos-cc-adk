## MIS Update — November 2025, December 2025, January 2026

### Revenue Summary (in thousands)

Gross Sales: Nov 519K > Dec 1,223K > Jan 2,437K (4.7x growth in 3 months)
Net Sales: Nov 409K > Dec 1,086K > Jan 2,214K

Channel-wise Gross Sales (Jan):
- Shopify/Website: 1,269K (52% of sales) - 3x MoM
- Cred: 459K (19%) - from 7K in Dec
- Flipkart: 366K (15%) - steady growth
- Amazon: 283K (12%) - 73% MoM growth
- Blinkit: 18K (1%) - declining
- Offline: 41K (2%) - collapsed from 324K

### Margin Stack (January 2026)

- Gross Margin: ~50% (up from 45% Dec, 22% Nov)
- Net Margin (after logistics/commissions/storage): ~20%
- Contribution Margin: Negative ~390K (improved from -485K Dec, -633K Nov)
- EBITDA: Deeply negative - marketing + fixed costs exceed gross income
- Total Marketing Spend: 1,397K vs Net Sales 2,214K = 63% of revenue on marketing

### Detailed Cost Breakdown (January)

- COGS: 1,162K (50% of net sales - assumed)
- Performance Marketing (Shopify/Meta): 938K - largest single line item
- Outward Logistics: 588K (Shopify 284K, Cred 128K, Amazon 87K, FK 86K)
- Channel Marketing: 252K (FK 121K, Amazon 91K, Cred 40K)
- Influencer Marketing: 190K
- Salaries: 357K
- Other Misc: 198K
- Rent: 75K
- Professional Fees: 47K

### Key Insights

1. Revenue scaling fast (4.7x in 3 months) but almost entirely marketing-funded. Shopify went from 33K (Nov) to 1,269K (Jan) - impressive, but Meta ad spend also went from 96K to 938K. ROAS is ~1.35x - below breakeven when you factor in COGS + logistics.

2. Cred channel explosion (7K to 459K) needs scrutiny. Logistics cost on Cred is 28% of sales (128K on 459K). Could be a sale/promotion-driven spike rather than organic demand.

3. Gross margin improving (22% to 45% to 50%) is a genuine positive. Suggests better pricing discipline or product mix shift. But COGS in Jan is listed as 50% of (Net Sales - Cancellations) - this might be an estimate, not actuals.

4. Offline channel collapsed (324K to 41K). Thapar channel went to zero. This was ~26% of Dec sales. If intentional (focusing on D2C), fine. If not, red flag.

5. Blinkit declining consistently (55K to 35K to 18K) despite being a quick commerce channel. May not be worth the operational complexity at this scale.

6. Brand building spend went to zero in January. All marketing budget shifted to performance. Short-term optimization that could hurt long-term brand equity and organic discovery.

7. Unit economics are upside down. Every Re 1 of net sales costs ~0.50 in COGS, 0.27 in logistics, and 0.63 in marketing = 1.40 total cost per Re 1 revenue. Path to profitability requires significantly better ROAS or organic demand generation.

### Key Questions

1. What is the Shopify ROAS trend by week in January? Team mentions account maturity improving in late Jan - need weekly cohort data to validate.

2. What drove the Cred explosion? Was it a specific promotion/sale event, or is this repeatable organic demand? What does Feb Cred look like?

3. Is the Jan COGS number actual or estimated? The formula shows 50% of (Net Sales - Cancellations) which looks like a placeholder. Actual COGS matters for real margin analysis.

4. Why did offline collapse? Was Thapar a one-time order in Nov (43K) or a recurring channel? What is the offline distribution strategy going forward?

5. What is the repeat purchase rate? With 63% of revenue going to marketing, customer retention is the only path to positive unit economics. What % of Jan orders are repeat customers?

6. What is the CAC and LTV? Even rough estimates would help frame whether the marketing spend is an investment or just buying revenue.

7. Flipkart marketing cost jumped 2.9x (42K to 121K) due to Google partnership ads experiment. What was the incremental revenue? Is it being continued in Feb?

8. Shopify logistics cost formula shows 86831-2500+200000. What is the 200K - a prepayment, damage, or one-time setup cost?
