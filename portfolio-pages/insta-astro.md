### Pricing per Minute

|  | Insta Astro | AstroTalk | Ask My Guru |

| Model | Human supply, transaction-led | Human supply (+ AI), transaction-led | AI supply, subscription + transaction |

| Per-minute rate | ~₹13/min (stable, Nov '22)[1] → ₹20/min minimum (Feb '26 app review)[1] | ₹22/min avg declining from ₹31 (Nov '22)[1]; AI astro at ₹5/min trial then ₹25/min[2] | ~₹5–8/min (per chat)[2] or unlimited via subscription (₹49/week, ₹149/month)[3] |

**Key takeaway:** AstroTalk commands the highest per-minute pricing (human astrologers ₹20–100/min). Insta Astro sits in the middle at ₹13–20/min. AMG is cheapest at ₹5–8/min (AI-only), and their subscription model effectively lowers the effective per-minute rate further.

Key Observations

1. **WTP gap:** Willingness to pay for AI Astro (AMG) is **significantly lower** than human astro — M0 GMV of ~₹100 vs ₹500–550 for AT/Insta Astro[[2]](https://www.notion.so/Ask-My-Guru-2e029bccb6fc80618f45f52faeac52c4)

2. **CAC advantage for AMG:** Much lower CAC (₹170–285) vs AT (₹400–544) and Insta Astro (₹650–853), but the revenue per user is also proportionally lower

3. **AMG's unit economics are still deeply negative** at CM2 level due to high marketing spend relative to GMV and high AI inference costs (54% of GMV), though CM1 recently turned positive[[3]](https://www.notion.so/AskMyGuru-1ce29bccb6fc80fc9e67e2329c5c9fd4)

4. **Insta Astro and AstroTalk** have broadly similar LTV/CAC profiles (~1.5–1.6x at 1Y) despite Insta Astro's higher CACs, because its user retention cohorts are slightly better[[1]](https://www.notion.so/Insta-Astro-10429bccb6fc80dfa607f705ae7c2c4d)
