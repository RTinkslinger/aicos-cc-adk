Similar to the pre-seed round, where we controlled out exposure and right sized the investment to a community cheque only (while Revant was nudging us to do a bigger one), in the follow-on too, we are limiting our exposure.

Post internal discussion with VV and RA we decided to still get to 2.5-3% ownership zone with an SPR, given the accelerated traction and product love.

About the Company

- Kilrr is a new-age spice and marinade brand built around clean-label, ready-to-use masalas for proteins.

- Kilrr offers marinades that are Indian in flavor, dry (vs. pouch-based gravies), and tailored to modern cooking habits.

- The brand has seen early traction via D2C and is now launching on Quick Commerce (Blinkit, Zepto) in NCR.

- As of Dec ’24 they have achieved 40 lacs per month run rate

Founder

- **Hitesh Bhagia – Founder & CEO**

- Ex-AVP Brand @ Byju’s, Head of Brand Studio @ Indian Express, co-founder @ Homeveda (Blume-backed wellness content network).

Recent Traction** (as of Dec 2024):**

- Grew in net revenue from 16 lacs per month to 40 in in a short span of 3 months and has high NPS and brand love

- Gross Margins: ~62%, CM1: ~52%, CM2: 14%, targeting EBITDA breakeven in in Q1 25

- AOV ~INR 450, 7.3K+ orders in Nov; repeat customers averaging 9.2 units/order

Investment Considerations

- Strong consumer insight and brand-first orientation – Kilrr is resonating with early customers, especially meat-focused homes

- Fast growth with improving margins – CM2 already positive, with clear levers for scale via Qcom

- Asset-light business with scalable SKU mix and good supply chain unit economics

Investment Risks

- Founder shows resistance to external feedback, prefers operating independently — may slow strategic evolution

- Still unclear if this is a 500 Cr brand opportunity without category expansion into RTE or broader Indian staples

- Brand is still early in lifecycle — high dependence on founder’s learning curve and compounding trajectory
