## Update Shared on 2023-04-27 

Dear All,

In March 2024, we landed one of the most well known beverage brands (Bira 91) along with fast growing organizations like Smart Joules & Rooter. We are now seeing a predictable movement from our pipeline into signed customers. With this, we are at $339k ARR. One more significant shift which has started happening from March is our efforts of focusing on cash flow and better Accounts Receivables Management has started to yield results with us receiving pending payments and also being able to negotiate better terms during contracting (upfront payment at the start of the quarter).

**Sales & GTM**

1. Good word of mouth is ensuring a steady stream of inbound leads. However, we need to do much better in doubling down on outbound motion. This is especially required in the North American market since we are trying to ensure more customers from the USA.

2. We need to focus more on the North American market which is being diluted by India demos and the need for me to be a part of existing CS implementations in some bigger customers like Allen.

**Product**

1. The engineering team continued to work at a breakneck speed even in March to ensure our Performance Management modules are at par with competing products in the USA and India. However, this also means that a few features were released without adequate testing which led to problems and more CS involvement. We have on boarded a new QA person to ensure these sorts of things can be prevented going ahead.

2. Other focus areas continue to remain OKRs, 1:1s, dashboards, and platform analytics.

3. Product is still a 1+1 people team (Daksha moved from QA to a Product role; while Atri looks at Product overall).

**Customer Success**

1. A large chunk of March continued to be taken up by Allen since they are a 10,000 person company. We are now hiring for a new CS person who would be able to balance out the customer split with the influx of new customers like DTDL, Bira 91, Cognition, etc.

2. We have not been able to focus on G2 Reviews and Case Studies. While I have spoken to mostly all customers (and they are all willing to do it), it's been a bandwidth issue. We will start this in April.

3. CS continues to be a 3 person team (Shreyas, Prerona, Anurag) with 1 floating resource (Adi who is a business analyst).

**People**

1. The team size (FTEs) remains constant with no new addition.

2. The team morale, especially engineering and CS, did take a bit of a hit because of the work pressure and the long hours. However, Anshul (our engineering head), Atri, and I are constantly in touch with people so that we can prevent any unnecessary attrition.

3. We held our All Hands where we explained Klaar's vision to the entire team, especially the newer set of people. We also utilized this opportunity to thank the teams for their dedication and support.
