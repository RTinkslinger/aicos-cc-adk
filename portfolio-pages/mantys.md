## Investor update shared with Divyanshi 2024-09-02T14:01:00.000+05:30 



Hi All,

We spent the last couple of months diving deeper into the three directions we were considering. To recap, these directions were:

- Building an end-to-end revenue platform for SaaS and consumption-based companies (Customer Master Data, Metric Builder, and Prediction)

- Developing a point revenue cycle management (RCM) solution for healthcare companies

- Focusing on a customer master data platform using AI for SaaS, consumption-based, and financial services companies

Based on our research and customer research, we are inclining towards the 2nd direction to build an automated revenue cycle management tool for independent physician practices using AI agents. There are a few factors which led to this:

- **The entire RCM value chain is highly manual. Traditionally, it was done by RCM services firms(charging $5-7 Mn a year), big RCM product companies serving big hospital chains or more RPA or rule based RCM companies. With the advent of AI, a lot more automation has become possible like:**

- 

- Instead of manually updating payer-specific rules whenever they change, AI agents can read payer websites and automatically update these rules.

- AI agents can replace manual bots for making calls, while intelligently summarizing the results.

- AI can now accurately read complex paper documents, such as prescriptions, which was previously challenging.

- The perceived value is higher with a clear impact on the healthcare firm’s cash flow, DSO or margins which makes the product relatively easier to sell. **RCM in healthcare is a multi-billion dollar industry. While US($10-20 Bn) is the largest market, GCC(~$200 Mn) is also a decent market with similar characteristics to the US market.**

- We have a few prospects in the Dubai market(ARR of ~$175k) to begin with which gives us enough time to build the product alongside customers.

- The core vision remains the same: managing customer revenue end-to-end and providing real-time visibility to prevent revenue leakage, increase Accounts Receivables collection, and reduce DSO, just focussed on healthcare. For SaaS and tech companies, we felt the urgency of need is not high which led to extremely long sales and implementation cycles and low ACVs

**Metrics:**  Cash: ~$470k  Burn: $22k Runway: 21 months

**Highlights:**

- After exploring the healthcare RCM market in the GCC, we identified a clear gap in product need and limited competition. Hospitals and independent clinic chains face high rejection rates (8-10%) and lengthy turnaround times for patients, affecting their overall experience.

- We are also restructuring the team and have several promising leads that we expect to close by the end of the month.

## Update 2025-05-03T18:28:00.000+05:30 

Hi All,

Over the past two months, we focused on launching and refining the eligibility verification product. We successfully completed full implementations across three chains and partial rollouts at two (first two clinics). We will spend the next 2-3 weeks completing the rollouts in the remaining ones.

1. **Product and Implementation**

Here are some key learnings from the current implementations:

1. **Accuracy**

- Initially, accuracy was a major challenge due to new edge cases and the different types of customers. We started with a GP telehealth provider where cases were relatively simple, and then moved to physical multi-specialty clinic chains, which introduced more complexity. A further nuance was the Dubai vs. Northern Emirates difference: customers operating in the Northern Emirates faced different payer sites and slight variations in insurance details. Also a lot of the latest AI models are not available in the UAE and thus, we have to work with limited sets due to Dubai residency issues.

- Though we have been able to achieve significant accuracy in clinic chains, building a robust evaluation framework and preparing for changes when scaling to hospitals still remain as problems to solve.

2. **Speed:**

- While we initially underestimated it, speed became critical for adoption, especially for front-office teams during peak hours. Patient Relationship Executives(PREs) are used to doing things a certain way and cutting corners to manage the crowd(which can be pretty wild), leading to errors. We thus had to figure out a way to increase speed without compromising on accuracy. We are still looking for more ways to optimise it.

- This is not the issue in all types of establishments. For example, in hospitals where appointments are common, the check can be done after midnight and this thus, doesn’t become a challenge. It becomes a problem in clinics where walk-ins are high.

3. **Customisation for HIS integration:**

- The UAE market has 10–12 primary HIS systems. We have integrated with four so far using browser automation (APIs are generally not available). Each HIS has its own nuances, making backfilling data challenging.

- Our hypothesis is that once we cover the primary systems, future customer implementations will be much faster.

- We are also exploring ways to abstract some HIS dependencies to make the product more flexible and balancing between depth and breadth of integrations.

4. **Infra: **Given the sensitivity of patient data and reliance on clinic credentials to access insurance portals, some customers are uncomfortable with cloud-only storage. For one chain, we implemented credential storage on their own server, a process that required additional setup and security configurations. For larger hospital customers, we anticipate they may require us to host both services and databases fully within their infrastructure. For some chains, their HIS are desktop versions and thus, we need to take a call on whether to work with these customers.

**Progress on Prior Authorization:**

We also released the first version of our prior authorization product. It is still at a very early stage. We aim to make significant progress this month, once eligibility implementations stabilize.
