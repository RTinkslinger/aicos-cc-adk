**Detailed notes**

- Spoke to **Siddhant from Deserve**, who leads **outbound growth**.

- They’ve been using **RevSpot since January**.

- Main use case: **buying data** for the **outbound team** to improve call accuracy.

- They spend **₹6–7 lakhs per quarter**, buying around **30,000 leads**.

- Chose RevSpot because of its **higher accuracy** than others Siddhant tried.

- RevSpot has **~90% accuracy** on phone numbers.

- Best alternative Siddhant tried had **~73% accuracy**.

- The buying journey took **3–4 months**. Tried multiple vendors:

- **Lusha, Apollo, ZoomInfo**, and smaller category-specific data sellers (e.g., HNIs, professionals).

- Cost comparison:

- Lusha: **₹60/lead**, expensive, not India-focused, ~75% accuracy.

- Bulk vendors: **₹1/lead**, but data is **noisy, unclean, inaccurate**, wastes BD time.

- RevSpot filled the **middle ground** — affordable with **high accuracy**.

- RevSpot pitched 3 offerings:

1. **Pure data purchase** (what Deserve uses today).

2. **Enrichment** of CRM leads, website visitors, etc.

3. **Automated outbound** via **AI voice agent**.

- Deserve is **interested in CRM enrichment**, not in voice agents for selling.

- Current **human sales conversion ~3%**; AI bots not viable for conversion.

- Voice agents may work for **FAQs, reminders**, etc.

- The **enrichment use case** is valuable to them:

- They want to enrich leads (who downloaded the app) with info like **location, job, assets**.

**Highlights (July + mid-August):**

- **Revenue growth:** July closed at ₹63.6 L MRR, up +23% MoM vs June (₹51.7 L). August MRR till Aug 14 is already at ₹73.3 L MRR, continuing our ~25% MoM growth run-rate since January and carrying us across the $1mn ARR mark.

- **Cash collections & cash flow:** July collections were ₹64.1 L, our highest month yet, taking total cash collected to ₹2.4 Cr. The first ₹1 Cr took ~9 months (Sep ’24–May ’25), while the next ₹1 Cr came in just 3 months. **Importantly, on an absolute cash-in vs. cash-out basis, July was also operating cash flow positive month by 2L.**

- **Margins:** Gross margin in July was 62.1% (₹39.5 L), up from 56.5% in June. Both core layers - data and activation - are becoming more efficient. We have also now cracked meta as an additional channel to outbound calling for lead generation.

- **Real estate expansion:** Revenue expansion in real estate delivered tremendous growth, confirming Revspot not only solves a real pain but delivers immense value. Aparna grew 4x in July to ₹11.75 L, Sumadhura expanded 50% in August to ₹7.5 L, Provident scaled 6x from June to July to ₹4.0 L, and White Lotus stepped up to ₹5.2 L in August.

**Lowlights :**

1. **Customer churn:** We added 6 new customers in July, but our total customer count stayed flat at **41**, as we also lost 6. A few exits were non-regrettable (untenable use cases, wrong target markets). But two were regrettable:

- Emversity - They ran AI calling for their own leads; while we matched throughput, we could not beat the cost efficiency of a low-cost human team in India.

- Brigade Group - This was an account management miss on our side. Was not managed properly.

2. That said, we’ve repeatedly shown we can win customers back (Leap, Masai, Decorpot being recent examples), and we are confident about re-engaging both these logos.

3. **Invoicing & receivables:** We’ve not followed proper invoicing rigor; current receivables (~₹1.4 Cr) are overstated due to random invoices that need cancellations/credit notes. Will clean this up in a week and start showing receivables correctly in MIS as Shiv suggested.

We’re excited about the pace and quality of growth we’re compounding - ARR, cash flow discipline, and customer value are all moving in the right direction. The next phase is about keeping this discipline while scaling faster. Always grateful for your support and guidance as we build.





## MIS Update - August

By mid-August we had already stepped up from July’s **INR 63.6 L MRR** to ~**INR 73 L**. We closed August at **INR 77 L MRR**, driven by both new customer additions and anchor accounts expanding. Even at this scale, we are averaging **>20% MoM growth run-rate since January**.

**Collections:** July was **INR 64.1 L**, August came in at **INR 62.1 L**. Last month, I had mentioned lack of diligence in invoicing and finance processes as one of the lowlights. This is now getting fixed, with **collections catching up to invoiced revenue**.

**Margins:** Gross margin dropped from ~62% to ~46%. This was expected. August is when we opened performance marketing as a channel, and our PM costs were 4x July. It **exponentially expands our TAM**, but just like our anchor channel, AI Calling, it requires time to unlock efficiency while in the experimental phase. We are already operating more efficiently than 99% of agencies and in-house teams, and will unlock further efficiency as we scale.

The same thing happened with **AI Calling** - costs were high and margins compressed early, but over the last three months costs have stayed flat while revenue scaled significantly. We expect the same trajectory in performance marketing over the coming months.

Importantly, although gross margins contracted, **EBITDA margin contraction was negligible at just 0.6%**, **thanks to higher revenue and flat opex.**

We are excited for September - **revenue continues to compound, collections are disciplined, and we’ve opened a new growth engine**. Meanwhile, let me know if you have any questions regarding the MIS.
