**Learnings**

2. Collections can’t be purely AI-driven so we have to setup/partner-with a call-center operations team to resolve nuanced cases.

3. Current collection cycles are so short and pressure driven (both on collection agents and as a result on defaulters) that they don’t give enough opportunity for genuine refinancing and patient follow ups.



**Key-focus for next month**

1. We want to compete to rise up from our current ranking in the bottom 20% of collection agency performance benchmark.

2. Partnering/setting-up a call-center to solve harder cases in AI debt-collection.

3. Hire an ML engineer to start building a risk-engine over collection-interaction data



**Asks**

2. We want to hire an engineer who has worked on building a credit-engine, any intros here would be helpful



Outside of this, we are now spending time becoming call-center agents ourselves and actively talking to borrowers to understand their behavior. We aim to use insights like these, combined with our tech-first approach to become the most efficient manager of bad-debt for banks.

This meeting was a strategic discussion about the founders' journey in collections and potential pivot options in the financial services space. The team sought advice from investors and advisors on their path forward.

**Company Journey**

- February: Switched from collections analytics software to doing collections directly

- April: Implemented voice AI for collections

- Days 0-5: AI performed well for early-stage collections

- Days 6-11: Humans outperformed AI (could do reference calling, apply pressure)

- DPD 1000+ (Non-Performing Assets): Conversations too complex for AI

**Key Learnings**

- Voice AI alone is insufficient for effective collections

- Collection agencies require a full-stack approach:

- Call centers (200-400 people)

- On-ground field teams (3000+ people)

- Traditional "Lala collection agencies" still outperform them

- Collection is a waterfall process - taking only end-stage cases limits effectiveness

**Strategic Framework**

- OPEX Reduction vs. Risk First approach:

- OPEX Reduction: Building AI agents to reduce operational costs for financial institutions

- Risk First: Developing capabilities to understand and price credit risk

**Potential Paths Forward**

- Collections path:

- Build full-stack capabilities including on-ground teams

- Eventually aim to become an Asset Reconstruction Company (ARC)

- Lending path:

- Build sourcing capabilities

- Move to Direct Lending Gateway (DLG)

- Eventually obtain NBFC license

- Technology enabler path:

- Create a "wedge" with voice AI in specific parts of the credit lifecycle

- Become a system of record/action for financial institutions

- Focus on being "Salesforce for BFSI"

**Advisor Recommendations**

- Leverage the team's technical strength to focus on OPEX reduction through AI

- Start with specific pain points where voice AI can add immediate value

- Consider monitoring at-risk borrowers (pre-DPD) like Resolve does

- Map the human elements in financial workflows that can be automated

- Connect with other fintech founders to gain industry perspective

## Discussion 2025-11-30T15:14:00.000+05:30 

See, even without doing a bunch of research 2 things are clear - the more contextually-aware voice-agents get, the more you can nudge a borrower towards repayment. Just call works best for early-stage reminders and we've seen even Navana do it at this scale, but the real legwork is in building workflows around channels like Call/WA/Email to really provide end-to-end recovery.

Players like Exotel, Smallest, Greylabs etc. provide this as a tech-service which is easy to deploy in early-stage recovery but hard to defend in pricing, strategy-control, ROI justification and scary thin moat over underlying TTS model. So that's why we like to own the stack, then build more services for the same borrower base.

Not at the moment, the amount of collections happening digitally has been on the increase + there's interesting experiences to create for a borrower's digital debt-resolution journey. Especially with a more contextually aware AI. So digital will be our focus on our collxn buckets

Blocking of phones, over-calling, figuring out contactability via field app, will solve debt resolution and delinquencies at a very surface level.

Debt-recovery itself is the surface problem. The deeper problem here is how people perceive and use credit. I'll still go back to saying that fixing credit awareness for delinquents, will help transition so many new-to-credit borrowers (across many types of loans) to be more mature-credit-users, and that's what Riverline focuses on addressing.

We're not too focused on becoming super efficient right now as much as we are about getting behind the mindset of each borrower with contextually aware AI agents and delicately nudging them towards the right approach to take towards credit.

It's a hard design problem and will take way longer to solve, but once we have done that we will have changed something fundamental in how loans are looked upon and treated and we get a shot at becoming a bank.
