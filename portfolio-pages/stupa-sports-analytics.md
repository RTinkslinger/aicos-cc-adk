### Didn’t work our way:

- Experimentation with Smart Court diverted focus from the AI roadmap, impacting the overall strategy and weakening the efforts of the technology team.

*Steps to resolve: We are shifting our focus exclusively to B2B clients for now only relevant to our SaaS and AI solutions. This strategic decision allows us to recruit the right talent to develop our product, ensuring we deliver the highest quality solution.*

- We received considerable inbound interest from sports like soccer, golf, basketball, and Volleyball, which slightly shifted our focus away from racket sports and ultimately limited the time we could allocate to closing deals in that area.

*Steps to resolve: We are core focused on racket and for anything strategic in nature other than non-racket, we will discuss with Board on case by case basis*

- We experienced some challenges in our hiring process, which led to slowed growth in last quarter and the next, as founders had to allocate their time to fix issues and get back on track.

*Steps to resolve: To address hiring challenges and support growth, we will refine our strategy to prioritize key roles, engage recruitment experts, and implement a structured interview process. Additionally, promoting internal mobility can quickly fill gaps without lengthy external hiring*

- By concentrating on high-value deals, we are working with longer conversion cycles, which naturally leads to slower overall conversion rates.

*Steps to resolve: To resolve the issue of longer conversion cycles with high-value deals, we plan to implement a dual sales approach by balancing large, long-term deals with smaller, quicker wins. We’ll streamline our lead qualification process, break down large deals into actionable milestones, and offer incentives for quicker decision-making to help accelerate conversions without compromising deal quality.*
