We recently met a peer comp in the space – Pingolearn (Titan backed, raised $270K in ’21). Key takeaways below:

AI-led model is more scalable than 1:1 live learning

Bambinos model is limited on qualified supply and is a linearly scaling model due to live learning
AI-led model is scalable and has data as a moat (incrementally improved conversations with each new audio sample added to the database)
However, building own model is not a scalable solution (Pingolearn founders seemed intent on doing that)

Thesis is immediate improvement in spoken English (qualitative improvement)

Unlike Duolingo’s vocabulary-learning model (requires users to cross the 0->1 language learning journey), this play relies on pre-existing functional English speaking abilities (skill gap much smaller)
Unlike live learning (typically structured, curriculum based), AI-led models are gamified and designed for immediate improvement in spoken English (basic syntax correction for social situations – how to order a coffee, asking teacher for help etc.)
Live P2P conversations facilitated on the platform to build retention and have real-world practice

Kids seems to be the key market to go after

High spend propensity among parents who want to improve their child’s conversational abilities
Product can become a core part of the child’s learning process à critical part of after-school learning and does not compete with wallet-share/time-share spent on tuitions

**Market is crowded; a thoughtful GTM is key**

The market is crowded with global comps on both the speech-first approach (Cambly, Speak, Elsa) and the vocab-first approach (Duolingo, Babbel).
There are multiple Indian comps also operating in the space - traditional coaching institutes, adult-first plays (Josh Talks, Hello English, Utter) and kids-first plays (Supernova, Pingolearn)
The adult-first plays are typically a learning + hiring play; kid-first plays are over-indexed toward 1:1 or small-format live classes
Supernova has adopted a state-first approach to building (targeting only Tamil Nadu users to find PMF before expanding to other states)
Pingolearn has geo-agnostic, with a <15% proportion of users coming from the US and Europe

**Edge against incumbents not clear**

Why will Cambly, Duolingo not do this anyway – since distribution is a key moat in the business, the best-case outcome for a new comp today seems muted (can be an acquisitive outcome)
Going highly vernacular seems to be an edge against incumbents (Supernova has a razor-focus on Tamil Nadu as a geo; going through local content, promotions and partnerships before expanding to other Southern states).

Overall, all these players are sub-scale, and outside of the global incumbents, no one has managed to pick up significant capital or scale. Among the teams we’ve seen so far, Supernova stands out because of Rishi (views in trail). We will carry out a couple refs on him to calibrate and decide next steps.
