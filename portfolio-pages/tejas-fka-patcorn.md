## Monthly Update for Nov ‘24

**Business Overview**

The past 2 quarters for Patcorn have been about solving/building the supply arm (getting products from factories to our dark stores). Initially for the first 8 months (Sept 23-April 24), Patcorn was mostly about just solving demand -onboarding contractors with a pre-mature product & only 2 SKUs. But we realized/learned that product depth, service/convenience layer, collections/credit stack, and supply/production side need to be solved. While we have solved some but still have lots to build & solve before we go about aggressively onboarding new contractors as we wish to maintain CCRL >2x with 2x the AOV on our next cohort of contractors.

We don't plan to onboard any new contractors at least until January 25. From Feb 25 onwards we plan to go & onboard 100 new contractors in <4 months.

- For example in convenience bit, our delivery times previously were ~5 hours which was just tarnishing our contractor relationship, which led us to commit to <2 hours delivery & currently our average delivery time in the last 2 months has been ~`1 hour 50 mins. (basics were solved like ample supply of electric autos for 20 bags delivery, 1/2 ton capacity auto, 1-ton tata-ace, tracking the vendor's proximity to warehouse - 3rd fleet management 101)

- Initially, our advance, same day & next day payments as a % of topline was only 30% which has now increased to 60%.

- SKUs we added & current toll stands at 8 SKUs, expected to reach 12 by Feb 25 covering >95% of the demand in the TA category.

- On-site service for complaints, and knowledge transfer on specific applications. We have built this & currently, we have one dedicated person for it.

The key thing we need to solve is production & adding quality contract manufacturers, scaling systems & organizational bandwidth.

*We are dead committed to building a full-stack scalable stack for construction chemicals products.*

**Challenges & Mitigation**

Beyond our control (Oct-Nov are the slowest month for building material companies as the market as the construction itself reduces to half):-

1. **Diwali Month:** At the time of Diwali most of the contractors, & laborers migrate to their hometown (Bihar & up mostly). This is the only time in the year they take a long vacation. With the lack of labor, the work on the site reduces, it is the slowest calendar month for construction materials companies.

2. **Marriage season: **As most of the laborers/misters are Hindu, their marriage season is in the month of nov-dec (Usually after Diwali). So a long vacation impacts construction activity. It takes at least 2 months (Nov mostly & Dec a bit) to get the demand in place.

In our control, to be solved (we could only fulfill ~60% of the demand we had)

1. **Supply constraint in Sept & Oct:** Logistics of labor optimizations in plants & optimizing for the short supply of materials was tedious- consumed tonnes of time for the founding team. It’s important to hedge this & not be reliant on only 1 contract manufacturer for each SKU *(we need at least 2 manufacturers for each SKU, we are looking for more contract manufacturers to onboard by March 25.)*

2. **Fundraising consumption - **~80% of Sailesh’s time went into fund-raising, Saijal's 50% & Nikhil’s 20%+ calendar was also disturbed during the Sept-Nov period. *Post round is concluded, 90%+ time of founders is expected to go towards building*

3. **Product launch delayed (***Type 4 Tile Adhesive, Grout Epoxy, & Power Tools*) - Infusion of capex & working capital required, currently in beta will become aggressive & launch full-scale post the raise.

4. **Resource Constraints:** The team is running maxed out, juggling across mostly repetitive ops stuff.* Hiring across multiple verticals must be done urgently for optimal high-value utilization, especially the founding team’s time.*

**Site Mistry & Labour Party**

Mistry and labour are a critical part of decision-making on product usage. Contractors often rely on their feedback to judge whether a product is good or not.

In addition to contractors, Mistry and labour also have direct interaction with site owners and supervisors who are regularly present at the site. Feedback, concerns, and complaints about the product are frequently routed through them.

For this reason, it is essential to ensure that Mistry and labour are satisfied with the product and its application. Their acceptance helps reduce on-site resistance and address issues early, even before they reach the owner or contractors.

**Crafting a mini playbook for managing ministry relationships is essential** (which we didn't focus on explicitly initially, & fake complaints suddenly increased, which has been mitigated to a great extent now). It is a 3-step process:-

1) Building a key Mistry accounts database of the existing 100 contractors.

2) Cultivating relationships (for e.g., on avg each contractor has 1-6 such key Mistry in a total team of 10-100 Mistry & labourers; 10-20% of these Mistrys go on to become contractors in the future)

3) Building a streamlined system for Mistry delight (~2% of revenues now, around 1% at scale).

This is a key component for NOR, contractors CCRL & complaints resolution (as mostly complaints are worksmanship problems or competitors' misrtry incentives, so fake complaints !)

7 such events with a cumulative spend of 1.5L have been conducted in the last 1.5 months. All were in our existing running sites.
