## Update from founders 2026-01-14 



- We’ve been spending time exploring **insurtech**, driven by conversations with operators and funds who believe the space is still underpenetrated due to legacy systems. Our working view is that real progress likely requires an end-to-end AI-native OS. While larger carriers have moved to cloud via Duck Creek or Guidewire, we’re increasingly focused on **mid-sized P&C operators** as potential design partners.

- We’re narrowing in on **F&I products like auto warranties**, starting with **revenue-critical workflows** such as submissions, data ingestion, underwriting logic, pricing, and quoting. The goal is to help carriers quote faster (withCoverage covers the opposite side) and increase throughput. Claims ingestion and GenAI-driven adjudication are interesting, but feel like a harder place to start. We also discussed how this differs from procurement-style approaches like WithCoverage, which focus on shopping rates, versus helping carriers produce quotes faster. Longer term, we’ve even talked through whether building an insurance company could be the cleanest expression of this.

- Separately, Federico has been building a **proprietary DSL**, a config layer to declaratively model common workflows -- for insurance, this would be intake, rules, pricing, and compliance, allowing us to move quickly without heavy per-customer customization. The DSL allowed us to basically have an enterprise app builder and we have weighed if there is a GTM for us to sell this alone (but lean to selling to a vertical and just have a faster implementation cycle).
