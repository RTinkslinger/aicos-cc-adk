## Vegas Trade Show

---

![](https://news.unifize.com/hs-fs/hubfs/unnamed%20(61)-1.png?width=500&upscale=true&name=unnamed%20(61)-1.png)

---

Apart from hitting up the Strip, Jamie and Ben did some productive work at a trade show of nutritional supplement manufacturers and raw material suppliers called Supply Side West.

We’d been encouraged to visit by one of our die-hard customers, Biovation.

Jamie and Ben got to spend a couple of days talking through potential AI-based use cases with the VP Ops of Biovation and then testing out their hypotheses in real-time at the trade show.

We spoke to 6 contract manufacturers, who all said that quoting for custom-made nutritional supplements is a collaborative nightmare currently done on Excel, with an average cycle time of 14 days, resulting in them losing orders.

5 out of 6 of the respondents said it was their biggest sales problem and 1 said they would pay between $30k and $50k to solve it.

All of them want a tool that can:

1. Query into their existing raw material data using AI

2. Help them collaborate with vendors for pricing

3. Measure and reduce quote cycle times

The quote process is an interesting use case as so many quality problems ultimately stem from the original specs obtained during quoting/RFP.

We’re exploring this further in November.

Better late than never
Well, we hit our $1m ARR goal for the 2024 financial year. This is a big milestone for us, so we need to take a moment to self-congratulate. While it took us 4 years, 10 months from our first sale, we can comfort ourselves that 10% of SaaS startups ever even reach this point.

Efficiency or austerity?
We still have $850k on our balance sheet, meaning we’ve only spent $650k to reach the $1m milestone. The average SaaS startup in the US spends $3m to reach this point, which means we’re about 4.6x more capital efficient.

We’re proud of this achievement, but perhaps we’ve gone a little too far :-)



Time to accelerate
For the first time in our history, we’ve begun to feel:
•	We know how to scale PPC and outbound
•	We have a decent idea of the range of our customer acquisition cost (CAC) across channels, and an understanding of our LTV

There are still challenges ahead on product, platform, and especially implementation, but we’re continuing to make significant progress here. More money to scale is now needed in these areas too.



So, 2026 will be about acceleration. We’re extremely excited to see how this unfolds.
