# Ananant Systems – India’s Home-grown 5G/6G Semiconductor Playbook (2024-26)

## Executive Summary
Ananant Systems is a Bengaluru and Noida-based fabless semiconductor startup focused on developing indigenous 5G and 6G wireless infrastructure chips [1] [2]. Founded by a former Qualcomm chief architect, the company leverages open-source RISC-V architecture to deliver digital signal processors (DSPs) at a fraction of the cost of incumbent solutions [2]. While the company has achieved significant visibility through government partnerships and global exhibitions, critical information gaps remain regarding its funding totals and commercial revenue metrics.

## 1. Company Snapshot & Value Proposition
Ananant Systems develops semiconductor products, intellectual property (IP), software, and systems for advanced 5G and 6G connectivity [3] [4]. Driven by India's "Atmanirbhar" (self-reliance) philosophy, the company ensures all IP is owned within India to provide sovereign, secure infrastructure [3]. 

Its core technological differentiator is the use of the RISC-V Instruction Set Architecture (ISA) for its DSP chips, which allows for efficient co-processing for wireless signal processing and AI inference [2]. By utilizing this open-source architecture, Ananant claims its solutions cost one-third of current wireless alternatives, positioning it competitively against Chinese vendors like Huawei [3] [5].

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership DNA
The company was founded in 2023 by Dr. Chitranjan (Chitu) Singh, who serves as CEO and Chief Architect [3] [1]. 

* **Dr. Chitranjan Singh:** Returned to India after 22 years in the US [6]. At Qualcomm, he served as Principal Architect and Chief Designer, delivering six generations of LTE/5G modems that shipped over 3 billion units [3] [7]. He holds a B.Tech from IIT Kanpur and a PhD in electronics and wireless communication from the University of Texas, Dallas [3].
* **Core Team:** The broader engineering team brings over 20 years of experience from companies like Maxlinear, Marvell, Nokia, Ericsson, and Mavenir, with educational backgrounds from top-tier Indian institutes (IITs and IISc) [3] [7]. Deepika Dubey is noted as the HR Leader [8].

## 4. Market Position & Competitive Landscape
Ananant operates within India's rapidly expanding semiconductor ecosystem, supported by government initiatives like the DIR-V (Digital India RISC-V) program [2]. 

| Competitor Profile | Core Focus | Market Position |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ananant Systems** | RISC-V DSPs, 5G/6G infrastructure | Emerging Indian player; 100% local IP; low-cost focus [3] [2] |
| **Qualcomm / MediaTek** | Proprietary ARM-based modems | Global incumbents; premium pricing; closed architecture [2] [4] |
| **Leapfrog Semiconductor** | Fabless RF and mixed-signal | US-based with Indian presence; mid-tier competitor [1] [6] |

The company differentiates itself by targeting embedded applications like base stations and small cells where RISC-V software ecosystems can be developed alongside the hardware, bypassing the locked CPU markets dominated by ARM and x86 [2].

## 5. Recent Milestones (2024-2026)
Ananant has maintained an aggressive public relations and partnership roadmap:
* **May 2024:** Joined the India Electronics and Semiconductor Association (IESA) and announced it was working with major local OEMs to develop a 5G chip for state-owned telecom operator BSNL [9] [5].
* **July 2024:** Participated as a panelist at the ETTelecom Open RAN Summit [9].
* **October 2025:** Showcased solutions at the Aspire Pavilion during the India Mobile Congress (IMC25) [1].
* **December 2025:** Participated in a Department of Telecommunications (DoT) Open House for telecom startups [1].
* **March 2026:** Exhibited at Mobile World Congress (MWC) in Barcelona at the Bharat Pavilion, where the booth was visited by India's Minister for Communications, Jyotiraditya M. Scindia [1].

## 6. Business Model, Traction & Information Gaps
Ananant operates a B2B model focused initially on telecom infrastructure. The company's phased roadmap targets base stations in its first year, followed by device-side chips (such as smart meters and 5G handsets) in subsequent years [5]. 

**Traction:** The primary confirmed commercial traction is its collaboration with BSNL vendors to develop a 5G fixed wireless access (FWA) chip [2].
