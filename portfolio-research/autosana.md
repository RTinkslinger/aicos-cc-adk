# Autosana — AI-Agentic QA Platform Disrupting Mobile & Web Testing

## Executive Summary
Autosana is an early-stage, San Francisco-based startup that provides an AI-agentic Quality Assurance (QA) platform for mobile and web applications [1] [2]. By converting natural language instructions into self-healing, end-to-end (E2E) tests, the company aims to eliminate manual QA bottlenecks [1]. Despite the user's focus on Indian startups, Autosana is headquartered in the US [3] [4], though it competes directly with Indian QA giants like BrowserStack and LambdaTest [5] [6]. Having recently raised a $3.2M Seed round in February 2026 [2], Autosana is experiencing rapid early traction, reporting over 100% month-over-month revenue growth since its June 2025 launch [2].

## Company Overview & Value Proposition
Autosana operates as an AI-powered QA platform that automates E2E testing for iOS, Android, and web applications [2] [3]. 

The core value proposition centers on a "no-code" approach: engineering teams can describe test scenarios in plain English, and Autosana's AI agents generate and execute the tests [1] [4]. The platform features "self-healing" capabilities, meaning tests automatically adapt to user interface changes without breaking [7]. It is framework-agnostic, supporting React Native, Flutter, Swift, and Kotlin, and integrates directly into existing CI/CD pipelines [1]. This allows teams to catch bugs before production while drastically reducing the time spent maintaining brittle test scripts [1] [8].

## Founding Team & Leadership
Autosana was founded by Yuvan Sundrani and Jason Steinberg, serial entrepreneurs who have been friends since middle school [2]. 
* **Yuvan Sundrani (Founder):** A lifelong builder who previously co-founded a contact-sharing app in 2020, served as Engineer #1 to CPO at a martech startup (reaching 7-figure ARR), and was Engineer #1 at an AI-therapy startup [1].
* **Jason Steinberg (Founder):** Worked on the founding teams of multiple mobile-first startups and played club hockey at the University of Maryland [1]. 

The company currently operates with a lean team size of 1-10 employees [1] [3].

## Business Model, Pricing & Traction
Autosana operates on a B2B SaaS revenue model. While enterprise pricing is custom-tailored to team size and testing needs [11], third-party directories indicate pricing can start around $500 per month [12]. 

**Key Traction Metrics:**
* **Growth:** The company claims to have grown revenue by over 100% each month on average since launching in June 2025 [2].
* **Scale:** Autosana powers QA for applications serving over 100 million daily active users (DAU) [2].
* **Customer Base:** Early adopters include Y Combinator, Lucra Sports, Zest, Hawala, SellRaze, and Majestyk [7].

## Strategic Recommendations
For organizations looking to modernize their QA pipelines, Autosana presents a high-ROI opportunity to reduce manual testing hours. Indian tech companies and app developers should consider running a pilot program with Autosana to evaluate its natural language test generation against local incumbents like LambdaTest. Investors should monitor Autosana's ability to scale its engineering team post-Seed round and track its penetration into enterprise accounts before a potential Series A.
