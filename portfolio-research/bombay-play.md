# Bombay Play 2024-26 Playbook: Hyper-Social Gaming, Funding Waves & Path to Scale in India

## Executive Summary

* **Funding Surge:** Bombay Play has raised a total of $9.5 million across multiple rounds, culminating in a $7 million Series A led by Kalaari Capital in April 2022 [1] [2]. 
* **Hyper-Social Niche Gains Traction:** The studio has amassed over 40 million players worldwide across its portfolio of casual multiplayer games [2]. 
* **Monetisation Model Shows Stress:** Recent user reviews (2024-2025) highlight growing ad-fatigue and technical glitches, threatening player retention [3] [4].
* **Market Context:** India's gaming market is booming, boasting over 568 million gamers as of 2024, making it the second-largest globally [5].
* **Information Gaps:** The company's current valuation, exact revenue figures, and precise daily active user (DAU) metrics remain undisclosed [6] [1].

## 1. Company Snapshot & Value Proposition

Founded in Bengaluru in 2017/2018, Bombay Play is a mobile game development studio pioneering the "hyper-social" and hybrid casual gaming genres [7] [8] [2]. The company's core value proposition lies in creating games that seamlessly bring friends, family, and strangers together online through real-time multiplayer mechanics and in-game chat features [6] [9]. Their stated mission is to create "unmissable entertainment, anywhere, with billions of smiles" [7].

## 2. Founders & Leadership DNA

Bombay Play is led by veteran gaming entrepreneurs who bring extensive global game-design expertise to the Indian market [9] [2]. 

* **Oliver Jones (Co-Founder & CEO):** A Welsh game designer who previously led game design at Glu Mobile and Zynga [10] [9]. He also co-founded Moonfrog Games, where he helped create *Teen Patti Gold*, one of India's most successful card games [7] [9].
* **Abhas Saroha (Co-Founder & CTO):** An experienced software engineer who previously led engineering teams at Zynga, Moonfrog, and MakeMyTrip.com [7] [9] [11]. 

The co-founders have worked together for nearly a decade, combining creative game design with data-driven development [7] [9]. Justin Waldron, Co-founder of Zynga and Playco, also joined the company's Board of Directors [9].

## 5. Indian Gaming Market Context

Bombay Play operates in a highly lucrative domestic environment. India's online gaming user base has surged, with the country hosting over 568 million gamers as of 2024, making it the second-largest gaming market in the world [5]. The market is characterised by a young demographic (half the population under 25) and a strong preference for mobile-first, socially integrated entertainment [11]. The government has also shown support for the sector; Bombay Play was notably shortlisted for the Karnataka government's Elevate 2018 incubation programme [13].

## 7. Business Model, Monetisation & Traction

Bombay Play operates on a Free-to-Play (F2P) business model, monetising primarily through in-app advertisements and in-app purchases [3] [4]. 
* **Ad Revenue:** Games feature heavy ad integration, including interstitial ads between rounds and rewarded videos for in-game currency [3].
* **In-App Purchases:** Players can buy coin packs, boosters, and "no ads" premium upgrades [3] [4].
* **Traction:** The studio claimed profitability as early as its 2020 seed round [9] and reported growing 3x YoY prior to its Series A [2]. 

## 8. Recent Milestones (2024-2026) & Operational Risks

Between 2024 and early 2026, Bombay Play has focused on scaling its team and maintaining its live-ops. 
* **Hiring & Expansion:** The company actively recruited game developers in 2024 [5]. Glassdoor data from late 2024 indicates an efficient hiring process, averaging 17 days [15].
* **Monetisation Friction:** Recent Google Play reviews (2024-2025) for *Card Party* and *Block Heads* reveal significant player frustration regarding ad frequency. Users report "watching a 20-second ad for a different app, and then have two more ads pop up" and describe the ads as "snervanti" (unnerving/exhausting) [3] [4]. 
* **Technical Stability:** Players have reported occasional app crashes and auto-play glitches in recent updates, which the developer team is actively addressing through maintenance patches [3] [4].

* **Active Users:** While lifetime downloads and total player counts (40M+) are touted, current Daily Active Users (DAU) and Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) are unknown [2].

## 10. Strategic Recommendations & Action Plan

Based on the research, Bombay Play should consider the following strategic actions:
1. **Optimise Ad-Load Balancing:** The rising volume of ad-fatigue complaints threatens long-term retention [3] [4]. The company must A/B test ad frequency caps and heavily promote its low-cost "no ads" IAP tier to preserve the player experience.
2. **Double Down on Live-Ops Stability:** Address the crash reports in *Block Heads* and *Card Party* immediately [3] [4]. Technical stability is paramount for real-time multiplayer games.
3. **Leverage Series A for IP Expansion:** Utilise the remaining $7M Series A capital to develop a new flagship IP that can push the studio past the 100M MAU mark, potentially exploring hybrid-casual RPG or social deduction genres.
4. **Enhance Financial Transparency:** To attract Series B investors or potential acquirers, Bombay Play should begin publishing high-level traction metrics (e.g., YoY revenue growth percentages, DAU milestones) to validate its valuation [6] [1].
