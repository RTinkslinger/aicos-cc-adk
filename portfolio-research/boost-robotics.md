# Boost Robotics — A U.S.‑Founded Data‑Center Robot Play‑to‑Enter India’s Growing Automation Market

## Executive Summary
Boost Robotics is a U.S.-based startup developing autonomous mobile manipulation robots specifically designed for data center inspection and maintenance [1]. Founded in 2025 by Carnegie Mellon University alumni, the company aims to address the severe staffing shortages and operational inefficiencies caused by the rapid scaling of AI infrastructure [1] [2]. Having raised $500K in pre-seed funding from notable investors including Y Combinator and Andreessen Horowitz [3], Boost Robotics is positioned to disrupt facility management. While currently headquartered in San Francisco, the company's value proposition—reducing downtime and saving millions in SLA violations—presents a strong potential fit for India's rapidly expanding and increasingly remote data center market [1] [3].

## Company Overview and Founding Team
Boost Robotics was founded in 2025 and is headquartered in San Francisco, California [1] [3]. The company is led by co-founders Hardik Singh and Hans Kumar, who met as engineering freshmen at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) in 2015 [1]. Both founders hold Master’s degrees from CMU’s Robotics Institute [1]. 

The leadership team brings deep technical pedigree to the enterprise:
* **Hans Kumar**: Previously served as a Staff Software Engineer at Boston Dynamics, where he worked on productizing computer vision algorithms for the Spot inspection robot [1] [2].
* **Hardik Singh**: Formerly a robotics team lead at an agtech startup, where he successfully pivoted the team to a new product that generated sales within one month [1] [2].

## Product and Technology
Boost Robotics builds general-purpose autonomous mobile manipulation robots that can physically interact with data center infrastructure [1]. Unlike passive observation drones, these physical agents are designed to remotely perform manual tasks, predict issues, and solve them faster even when human technicians are not on-site [1]. 

Key capabilities include:
* **Context-Aware Teleoperation**: Enables a single skilled technician to debug multiple issues across various sites [1].
* **24/7 Monitoring**: Provides continuous security monitoring and inventory management [1].
* **Digital Twin Integration**: Collects comprehensive data to simplify compliance, financial audits, and digital twin modeling [1].

## Funding History and Capital Structure
Boost Robotics is a privately held, venture capital-backed startup [3]. The company participated in the Y Combinator Spring 2025 batch [1]. 

To date, the company has raised **$500K** in pre-seed funding [2] [3]. The investor syndicate includes prominent venture capital firms and accelerators:
* Andreessen Horowitz [3]

* Y Combinator [3]

## Market Position and Competitive Landscape
The surge in AI applications is forcing data centers to scale faster than their operations teams can manage, often resulting in facilities being built in remote locations with shortages of qualified onsite personnel [1] [2]. Inefficiencies in thermal management and routine maintenance lead to costly downtime [2]. 

While AI companies are increasingly turning to quadruped robots for security solutions [4], Boost Robotics differentiates itself through dexterous manipulation.

| **Boost Robotics** | Autonomous mobile manipulation | Data Centers | Yes (Bi-Manual Teleoperation) [1] |

Boost's focus on physical interaction gives it a distinct advantage over pure-inspection robots, allowing it to actively triage and address failures rather than just reporting them [1].

## Recent News and Business Model
Boost Robotics officially launched its platform via Y Combinator in June 2025 [1] [6]. By late 2025, the company was actively expanding its technical team, seeking founding engineers with deep expertise in machine learning for manipulation and mobile robotics [7]. 

The company's business model centers on B2B enterprise sales and pilot programs with forward-thinking data centers [1]. By increasing the frequency of inspection and maintenance, Boost Robotics provides a clear ROI to its customers: saving data centers millions in insurance premiums and SLA violations [1].
