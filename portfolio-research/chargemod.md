# ChargeMOD 2024-2026: India's Fast-Growing EV-Charging Platform – Growth Engines, Partnerships & Risks

## Executive Summary
ChargeMOD has rapidly evolved from a regional Kerala-based startup into a significant player in India's electric vehicle (EV) charging ecosystem. Operating over 5,000 chargers across 80+ cities, the company has secured major strategic partnerships with OEMs like Mercedes-Benz and Ather Energy [1] [2] [3]. Despite dominating the Kerala market with an estimated 90% share, ChargeMOD faces a severe capital disadvantage compared to national rivals, having raised only $664K to date [2] [4]. For investors and strategic partners, ChargeMOD presents a high-growth, capital-efficient acquisition or investment target, though its limited funding poses a risk to its ambitious nationwide expansion goals.

## Company Overview
Founded in 2019 and headquartered in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, ChargeMOD (BPM Power Private Limited) provides end-to-end EV charging solutions for residential, commercial, and fleet applications [2] [4]. The company bridges the gap in India's EV infrastructure by offering both proprietary hardware and a robust software ecosystem, enabling seamless charging experiences for EV owners while providing management tools for station operators [2] [5]. 

## Founding Team & Governance
The company was founded by a team of four engineering graduates from Government Engineering College, Kozhikode [6] [4]:
* **Raman M (CEO & Co-Founder):** Leads the company's strategic vision and operations [2] [6].
* **Chris Thomas (Co-Founder):** Key executive driving operational growth [2].
* **Anoop V & Advaith C (Co-Founders):** Technical leaders responsible for product development and engineering [2].

## Product & Technology Stack
ChargeMOD's value proposition lies in its integrated hardware-software synergy:
* **Hardware:** EV charging stations ranging from 3.3 kW AC chargers for homes to 180 kW DC fast chargers for commercial hubs [5].
* **Software (CMS):** A centralized Charger Management System for remote monitoring, optimization, and OCPI/OCPP-compliant roaming [5].
* **User Interface:** The ChargeMOD Mobile App for locating stations and managing payments, supplemented by the "Alpha RFID Card" for contactless access [5].

## Business Model & Traction
ChargeMOD monetizes through hardware sales, SaaS subscriptions for its CMS, and charging session revenues. 
* **Key Metrics:** The company reported an annual revenue of ₹10.8 Cr as of March 31, 2025 [2]. 
* **Network Size:** It operates over 5,000 chargers across 80+ cities, with a stated commitment to reach 20,000+ stations [1]. 
* **Team Size:** Tracxn reports 22 employees as of July 2024, though LinkedIn data suggests a broader workforce of 51-200 [2] [7].

## Recent Milestones (2024-2026)
ChargeMOD has aggressively pursued strategic partnerships to expand its footprint beyond Kerala:
* **Feb 2026:** Integrated natively with Mercedes-Benz's digital ecosystem (MB.CHARGE), allowing seamless access via the MBUX system [3].
* **Feb 2025:** Partnered with Ather Energy to expand the two-wheeler EV charging network [2].
* **Nov 2024:** Collaborated with HPCL to deploy fast chargers and enable OCPI roaming [2] [5].
* **Sep 2024:** Launched Kerala's largest EV charging hub in Angamaly [2].
* **May 2024:** Partnered with A Plus Charge to deliver 1,000+ charging solutions in North East India, signaling geographic diversification [2].

## Strategic Recommendations
1. **Accelerate Series A Fundraising:** ChargeMOD must secure institutional capital ($10M-$15M) immediately to defend its market share against well-funded rivals like Statiq and Bolt.Earth.
2. **Monetize the Software Layer:** Expand the SaaS offering by licensing the Charger Management System (CMS) to third-party fleet operators and real estate developers.
3. **Deepen OEM Integrations:** Use the successful Mercedes-Benz and Ather Energy integrations as case studies to lock in exclusive charging-as-a-service contracts with other major automakers entering the Indian market.
