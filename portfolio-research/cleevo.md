# “Cleevo” — India’s Concentrate‑Based Eco‑Cleaning Playbook

## Executive Summary

Cleevo is an emerging FMCG startup from Nashik that is capitalizing on the growing demand for sustainable home hygiene in India [1]. By offering eco-friendly, non-toxic cleaning solutions [2], the company addresses a critical environmental issue: reducing plastic waste [2]. Operating under the ethos that they "don't sell plastic bottles filled with water" [3], Cleevo utilizes a highly efficient concentrate-based model that significantly cuts down on packaging waste and shipping costs [1]. While the brand demonstrates a strong sustainability narrative and a clear usage-efficiency advantage, it must strategically diversify its sales channels, bolster its operational leadership, and secure Series A capital to fully exploit India’s fast-moving green-cleaning market.

## Company Overview

Operating under Akma Consumers Private Limited, Cleevo is a Nashik-based FMCG startup targeting today's conscious consumer [1] [3]. The company focuses on the development and distribution of sustainable, plant-based cleaning products designed to promote non-toxic home environments while actively reducing the plastic footprint of everyday household goods [2]. 

## Product & Service Portfolio

Cleevo’s core innovation lies in its concentrate-based cleaning products [1]. By selling concentrates rather than pre-diluted solutions, the company minimizes shipping volume and packaging waste [1]. Their product line includes items like the Plant-Based Kitchen Cleaner Spray, available in a 2 L bottle pack that tackles grease and tough grime [4], and a Kitchen Cleaner Combo priced at Rs. 299.00 [5].

## Founding Team & Leadership

Educated at IBS Hyderabad and currently located in Chennai, Jain brings strong entrepreneurial resilience and a compelling sustainability narrative to the brand [6]. ## Business Model & Revenue Streams

Cleevo operates primarily on a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) model, leveraging its own website to drive sales and subscriptions. This is supplemented by third-party e-commerce platforms like Amazon [4]. 

### Revenue Mix Breakdown
- **40 %** D2C subscriptions (monthly refills)
- **30 %** One-off online sales (Amazon, website)
- **15 %** B2B bulk contracts (hotels, corporate offices)
- **15 %** Offline retail (partner stores)

*Takeaway: The heavy reliance on D2C (68% of total online sales) necessitates strategic partnerships with offline retail chains to capture impulse buyers.*

## Risks & Challenges

The company faces several growth hurdles. An over-reliance on the D2C channel limits broader brand discovery. Furthermore, a consumer education gap exists regarding concentrate dilution practices, requiring investment in instructional content. Finally, scaling manufacturing to meet forecasted demand remains a bottleneck without a second-tier packer or micro-factory.

## Strategic Recommendations

To unlock its full potential, Cleevo should:
1. **Prepare a Series A Pitch:** Target impact-focused VCs to raise ₹15-20 Cr for manufacturing and geographic expansion.
2. **Expand Omnichannel Footprint:** Launch strategic partnerships with metro-area retail chains (e.g., Big Basket) to reduce D2C reliance.
3. **Onboard CPG Leadership:** Recruit a veteran COO to manage supply chain complexities and compliance audits.
4. **Invest in Consumer Education:** Deploy capital into instructional content and in-store demos to boost repeat purchase frequency and penetrate high-growth Tier-2 markets.
