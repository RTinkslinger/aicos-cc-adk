# Digital Paani – IoT-Powered Wastewater Turnaround for India’s Water Crisis

## Executive Summary
Founded in 2020, Digital Paani is an Indian climate-tech startup revolutionising wastewater management through an IoT-enabled lifecycle management platform [1] [2]. Addressing a critical infrastructure gap where 75% of India's 85,000 to 90,000 sewage treatment plants (STPs) remain non-functional, the company transforms defunct facilities into compliant, high-efficiency units [1] [3] [4]. By automating plant operations rather than just monitoring them, Digital Paani enables clients to reduce operating costs by 25% to 35%, increase treatment capacity by up to 30%, and save millions of litres of freshwater [1] [5] [6]. Backed by a $1.2 million seed round and led by a highly experienced father-daughter duo, the company is rapidly scaling its footprint across Indian municipalities and major corporate campuses [3] [7] [4].

## Product & Technology
Digital Paani provides an end-to-end IoT and AI-driven software platform that acts as a "medical doctor for water systems" [7]. Unlike traditional systems that rely entirely on manual operators, Digital Paani's platform uses custom-made sensors and over 75 proprietary algorithms to comprehensively detect issues, launch automated workflows, and provide real-time maintenance recommendations [1]. 

The system is highly adaptable, featuring a plug-and-play deployment model that takes only 30 to 45 days to install across various treatment technologies (biological, chemical, RO, UF) [4]. Each partnered plant saves approximately 58 million litres of freshwater and offsets carbon equivalent to 5,200 trees [1].

## Founding Team
The Gurgaon-based startup is spearheaded by the father-daughter duo of Rajesh Jain and Mansi Jain [1] [3]. 
* **Rajesh Jain (Co-Founder):** An IIT-Delhi alumnus and veteran environmental engineer with over 30 years of experience, having designed and operated over 400 wastewater treatment facilities in India [3] [4].
* **Mansi Jain (Co-Founder & CEO):** A Stanford University graduate in environmental economics who brings global climate-tech strategy experience from stints with the California Governor’s Office, WWF, and NRDC [3] [8] [4].

## Funding History
Digital Paani has raised a total of $1.2 million in seed funding across rounds culminating in late 2023/early 2024 [1] [7] [5]. The cap table features a strong mix of climate-focused funds and prominent angel investors:
* **Institutional Investors:** Elemental Excelerator, Enzia Ventures, Climate Angels Fund, Peer Cheque, SAE, DevC, and Bharat Founders Fund [7] [5].
* **Angel Investors:** Ashish Goel (Urban Ladder), Alok Mittal (Indifi), Mohit Sadani (Moms Co), and Mohit Tandon (Delhivery) [7] [5].

## Business Model
The company monetises its technology through a dual business model that de-risks adoption for facility owners [3] [4]:
1. **SaaS Subscription (Software-Only):** Clients use the software to manage plants with their existing, under-utilised staff (like electricians), guided by Digital Paani's remote management, saving up to 66% on operating costs [4].
2. **Full-Operations Management:** Digital Paani takes over the entire operations contract, deploying a shared fleet of service providers to manage clusters of plants efficiently [4].

In the 2023-24 financial year (up to February 2024), the company generated revenues of Rs 2.2 crore [4].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape
India's wastewater treatment market is vast but highly fragmented and inefficient, with 95% of the market managed manually [4]. Digital Paani differentiates itself by offering active operational control rather than passive monitoring.

| Feature/Metric | Digital Paani | Traditional Competitors (e.g., REVY, WEGoT, Aquasense) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Offering** | IoT automation, AI diagnostics, and workflow execution [1] [4] | Static data dashboards, basic pump automation, or biotech solutions [3] [4] [9] |
| **Operational Impact** | Runs the plant proactively; auto-adjusts equipment [3] [4] | Passive monitoring; requires manual intervention [3] [4] |
| **Cost Reduction** | 25% - 35% OPEX reduction [1] [6] | Minimal direct OPEX reduction [4] |

*Digital Paani's ability to guarantee savings and automate workflows gives it a distinct advantage over dashboard-only competitors.*

The company has secured marquee clients, including Tata Power, Britannia, Taj Hotels, Plaksha University, and the Delhi Jal Board, operating in over 14 states with more than 40 active plants [3] [4].
