# Dodo Payments — India-Built MoR Platform Scaling Global SaaS & AI Billing

## Executive Summary

Dodo Payments is a rapidly growing Merchant of Record (MoR) and billing platform designed specifically for AI-native and SaaS businesses, with a strong focus on empowering founders in India and emerging markets to sell globally [1] [2]. By abstracting away the complexities of cross-border tax compliance, local payment methods, and usage-based billing, Dodo enables startups to monetize digital products across 150+ countries from day one [1] [3]. While still in its early stages with a $1.1M pre-seed round, the company has quickly captured market share among AI developers by offering specialized credit-based metering and significantly faster onboarding than legacy incumbents [2] [3].

## Company Overview & Value Proposition

Founded in 2024 and operating out of Bengaluru, India, and Dover, US, Dodo Payments provides an end-to-end monetization engine [1]. As a Merchant of Record, Dodo assumes the legal liability for transactions, handling global tax calculation, filing, fraud protection, and compliance automatically [2] [3]. 

Its core value proposition centers on flexibility for modern software architectures. The platform supports one-time payments, subscriptions, and complex usage-based billing (e.g., token usage or AI generation credits) [3]. For Indian founders who traditionally face high failure rates and compliance nightmares with international checkouts, Dodo offers a unified API that supports over 80 currencies and 25+ local payment methods, including Apple Pay, Klarna, and UPI [2] [3].

## Founding Team

The leadership team brings direct experience in cross-border fintech and digital product distribution:
* **Rishabh Goel (Co-Founder & CEO):** Previously built the cross-border Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) product for Bzaar, where he experienced the friction of international payments firsthand. He is an alumnus of London Business School [2] [4].
* **Ayush Agarwal (Co-Founder & CPTO):** Previously founded Tournafest, where he faced severe logistical challenges in collecting global payments and managing international taxes, which directly inspired Dodo's MoR model [1] [2].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

Dodo Payments competes directly with established global payment gateways and MoR platforms, positioning itself as a more agile, developer-friendly, and AI-ready alternative [3]. 

| Competitor | Core Model | Dodo's Differentiation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stripe** | Payment Gateway | Dodo acts as an MoR (handling tax liability) and offers faster KYC/onboarding for Indian sole proprietors and micro-SaaS founders [3]. |
| **Paddle / Lemon Squeezy** | MoR | Dodo offers more granular usage-based billing for AI apps (e.g., per-second GPU metering) and highly responsive community support [1] [3]. |
| **Razorpay / Cashfree** | Domestic Gateway | Dodo focuses explicitly on frictionless cross-border SaaS/digital product sales without requiring complex international entity setups [2] [3]. |

*Takeaway: Dodo is carving out a niche by targeting the specific billing patterns of generative AI applications (credit drips, dual-layer compute billing) while solving the geographic disadvantage of Indian developers.*

## Business Model & Traction

Dodo Payments monetizes through transaction fees, subscription add-ons, and usage-metering charges, avoiding fixed monthly costs for standard users [3]. 

| Fee Category | Pricing Structure |
| :--- | :--- |
| **US Domestic (Cards/Wallets)** | 4% + $0.40 per transaction [3] |
| **India Domestic (INR/UPI)** | 4% + ₹4 (one-time) or 4% + $0.15 (subscriptions) [3] |
| **International Payments** | +1.5% surcharge [3] |
| **Subscriptions / Invoicing** | +0.5% for subscriptions; +0.4% for invoicing [3] |
| **Usage-Based Billing** | $1 per Million Events [3] |

**Traction:** As of February 2025, Dodo had onboarded over 1,000 merchants across 30+ countries. The company stated a goal to reach 10,000+ merchants globally by the end of 2025 [2].

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **For AI Startups:** Leverage Dodo's native credit-based billing and open-source `BillingSDK` to avoid building custom ledger infrastructure for token/GPU metering [1] [5].
