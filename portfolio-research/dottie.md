## Dottie: IndiGo's AI Chat Assistant

The most significant Indian tech footprint for "Dottie" belongs to IndiGo, India's popular airline, which introduced the conversational AI-powered assistant to handle high-volume customer queries [1]. 

### Product Description and Value Proposition
Dottie is designed to facilitate self-service in the aviation sector. The AI assistant allows passengers to bypass traditional customer care call centers to resolve common issues [1]. Key functionalities include booking flights, performing web check-ins, auto-assigning seats, retrieving itineraries, checking flight status, and answering queries related to baggage allowances, refunds, and cancellations [1]. The bot is optimized to understand short phrases and keywords (e.g., "baggage allowance") [1].

### Market Position and Business Model
Operating within the highly competitive Indian aviation market, Dottie serves as an internal cost-reduction and customer-experience enhancement tool rather than a revenue-generating standalone product. By automating routine tasks like boarding pass retrieval and flight status checks, IndiGo significantly reduces its call center load [1]. The assistant was crafted in alliance with Yellow.ai, transforming the airline's WhatsApp engagement strategy [6]. 

### Recent Milestones (2024-2026)
While Dottie has been available for chat support since at least 2020 [7], IndiGo has continued to evolve its AI capabilities. In late 2023, IndiGo also launched "6Eskai," an AI chatbot developed with Microsoft and powered by GPT-4 technology, capable of handling 10 languages [8]. This indicates a broader, aggressive push by the airline into generative AI, with Dottie serving as a foundational conversational persona [9].
