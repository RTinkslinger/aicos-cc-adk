# EdgeQ – The "Base-Station-on-a-Chip" Play that's Re-defining Indian & Global 5G Small-Cell Markets

## Executive Summary
EdgeQ is a semiconductor startup disrupting the 5G infrastructure market with its unified 4G/5G and AI "Base-Station-on-a-Chip." By leveraging an open RISC-V architecture, the company targets the rapidly expanding private 5G and small-cell markets. With strong engineering roots in India and $126 million in funding, EdgeQ has secured major partnerships with telecom and hardware players like Vodafone, Airspan, and BATS Wireless. While commercial momentum is accelerating in 2026, specific revenue and margin data remain undisclosed.

## 1. Product Description and Value Proposition
EdgeQ has developed the world’s first fully programmable, massively integrated 4G+5G+AI "Base-Station-on-a-Chip" [1]. The system-on-a-chip (SoC) uniquely combines RISC-V and ARM architectures to compact an entire rack of base station equipment into a single, power-efficient chip [1]. 

**Value Proposition:** EdgeQ allows operators and enterprises to deploy public and private networks that are scalable and affordable [1]. Its software-defined nature enables seamless switching between 4G and 5G, or simultaneous operation, without requiring hardware replacements [2]. 

## 2. Founding Team and Leadership
Headquartered in Santa Clara, California, EdgeQ is led by veterans from top semiconductor firms, including Qualcomm, Intel, and Broadcom [3]. 
* **Vinay Ravuri (Founder & CEO):** Former Vice President of Product Management at Qualcomm, Ravuri founded EdgeQ to bring software-driven, open programmability to baseband infrastructure [3] [4].
* **Hariprasad Gangadharan (VP & Head of Silicon Engineering, Country Head India):** Leads the company's extensive silicon verification and engineering efforts [5].
* **Jim Anderson:** President and CEO of Lattice Semiconductor, who joined EdgeQ's Board of Directors in 2023 [4].

## 3. Funding History
EdgeQ has raised a total of $126 million across three funding rounds (one seed and two early-stage rounds) [5].

## 4. Market Position and Differentiation
EdgeQ operates at the intersection of the private 5G and small-cell RAN markets. The private 5G market is valued at $3.86 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $17.55 billion by 2030 (35.4% CAGR) [6]. Furthermore, small cell RAN revenues are expected to grow more than 20 percent by 2027 [7].

| Competitor | Architecture Approach | EdgeQ Differentiator |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Qualcomm / Marvell | Largely proprietary ARM-based SoCs [8] | Open RISC-V architecture; native AI integration [1] [5] |
| Picocom | Small-cell focused baseband [9] | Converged 4G/5G on a single chip with L1 inline acceleration [1] [10] |

## 5. Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)
EdgeQ has transitioned from silicon development to commercial hardware integration:
* **February 2025:** Partnered with BATS Wireless to launch the ECHO 401 and 405, America's first converged 4G/5G CBRS radios for defense and commercial use [2].
* **February 2026:** Airspan Networks launched its AirUnity Small Cells portfolio, powered by EdgeQ's baseband processor for indoor and outdoor densification [11].
* **March 2026 (MWC):** Unveiled the industry's first all-in-one 5G Femtocell in collaboration with Askey, and announced a joint communications platform with Texas Instruments [12] [13].

## 6. Business Model, Traction, and Indian Market Context
**Business Model:** EdgeQ monetizes through chip sales bundled with base software. Additional features, such as AI functionality or simultaneous 4G+5G operation, are licensed separately [5]. The company also charges maintenance fees for updates over the chip's 5- to 7-year lifecycle [5]. 

**Indian Market Context:** While headquartered in the US, EdgeQ relies heavily on its Indian operations. The company maintains engineering hubs in Bangalore, Pune, Noida, and Hyderabad [5]. This distributed model allows EdgeQ to tap into India's rich pool of ex-Qualcomm and Intel talent, positioning the company well for India's domestic 5G rollout and "Make in India" manufacturing incentives [4] [5].
