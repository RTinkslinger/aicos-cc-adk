# ElecTrade 2026 Deep-Dive: India's Emerging B2B Electronics Marketplace

## Executive Summary

ElecTrade (previously known as Hatchnhack) is a Delhi-based seed-stage startup operating as a B2B marketplace for electronic components and semiconductors in India [1] [2]. Founded in 2019, the company aims to streamline the procurement process for businesses seeking specialized electronic parts [2]. While positioned as a B2B platform, ElecTrade's current catalog reveals a strong niche focus on drone manufacturing, robotics, and electric vehicle (EV) infrastructure components [3]. Led by a team with deep corporate and logistics experience, the company represents a growing segment of localized supply-chain solutions in India's electronics manufacturing sector.

## Company Overview & Founding Team

ElecTrade was established in 2019 and is headquartered in Delhi, India [2]. The company originally operated under the name Hatchnhack before rebranding to Electrade to better reflect its focus on electronic trading and B2B procurement [1]. 

The founding team brings a mix of operational and corporate expertise:
* **Rishikesh Ranjan:** Serves as the CEO of Electrade, driving the vision to build a comprehensive electronic components marketplace [4].
* **Milind Srivastava:** Co-founder who brings extensive industry experience, having previously worked at major global and regional firms including Flipkart, Goldman Sachs, and Samsung Electronics [5]. 

## Product & Service Portfolio

While ElecTrade is marketed as a general destination for B2B buyers seeking semiconductors and electronic components [1], its active inventory demonstrates a highly specialized focus on aerial robotics, FPV (First-Person View) systems, and emerging tech hardware [3].

## Funding History & Business Model

ElecTrade is currently classified as a seed-stage company [2]. Operating as a digital marketplace, the company bridges the gap between electronic component manufacturers and Indian buyers [1]. By offering localized pricing in INR (₹) and domestic shipping, ElecTrade reduces the friction typically associated with importing specialized components like flight controllers and micro-cameras from overseas distributors [3]. 

## Market Position & Recent Developments

ElecTrade occupies a unique position in the Indian market. By transitioning from the Hatchnhack brand to Electrade, the company has signaled a maturation toward serious B2B procurement [1]. However, the heavy presence of consumer-grade and prosumer drone kits (such as the DJI Mini 2 SE and Autel Evo Nano Plus) alongside raw industrial components (like CMOS sensors and brushless motors) suggests the company is currently straddling the line between a specialized B2B distributor and a high-end electronics retailer [3]. 

To solidify its market position moving forward, ElecTrade will need to leverage its founders' enterprise experience [5] to expand its core semiconductor offerings while capitalizing on its established niche in India's rapidly growing drone and EV manufacturing sectors.
