# Fabriclore: Scaling the "Digital Thread" from Jaipur to Global Fashion Hubs

## Executive Summary

Fabriclore has successfully transformed from a niche B2C online fabric retailer into a tech-enabled B2B fabric sourcing powerhouse for global fashion brands. Founded in Jaipur in 2016, the company pivoted entirely to a B2B model in 2023, leveraging its deep textile expertise to solve supply chain fragmentation for small and mid-sized fashion labels [1] [2]. By integrating over 50 mills and printers into a centralized tech platform, Fabriclore offers ultra-low Minimum Order Quantities (MOQs), custom digital printing, and end-to-end garment manufacturing [3]. Backed by a pivotal $1.6 million funding round in 2024, the company has expanded its footprint into the Middle East and launched comprehensive woven garment production programs for the Spring/Summer 2026 global fashion cycle [4] [1].

## Product and Service Ecosystem

Fabriclore provides a full-stack sourcing solution that bridges traditional Indian craftsmanship with modern digital efficiency. The platform's core offerings include:

* **Made-to-Order Customization:** The company provides digital, screen, and block printing, alongside dyeing solutions across materials like cotton, linen, viscose, and sustainable fabrics [5] [1].
* **Woven Fabric Garmenting:** Launched ahead of the Spring/Summer 2026 season, this program combines premium fabric sourcing with end-to-end garment manufacturing, allowing brands to source and produce export-ready garments seamlessly [4] [6].
* **Ready Stock & Greige:** Fabriclore supplies mill-dyed, yarn-dyed, and RFD (Ready-for-Dyeing) fabrics for immediate purchase [5].

## Founding Team and Cultural DNA

Fabriclore was founded in 2016 by a trio of entrepreneurs: Vijay Sharma (CEO), Anupam D Arya (VP of Product), and Sandeep Sharma [5] [1]. Operating out of Jaipur, Rajasthan, the founders combined traditional merchant heritage with modern technological execution [3] [2]. Demonstrating immense financial discipline during their 2023 B2B pivot, the founders revealed during their March 2024 appearance on *Shark Tank India 3* that they forewent their salaries for seven months to stabilize the company's transition [5] [1].

## Market Position and Competitive Landscape

In the highly fragmented Indian textile market, Fabriclore differentiates itself by focusing on design-driven, low-MOQ sourcing for private labels, contrasting with competitors who focus on high-volume enterprise manufacturing.

*Fabriclore's niche in customizable, low-batch artisanal and sustainable fabrics protects it from the pure price-war dynamics of bulk manufacturing platforms.*

## Business Model

Fabriclore operates a "sourcing-as-a-service" B2B model. After serving over 300,000 customers in its B2C phase, the company pivoted to serve over 400 private labels [1] [10]. The model relies on a tailor-made ERP system that connects warehouse operations to production, providing real-time tracking. This technological integration has successfully reduced customer fabric rejections by 33%, solving a major pain point in international textile procurement [1]. 

## Recent News and Future Projections

Fabriclore is aggressively targeting international expansion. In February 2026, the company announced the strengthening of its woven fabric garment production offerings to cater to global fashion businesses preparing for the Spring/Summer 2026 season [4] [11]. The company aims to triple its Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and supply 40 lakh meters of made-to-order fabrics annually by FY2027, driven by its expansion into the Middle East, Europe, and the USA [1].
