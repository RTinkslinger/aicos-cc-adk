# Democratizing Wellness: Felicity's Strategic Play for India's Underserved Mental Health Market

## Executive Summary
Felicity (legally incorporated as Orgfit Global Consultancy Private Limited) is a Jaipur-based health-tech startup that provides an end-to-end digital ecosystem for mental health counseling and therapy [1] [2]. Founded by a sibling duo, the platform distinguishes itself by targeting "Bharat"—the semi-urban population of India—where 50% of its user base currently resides [1]. By utilizing a freelance model for vetted psychologists and offering a tiered pricing structure that starts at just ₹999, Felicity addresses the critical gaps of affordability and accessibility in the Indian healthcare sector [1]. While other startups sharing the "Felicity" name in different sectors have raised millions, the mental health platform remains a lean, unfunded operation focused on sustainable unit economics and high-quality clinical care [3] [4].

## Product and Service Ecosystem
Felicity operates as a comprehensive mental health and wellness ecosystem rather than a simple marketplace [1]. The core offering revolves around online video counseling, allowing users to connect with clinical psychologists from anywhere across the country [1]. 

* **Core Therapy Sessions:** Users book 45-minute video therapy sessions with experts who hold Masters degrees in clinical or counseling psychology and possess at least two years of clinical experience [1]. 
* **Continuous Support:** Beyond synchronous video calls, the platform provides users with the ability to send voice messages and text chats to their doctors [1].
* **Integrated Wellness Hub:** Registered users gain access to a broader community where they can participate in forum discussions, take mental health assessment tests, and utilize Felicity’s journaling product to maintain their mental wellbeing between sessions [1].

## Founding Team and Leadership
Incorporated in December 2019 and officially launched in June 2020, Felicity is led by a sibling team that bridges the gap between corporate human resources and technology [1] [2]. 

* **Pratika Khandelwal (Co-founder & CEO):** An XLRI Jamshedpur graduate with over 12 years of HR and business experience across organizations like Bosch, HCL, and Edureka [5] [1]. 
* **Vaibhav Khandelwal (Co-founder & CPO):** An IMT Ghaziabad graduate with a background at Deloitte in valuation and M&A. He previously founded Fuzzy Circuit, an IoT/smart home startup [1] [6].
* **Clinical Advisory Board:** The clinical direction is guided by veterans like Dr. Shubha Sadanand Thatte, a clinical psychologist with nearly 50 years of experience, and Dr. Anil Tambi, a psychiatrist with 30 years of academic and practical experience [5].

## Business Model and Pricing
Felicity operates a B2C subscription and package-based revenue model. To keep overhead low and scale efficiently, the company onboards psychologists on a freelancing model, paying them per session [1]. This allows Felicity to pass cost savings onto the consumer.

## Market Position and Competitive Landscape
Felicity competes in a crowded mental health market against domestic players like YourDOST and BetterLYF, as well as global giants like BetterHelp and Talkspace [1]. 

However, Felicity has carved out a unique moat by focusing on semi-urban India. The founders noted that 50% of their users come from semi-urban cities, driven by a severe lack of local, well-qualified psychologists in these regions [1]. By breaking geographical boundaries and offering therapy in regional languages, Felicity effectively captures the underserved "Bharat" demographic that premium, metro-focused competitors often overlook [1].

## Funding History
Unlike many of its venture-backed competitors, Felicity (Orgfit Global Consultancy Private Limited) is currently an unfunded company [3]. *Note: Investors analyzing the space should be careful not to confuse this entity with "Felicity Games" (Felicity Labs Pte. Ltd.), an AI game-tech company that recently raised a $3M seed round and expanded to Singapore [4].* The mental health startup has maintained a bootstrapped, capital-efficient approach since its inception [3].

## Recent News and Strategic Roadmap
Looking ahead, Felicity is actively pursuing strategic B2B partnerships to build out its ecosystem. The company aims to integrate its services with educational institutions, hospitals, psychology colleges, corporate entities, and insurance companies to ensure wider reach [1]. Furthermore, leadership has outlined ambitious plans to expand services into the US and UK markets, capitalizing on the global arbitrage opportunity where Western mental healthcare remains extremely expensive compared to Indian-provided telehealth services [1].
