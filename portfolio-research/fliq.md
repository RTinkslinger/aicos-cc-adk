# Fliq (formerly EthosX): A Creator-Led Prediction-Market Play Targeting India's 95% Untapped Trading Population

## Executive Summary
Fliq (formerly EthosX) is a creator-owned prediction market platform designed to bring decentralized finance to the masses, specifically targeting India's vast non-trading population. Originally launched as a B2B derivatives protocol under the Y Combinator Summer 2022 batch, the company successfully pivoted and rebranded to Fliq to focus on consumer-friendly, "yes/no" event trading. Backed by prominent investors including Y Combinator and Franklin Templeton, Fliq leverages the high-speed Aptos blockchain to offer micro-betting with limit orders and creator revenue sharing. By gamifying the trading experience and integrating it with creator-led communities, Fliq aims to convert India's 436 million digital news consumers into active market participants.

## Product and Service
Fliq operates as a decentralized prediction market where users can trade on the outcomes of real-world events across sports, politics, pop culture, and cryptocurrency [1]. The platform simplifies complex financial derivatives into intuitive "yes/no" questions, such as "Will Apple's stock rise?" [2]. 

Unlike traditional decentralized exchanges, Fliq is built on the Aptos blockchain, which allows for sub-second finality and extremely low gas fees [2]. The platform differentiates itself from competitors by offering advanced exchange-grade features tailored for retail users:
* **Limit Orders:** Allowing users to set specific price points for their predictions [3].
* **Creator Revenue Share:** Incentivizing influencers to create and promote markets [3].
* **XP Rewards:** A gamified leaderboard system that rewards top performers [3].

## Funding History
Fliq has secured strategic capital to fuel its transition from a B2B infrastructure provider to a consumer-facing application. On January 13, 2024, the company raised US $1.8 million in a Series Unknown funding round [1]. The startup is backed by a strong roster of institutional and Web3 investors, including Y Combinator (Batch S22), Franklin Templeton, and Hashed Emergent [4] [5]. This capital provides the necessary runway to scale user acquisition and maintain liquidity pools on the Aptos network.

## Market Position
Fliq is strategically positioned to capture the massive untapped retail market in India. Currently, only 5-6% of the Indian population participates in financial trading, compared to 50-60% in the United States [2]. This locks billions of dollars out of wealth creation. 

Fliq targets India's 436 million news consumers by blending news consumption with low-stakes betting [2]. By positioning trading as an engaging, creator-led activity—likened to consuming content on TikTok or Spotify—Fliq lowers the barrier to entry for the 95% of the population that currently does not trade [2] [5].

## Business Model
Fliq's business model relies on high-volume, low-friction trading facilitated by its creator network. The platform generates revenue through trading fees applied to settled prediction markets. A core pillar of its growth strategy is the **Creator Revenue Share** model, which distributes a portion of the platform fees back to the creators who generate and promote specific markets [3]. Additionally, the platform utilizes an XP reward system to drive daily active usage and user retention, creating a sticky ecosystem where users are incentivized to trade frequently [3].
