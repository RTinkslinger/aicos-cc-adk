# Garuda Aerospace 2024-2026 Playbook: Scaling India's Fast-Growing Drone Champion

## Executive Summary
Garuda Aerospace has rapidly evolved into one of India's premier drone technology companies, driven by a 135% year-over-year revenue surge to ₹110.8 Cr in FY24 [1]. Backed by strategic investors including cricket icon MS Dhoni, the Chennai-based startup has successfully expanded its footprint beyond India, securing crucial export licenses for the U.S., Australia, and the Middle East in mid-2025 [2]. With a recent $11 million Series B round valuing the company at $250 million [3], Garuda is aggressively targeting the booming Indian and global agricultural and defense drone markets.

## Company Snapshot & Value Proposition
Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Chennai, Garuda Aerospace specializes in the design, manufacturing, and customization of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) [4] [5]. The company offers a comprehensive "Drone-as-a-Service" (DaaS) model alongside hardware sales, providing over 30 different types of drones and more than 50 drone-related services [5]. Their core value proposition centers on precision agriculture (crop monitoring and spraying), industrial infrastructure inspection, and defense surveillance [6] [3]. 

## Founders & Leadership Team
The company is led by a dynamic founding team with strong academic and operational backgrounds:
* **Agnishwar Jayaprakash (Founder & CEO):** A Harvard Business School alumnus and former international swimmer, Jayaprakash has driven the company's strategic vision and global expansion [7] [2].
* **Rithika Mohan (Co-Founder & CIO/COO):** Holding executive education credentials from Harvard Business School, Mohan oversees the company's administrative and information operations [8] [9].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape
The Indian drone market is experiencing explosive growth, valued at $0.47 billion in 2025 and projected to reach $1.39 billion by 2030 (a 24.4% CAGR) [10]. The agriculture drone segment alone is expected to hit $2.11 billion by 2033 [11].

## Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)
Garuda has executed several transformative strategic moves between 2024 and early 2026:
* **Export Breakthrough (July 2025):** Secured export licenses to supply drones to the U.S., Australia, and the Middle East, focusing initially on the "Garuda Kisan Drone" for precision farming and surveillance drones for the Middle East [2].
* **Manufacturing Expansion (June 2025):** Inaugurated a new Agri-Drone Indigenization Facility in Chennai, expanding its production footprint by 35,000 sq. ft. to double annual production to 10,000 drones [12] [13].
* **Defense Partnership (January 2026):** Signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL) to jointly develop mission-ready Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) for indigenous defense opportunities [14] [15].

## Business Model and Traction
Garuda operates a diversified revenue model encompassing direct hardware sales, DaaS, and pilot training. 

**Key Traction Metrics:**
* **Revenue:** Reached ₹110.8 Cr in FY24, representing a 135.41% increase over the previous fiscal year [1].
* **Operational Scale:** Operates a fleet of over 400 drones with 500 pilots across 84 cities in India [12].
* **Training Ecosystem:** Claims to have trained over 100,000 students as drone pilots, creating a massive pipeline for its DaaS operations [3].
* **Customer Base:** Serves major enterprise clients including TATA, Godrej, Adani, Reliance, Swiggy, Amazon, and Delhivery [12].
