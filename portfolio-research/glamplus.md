# GlamPlus – Accelerating the Digitisation of India’s $200 bn Salon & Spa SMB Market

## Executive Summary

GlamPlus has rapidly emerged as a pivotal player in digitising India's unorganised salon and spa sector. Founded during the 2020 pandemic, the Bengaluru-based startup successfully pivoted from a pure SaaS management tool to a comprehensive B2B2C beauty marketplace. Backed by a recent ₹16.5 crore pre-Series A funding round in January 2024, GlamPlus is aggressively expanding its footprint across Tier I and Tier II cities [1]. By offering highly localised, cost-effective software alongside a direct-to-salon product supply chain, the company is well-positioned to capture a significant share of the $200 billion global SMB salon economy [2]. 

## Company Overview & Founding Team

Registered as RDV Design Technologies Pvt. Ltd., GlamPlus was founded in July 2020 and is headquartered in Bengaluru, Karnataka [3] [2]. The company was built to transition offline, pen-and-paper salon businesses into data-driven enterprises [2]. 

The founding team brings deep operational and technical expertise from prominent Indian startups:
* **Divyanshu Singh (CEO):** Previously worked with Pagarbook, Bounce, Ninjacart, and Ola [2].
* **Rohan Singh (COO):** Brings logistics and operations experience from Transln Logistics and Blackbuck [2].
* **Vatsalya Agarwal (CTO):** A full-stack developer with experience at Quotient Technology and Navya Networks [2].

## Product & Service Portfolio

GlamPlus operates a dual-track platform combining operational software with a product procurement marketplace. 

| Core Module | Key Features & Capabilities | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Salon & Spa SaaS** | Appointment scheduling, digital invoicing, staff management, and expense tracking [3] [2]. | Replaces manual ledgers; provides analytics on staff productivity and monetary health [2]. |
| **B2B Beauty Marketplace** | Online marketplace featuring over 2,000 SKUs from brands like L'Oreal, Lotus, and Mamaearth [3] [2]. | Allows salons to procure professional products directly from manufacturers at better margins [2]. |
| **Customer Engagement** | WhatsApp chatbot integration for future bookings and customer queries [2]. | Enhances customer retention and enables targeted promotional campaigns [2]. |

## Business Model & Revenue Streams

GlamPlus monetises through a hybrid B2B SaaS and B2B2C marketplace model:
* **SaaS Subscriptions:** The platform offers a 7-day free trial, followed by subscription packages (three-month, six-month, one-year) ranging from ₹1,500 to ₹5,000 [2]. 
* **Marketplace Margins:** By tying up directly with manufacturers, GlamPlus supplies personal care products to salons and modern trade centres (like Spencer and Big Bazaar), earning margins on product sales [2].
* **Marketing Services:** The startup runs promotional WhatsApp campaigns for member salons at an additional cost to help them attract customers [2].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

The Indian beauty and wellness market is projected to reach ₹2,463.49 billion by 2024, growing at a CAGR of ~18.40% [2]. While larger players like Zenoti, MioSalon, and Easy Salon dominate the enterprise and international spaces, GlamPlus specifically targets the underserved SMB segment [2]. 

GlamPlus differentiates itself through aggressive pricing and deep localisation. By offering software that is significantly cheaper than global competitors and bundling it with a product supply chain, GlamPlus has built a user base of over 9,500 beauty customer clients and 6 lakh B2C customers [1].

## Recent Developments & News (2024-2026)

In January 2024, GlamPlus secured ₹16.5 crore to scale its operations [4] [1]. To support its growing B2B2C marketplace, the company recently launched a centralised warehouse designed to facilitate faster order processing, improve inventory management, and minimise shipping costs [1]. The platform has registered a 12% annual growth in its SKU listings and aims to cater to 1,000 B2B beauty clients spanning luxury to small-sized parlours [1].

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Optimise App Stability:** User reviews on the Google Play Store indicate occasional glitches with the partner app [5]. GlamPlus must prioritise QA testing and release stability patches to prevent churn among less tech-savvy salon owners.
2. **Deepen Exclusive Brand Partnerships:** To defend its marketplace margins, GlamPlus should leverage its recent funding to secure exclusive distribution rights for emerging D2C beauty brands in Tier II cities.
3. **Expand Logistics Infrastructure:** While the new centralised warehouse improves fulfilment [1], scaling into Southeast Asia and deeper into India will require a robust 3PL partnership network to maintain delivery speeds.
