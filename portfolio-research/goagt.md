# GoAGT – From $1 M-a-Month Maritime-Security Star to 2014 Insolvency: Lessons for Today’s PMSC Landscape

## Executive Summary
Gulf of Aden Group Transits (GoAGT) was a prominent UK and Malta-based Private Maritime Security Company (PMSC) that provided armed guards to protect commercial vessels from piracy. Despite generating approximately $1 million per month at its peak, the company abruptly ceased trading in August 2014 due to severe financial mismanagement, leaving hundreds of armed guards stranded at sea without pay [1]. **Crucial Context for Indian Market:** GoAGT was not an Indian startup or tech company; it was a UK-incorporated maritime security firm (Company No. 07746066) that officially dissolved in February 2016 [2]. 

## Service Offering and Business Model
GoAGT operated as a tier-one maritime security provider, offering armed security personnel to protect ships and tankers transiting High-Risk Areas (HRAs), particularly off the coast of Somalia [3]. 

* **Value Proposition:** The company provided ISO 28007-accredited armed security teams to major shipping clients, including oil and gas giants like Shell [1]. 
* **Traction and Scale:** At its peak, GoAGT was considered one of the world's top 10 PMSCs. It maintained a pool of approximately 250 deployable ex-military staff and executed 50 to 60 ship transits per month [4]. 
* **Revenue Model:** The firm generated revenue through transit contracts, reportedly raking in about $1 million every month before its collapse [1].

## Founding Team and Governance Failures
The company's downfall was heavily tied to its centralized and opaque governance structure. 

* **Nick Davis (Founder & CEO):** A retired RAF helicopter pilot, Davis founded Anti Piracy Maritime Security Solutions (APMSS) in 2008 before establishing GoAGT [1]. He was the sole beneficial owner and maintained total authority over the company's finances [1]. Former employees alleged that Davis used company funds to finance other personal business ventures, such as an aviation advisory firm called 888 aero [1].
* **Cmdr. Gerry Northwood (COO):** A former commander of the UK counter-piracy task group, Northwood was brought in to provide operational gravitas. However, he resigned in July 2014 after discovering the full extent of the company's hidden debts and losing confidence in the CEO's ability to secure a rescue package [1].

## Financial Collapse and Insolvency Timeline
GoAGT did not follow a traditional venture capital funding path; there are no public records of institutional funding rounds, lead investors, or total equity raised. The company appeared to be entirely self-funded through its operational cash flow. 

The financial structure collapsed entirely in the summer of 2014.

When the company ceased trading in late July 2014, rival security firms like Sea Marshals and Tundra had to step in to disembark and repatriate the stranded GoAGT personnel at their own expense [4].

**Information Gap Flag:** Because GoAGT went insolvent in 2014 and was legally dissolved in 2016 [2], there are no recent news, milestones, or operational updates for the 2024-2026 period. 

During its operational years, GoAGT differentiated itself through its ISO 28007 accreditation and high volume of transits [4] [1]. However, its collapse served as a major cautionary tale for the maritime security industry regarding the risks of founder-centric financial control and the necessity of escrowed payroll accounts to protect deployed personnel.
