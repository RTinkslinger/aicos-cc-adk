# GoSocial (India) – How a Founder-Backed Creator-Monetisation Platform Is Positioning Itself in a $12 BN Indian Creator Economy

## Executive Summary
GoSocial is an early-stage, Gurugram-based creator monetisation platform operating as a sub-organization of Hapramp Studio [1] [2]. Despite operating in India's booming creator economy [3], GoSocial exhibits signs of stagnation as of early 2026. The platform reports minimal traction, with annual revenue of just ₹10L as of March 2025 [2] and only 503 total app downloads [1]. While its parent company, Hapramp Studio, successfully raised $1.81M across multiple rounds—including a high-profile $1M investment from Anand Mahindra in 2020 [4] [5] —GoSocial itself appears to be struggling with user acquisition and brand continuity, highlighted by its primary domain (gosocial.io) being listed for sale [6]. 

## Company Overview & Value Proposition
Founded in 2019 and headquartered in Gurugram, Haryana, GoSocial is a mobile application designed to help creators build engaged communities and monetise their expertise [1] [2]. The platform focuses heavily on visual and literary creators, allowing photographers, designers, artists, and writers to participate in contests and challenges to win rewards [2] [7]. GoSocial operates on the concept of "#100TrueFans," providing a creator stack that enables users to host digital offerings such as 1-on-1 mentorships, workshops, courses, and webinars [1] [6]. 

## Founding Team & Leadership
GoSocial was built by the founding team of its parent company, Hapramp Studio. The startup was founded by five engineers from the Indian Institute of Information Technology (IIIT) Vadodara: Pratyush Singh, Mofid Ansari, Ankit Kumar, Shubhendra Vikram, and Rajat Dangi [8] [7]. 
* **Shubhendra Vikram** serves as the Co-founder and current CEO of Hapramp Studio, spearheading the vision for GoSocial [5] [7].
* **Pratyush Singh, Mofid Ansari, and Rajat Dangi** are actively listed as Co-Founders of the GoSocial platform [1].

## Business Model & Traction
GoSocial's financial and user metrics indicate a failure to scale. As of March 31, 2025, the company reported an annual revenue of just ₹10L [2]. User engagement metrics are similarly low, with Apptopia reporting only 503 total app downloads and Crunchbase noting a mere 78 monthly website visits [1]. The platform's revenue model relies on facilitating paid communities, workshops, and contest participation [1] [10], but the low user base severely limits its earning potential.

## Recent News, Risks & Information Gaps (2024-2026)
Several critical risks and information gaps define GoSocial's current status:
* **Brand Abandonment Risk:** As of 2026, GoSocial's primary domain (gosocial.io) is parked and listed for sale, strongly suggesting a pivot, shutdown, or rebranding effort by Hapramp Studio [6]. 
* **Regulatory Headwinds:** The Indian creator market is facing increased scrutiny, such as the Goa government's 2024 introduction of a portal for the "Empanelment of Social Media Influencers," which adds compliance layers for creator platforms [11].
* **Information Gaps:** There is a distinct lack of public PR, feature updates, or active user metrics for GoSocial post-2025, making it difficult to ascertain if the app is still actively maintained by the Hapramp team.

## Strategic Recommendations
1. **Clarify Operational Status:** Given the domain sale [6] and low download metrics [1], stakeholders must immediately verify if GoSocial is still an active project within Hapramp Studio.
2. **Pivot or Consolidate:** If active, GoSocial must pivot away from direct competition with Instagram and GuruShots [2]. It should leverage Hapramp's blockchain security expertise [5] to offer unique, tokenized rewards for creators, differentiating itself in the crowded Indian market.
3. **Address Compliance:** To attract enterprise-level creators, the platform must build tooling to navigate emerging Indian influencer regulations [11].
