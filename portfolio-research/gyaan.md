# Gyaan AI: Navigating India's EdTech and Enterprise AI Markets

## Executive Summary
Research into "Gyaan" within the Indian startup ecosystem reveals two distinct technology companies operating under the same or similar names. The first is **Gyaan AI (EdTech)**, an emerging K-12 adaptive learning platform focused on the Indian Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) market. The second is **Gyaan AI (Enterprise)**, a revenue intelligence startup with offices in Pune and California, which recently rebranded to **MaxIQ**. This report delineates the strategic positions, funding, and market traction of both entities to provide a comprehensive overview of the "Gyaan" brand footprint in Indian tech.

## 1. Company Overview & Value Proposition

### 1.1 Gyaan AI (EdTech)
Gyaan AI is an AI-powered educational assistant designed for Class 6-12 students [1] [2]. The platform aims to replace expensive traditional tuition (which averages ₹8,000–₹15,000 monthly) with an affordable, real-time adaptive learning ecosystem [1]. Core features include 24/7 instant doubt solving via text, voice, or camera, customized mock tests, and a repository of over 550 previous year exam papers [1]. To cater to the diverse Indian market, the platform supports multiple languages, including English, Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, and Hinglish [1]. Additionally, the company offers a white-labeled B2B solution for schools to deploy customized AI-driven education platforms [3].

### 1.2 Gyaan AI / MaxIQ (Revenue Intelligence)
Formerly known as Gyaan AI, MaxIQ is an agentic AI and revenue intelligence platform that connects sales, customer success, and RevOps teams [4] [5]. Founded in 2022, the company maintains operations in both Pune, India, and California, USA [6]. 

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership

The leadership teams for both entities reflect deep technical and growth expertise:

* **Gyaan AI (EdTech)**: The company is spearheaded by Founder & CTO Harshit Sharma, an IIT Delhi alumnus with a background in full-stack AI app development [7]. He is joined by Founder Mukul Rathee, a 24-year-old entrepreneur with prior experience building AI productivity tools [8], and Head of Growth Ilan Bhatia, who focuses on scaling the platform's impact across India [3].
* **MaxIQ (formerly Gyaan AI)**: The enterprise SaaS platform was founded by Sonny Aulakh in 2022 [6].

## 4. Market Landscape & Competitive Set

The Indian K-12 EdTech market is highly competitive, dominated by legacy giants with massive content libraries. Gyaan AI (EdTech) differentiates itself by focusing on AI-driven personalization rather than static video content.

### Table 2: Competitor Feature Matrix (EdTech)

|---|---|---|

*Takeaway: Gyaan AI's competitive moat relies on its multilingual LLM capabilities and instant doubt resolution, targeting students frustrated by the rigid structures of traditional EdTech platforms.*

## 5. Business Model and Traction

**Gyaan AI (EdTech)** operates on a dual business model. Its B2C app utilizes a freemium structure, where users can ask initial doubts for free before hitting a premium paywall [2] [10]. On the B2B side, it licenses its technology to educational institutions [3]. Historically, the platform has claimed traction of over 10,000 students, 2,000 teachers, and 1,000 institutes [11]. On the Google Play Store, the app maintains a 4.9-star rating with over 100 downloads as of late 2025 [2]. 

**Information Gaps & Risks**: There is limited public information regarding the EdTech company's exact subscription pricing, Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR), and customer acquisition costs. Furthermore, the app's Google Play Data Safety section indicates that user data is not encrypted, which poses a potential privacy risk for a platform targeting minors [2].
