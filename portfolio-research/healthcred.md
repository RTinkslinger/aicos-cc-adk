# HealthCred — FinTech-Enabled Working-Capital Engine for India’s Hospital Backbone

## Executive Summary

Founded in 2022 and based in Delhi, HealthCRED is a specialized B2B healthcare financing startup operating as a healthcare-focused small finance bank (SFB) [1] [2]. The company addresses a critical bottleneck in India's rapidly expanding healthcare ecosystem: the severe working capital gap faced by small and mid-sized hospitals due to delayed insurance claim realizations [2]. By providing intelligent, alternate data-driven financing solutions against cashless insurance receivables, HealthCRED stabilizes cash flow cycles for healthcare providers, ensuring uninterrupted patient care and aligning with universal healthcare coverage goals [2]. 

## Product & Service Offering

HealthCRED delivers swift, comprehensive, and adaptable financing solutions tailored specifically for the healthcare sector [3]. The platform's core offering revolves around early financing of delayed insurance receivables, which have become a significant stress point for hospitals following the rapid post-COVID-19 penetration of health insurance [2]. 

The company partners with a diverse array of healthcare stakeholders, including:
* General hospitals and clinics [2].
* Specialized healthcare service providers (HSPs) across oncology, nephrology, and diagnostics [2] [3].
* Insurers, pharmaceutical companies, and consumable supply chain partners [2].

To scale these offerings, HealthCRED is developing state-of-the-art B2B loan onboarding and loan management systems to create an end-to-end digitized healthcare lending framework [2].

## Founding Team & Expertise

HealthCRED was founded in 2022 by Shrey Jain and Arpit Jangir [4] [2]. Shrey Jain, a serial entrepreneur who serves as the Co-Founder and CEO, drives the company's strategic vision to stabilize hospital cash flows and improve alignment with SDG Target 3.8 for universal healthcare coverage (UHC) [5] [2]. The leadership team's combined expertise in fintech and healthcare operations enables them to navigate the complex regulatory and operational hurdles of India's medical financing landscape.

## Market Position & Landscape

HealthCRED targets the burgeoning needs of India’s $300 Billion+ healthcare industry [2]. The market presents a massive opportunity because traditional banks and Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) typically focus on large hospital infrastructural loans, leaving small to mid-sized hospitals heavily underserved [2]. 

As health insurance penetration increases, the delay in insurer payments disproportionately impacts these smaller facilities, which often lack robust capital access and advanced claims management capabilities [2]. HealthCRED positions itself directly in this multi-billion dollar gap, offering working capital finance against cashless insurance receivables [2].

## Business Model & Revenue Mechanics

HealthCRED operates a B2B lending model driven by alternate data-driven underwriting [2]. By assessing the reliability of insurance receivables rather than relying solely on traditional hard-asset collateral, the company can underwrite loans faster and more accurately for healthcare providers. The company has set ambitious growth targets, aiming to disburse over $1 billion through digital means over the next five years [2]. Revenue is generated through the interest spread on these working capital advances and associated processing fees for its digitized loan management services.

## Recent News & Strategic Partnerships

Following its 2024 seed funding, HealthCRED has focused on expanding its operational footprint into the Southern and Western parts of India [2]. In a significant move to bolster its regulatory and public-sector footprint, HealthCRED recently named former Florida Deputy Secretary Steve Casey as the Executive Vice President of Government & Strategic Partnerships [6]. This appointment signals HealthCRED's ambition to navigate complex governmental healthcare frameworks and potentially secure large-scale public-sector healthcare financing partnerships.
