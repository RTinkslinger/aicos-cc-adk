# IG Drones (IG Defence) 2024-26: India's Fast-Tracking Indigenous UAV Playbook

## Executive Summary
IG Drones, recently rebranded as IG Defence, has successfully executed a strategic pivot from civilian enterprise drone services to a defense-first manufacturing powerhouse. Driven by a **330% YoY revenue jump to ₹22.4 Cr in FY25** [1], the company has cemented its position in India's military supply chain. Following its critical role in the 2025 Operation Sindoor and a fresh **$5M Pre-Series A funding round in March 2026** [1], the company is aggressively scaling its indigenous FPV (First-Person View) and VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing) capabilities to capture a larger share of India's rapidly expanding drone market.

## 1. Company Snapshot & Value Proposition
Originally founded in 2018 to provide enterprise drone solutions for powerline monitoring, smart city surveys, and mining [2] [3] [1], the Noida-headquartered company has strategically evolved into IG Defence [4]. The company now focuses exclusively on defense-grade unmanned systems, homeland security, and ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance) technology [4]. 

Their value proposition centers on "100% Made in India" indigenous innovation, boasting 25+ patents and India's first 5G drone technology [4] [5].

## 5. Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)
* **Operation Sindoor (2025)**: IG Drones served as a critical partner for the Indian government, supplying FPV striker drones and reconnaissance services during this major military operation [1] [11].
* **Indian Army Contract (Jan 2025)**: Secured a significant order to deliver domestically built VTOL and FPV drones within a month [1] [12].
* **Project KAL Launch (2026)**: Unveiled an indigenous one-way attack unmanned system (inspired by the Shahed-136) designed for deep-penetration missions [1].

## 6. Business Model and Traction
IG Defence operates a hybrid B2B and B2G business model, transitioning from enterprise SaaS and mapping to high-value defense hardware contracts. 

* **Revenue Growth**: Reported a massive 330% YoY jump in revenue to ₹22.4 Cr in FY25 (up from ₹5.17 Cr in FY24) [1]. The company is targeting ₹100 Cr in revenue for FY26 [1].
* **Customer Mix**: Revenue is split 60:40 between government defense contracts and private sector enterprise clients [1].
* **Operational Scale**: The company sold over 5,000 drones in FY25 and has expanded its manufacturing capacity to produce up to 1 Lakh FPV drones annually [1]. Historically, they have completed 3,500+ drone flights across 175+ projects [3].

## 7. Strategic Recommendations & Risk Assessment
* **Risk - Over-reliance on Defense**: While the 60% government revenue split is lucrative, defense procurement cycles can be volatile. *Action*: Maintain and upsell the high-margin enterprise SaaS platform (AI-driven inspection reports) to their existing base of 52+ industrial clients [3].
* **Risk - Manufacturing Verification**: While claiming a 1 Lakh unit capacity [1], third-party manufacturing audits are not publicly visible. *Action*: Secure and publicize AS9100/ISO certifications to de-risk future global export ambitions.
* **Opportunity - Export Markets**: With Project KAL and the Striker FPV proven in Operation Sindoor [5] [1], IG Defence should leverage its $5M war chest to pursue export licenses for allied nations seeking cost-effective, combat-validated autonomous systems.
