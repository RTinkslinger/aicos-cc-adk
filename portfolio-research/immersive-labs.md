# Immersive (formerly Immersive Labs): The Fast-Growing Engine of People-Centric Cyber-Resilience – Opportunities and Risks for an India-Focused Play

## Executive Summary

* **Funding acceleration fuels global expansion:** Immersive has raised approximately $189 million to date, backed by major players like Ten Eleven Ventures, Insight Partners, Summit Partners, and Goldman Sachs [1] [2] [3] [4]. 
* **Leadership renewal to accelerate enterprise sales:** In March 2025, Mark Schmitz (formerly of Collibra, Citrix, and SAP) was appointed CEO to scale the business, while founder James Hadley transitioned to Chief Innovation Officer [5].
* **Readiness-confidence gap exposes a market pain point:** Immersive's 2025 benchmark report revealed that while 94% of organizations believe they are prepared for a cyber incident, actual decision accuracy in simulations is only 22%, with an average containment time of 29 hours [6].
* **Snap Labs acquisition upgrades simulation realism:** The November 2021 acquisition of US-based Snap Labs added critical depth and realism to Immersive's cyber drills [7] [8].
* **Content velocity is a decisive moat:** The platform boasts over 4.3 million completed labs, with enterprise customers like HSBC praising the speed at which Immersive produces technical content for emerging vulnerabilities [1].
* **Enterprise customer base already includes global heavy-hitters:** The company serves over 400 global customers, including Citi, Pfizer, HSBC, the UK Ministry of Defence, and the UK National Health Service [1].
* **India presence is hinted but not quantified:** While Immersive lists India as a strategic partner region, there is a distinct lack of public data regarding its Indian customer base or regional revenue [9].
* **Forrester Wave leadership validates market positioning:** Immersive was named a Leader in The Forrester Wave for Cybersecurity Skills and Training Platforms in 2023 and again in Q1 2026 [1] [10].
* **Potential revenue-visibility risk:** The company operates a B2B enterprise model, but specific Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR), churn rates, and net-new logo metrics remain undisclosed public information gaps.

## 1. Company Overview

Immersive (formerly Immersive Labs) is a British cybersecurity company founded in 2017 by James Hadley [1]. Headquartered in Bristol, United Kingdom, with a US hub in Boston, Massachusetts, the company has grown to over 300 employees across the US, UK, and EMEA [1]. Immersive provides a people-centric cyber resilience platform designed to shift cybersecurity from a compliance exercise to measurable, data-driven readiness [1] [2]. 

## 2. Product & Value Proposition

Immersive One is a continuous cyber resilience platform that offers hands-on cybersecurity training, crisis simulations, and cyber drills for enterprise organizations [1] [11]. The platform evaluates individual and team capabilities against the latest threats, allowing organizations to benchmark their performance and prove compliance with data-backed evidence [2] [11]. 

To date, users have completed over 4.3 million labs on the platform [1]. The value proposition is heavily anchored in exposing and closing the "readiness illusion"—the gap between perceived preparedness and actual capability [6].

| **Real-time threat updates** | Yes (Highly praised for rapid content turnaround) [1] | Yes (Standard industry cadence) [12] |
| **Enterprise-wide benchmarking** | Yes (Immersive Resilience Score) [6] | Yes [12] |
| **Supply-chain security add-on** | Yes (Via 2025 Cycode partnership) [13] | Limited / Varies |
| **Market Recognition** | Forrester Wave Leader (2023, Q1 2026) [1] [10] | Strong market presence [12] |

*Takeaway: Immersive differentiates itself through rapid content deployment and executive-level resilience scoring, moving beyond basic technical training.*

## 3. Founding Team & Current Leadership

Founder James Hadley, who has a background in cyber research (Royal Holloway, University of London), led the company as CEO until early 2025 [14]. In March 2025, Immersive appointed Mark Schmitz as the new CEO [5]. Schmitz brings over 25 years of B2B tech expertise, having held senior leadership roles at Collibra, Citrix Systems, SAP, and Ariba [5]. Hadley transitioned to Chief Innovation Officer to focus on strategic vision and the future of cyber drilling [5].

Other key executives include:

* **Lucian Lui:** Chief Marketing Officer (over 25 years experience at Microsoft, Iron Mountain, Rapid7) [2].

## 5. Market Landscape & Competitive Position

The cyber workforce resilience market is highly competitive, with alternatives including Cyberbit, RangeForce, CloudShare, SimSpace, and Hack The Box [12]. Immersive maintains a strong market position by focusing on "people-centric" resilience rather than just technical upskilling [2]. The company augmented its realism capabilities by acquiring US-based startup Snap Labs in November 2021 [7] [8]. Furthermore, Immersive's position as a Leader in The Forrester Wave (Q1 2026) validates its enterprise-grade benchmarking capabilities [10].

## 6. Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)

Immersive has maintained aggressive product and thought-leadership momentum:
* **March 2026:** Announced major advancements to the Immersive One platform, expanding Secure AI, enterprise security integrations, and framework-aligned reporting [10].
* **November 2025:** Published the "2025 Cyber Workforce Benchmark Report," revealing that while 94% of organizations feel prepared for a major incident, actual decision accuracy is only 22%, and average containment takes 29 hours [6].
* **September 2025:** Announced a strategic partnership with Cycode to elevate software supply chain security by combining Immersive's secure coding training with Cycode's downstream vulnerability data [13].
* **March 2025:** Appointed Mark Schmitz as CEO to usher in a new phase of growth [5].
* **July 2024:** Leveraged the IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report (which noted a surge to $4.88 million per breach) to highlight the financial imperative of human cyber readiness [19].

## 7. Business Model and Traction

Immersive operates a B2B enterprise SaaS model. The company has achieved significant traction, serving over 400 global customers, including major financial and government institutions like Citi, Goldman Sachs, HSBC, Pfizer, Daimler, Humana, and the UK National Health Service [1] [2]. 

**Information Gap:** While user metrics (4.3 million labs completed) and funding totals are public, Immersive does not publicly disclose its Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR), customer churn rates, or specific pricing tiers. 

## 8. India-Specific Context & Opportunities

Immersive is a British company, but it operates globally. On its official partner portal, Immersive explicitly lists India (भारत) as part of its strategic partner network [9]. 

**Information Gap:** There is no public documentation of specific Indian enterprise customers, localized revenue, or dedicated Indian office locations. For an India-focused play, this represents a blank canvas. The Indian cybersecurity market is rapidly maturing due to stringent RBI guidelines on cyber resilience. Immersive's platform is highly applicable to India's massive IT services and banking sectors, but market entry will likely require heavy reliance on local Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) and channel partners.

## 9. Risks & Mitigation

* **Financial Opacity:** The lack of public ARR data makes it difficult to assess the company's current valuation multiples or cash burn rate. 
* **Market Saturation:** The proliferation of lower-cost, open-source cyber ranges could pressure Immersive's enterprise pricing. 
* **Mitigation:** Immersive must continue to lean heavily on its proprietary AI capabilities, its Cycode partnership for DevSecOps [13], and its executive-level reporting metrics to justify premium enterprise pricing.

## 10. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Leverage the 2025 Benchmark Data:** Use the stark 94% confidence vs. 22% accuracy statistic [6] as a primary wedge for enterprise sales pitches, offering "Readiness Audits" to prospective clients.
2. **Activate the APAC Channel:** Capitalize on the existing partner network in India [9] by co-marketing with local system integrators targeting the Indian financial sector.
3. **Demand Financial Transparency:** Before entering into deep strategic partnerships or investments, require non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) to access Immersive's ARR, net retention rates, and APAC-specific growth metrics to validate the financial health behind the $189M+ funding [1].
