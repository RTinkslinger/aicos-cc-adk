# Inmovidu (Movidu) – India’s Low-Funded Ed-Tech Marketplace: Growth Levers, Hidden Risks & What to Do Next

## Executive Summary
Inmovidu (operating interchangeably as Movidu Technology Pvt Ltd) is a Bengaluru-based ed-tech and staffing startup that bridges the gap between academia and industry [1] [2]. Despite having no disclosed external funding, the company has demonstrated notable cash flow, generating ₹4.79 Cr in FY2022 [3]. However, severe operational red flags—including a "deadpooled" classification by data platforms [4] and widespread allegations of deceptive hiring practices [5] —threaten its long-term viability. 

## 1. Company Snapshot – What Inmovidu Does
Founded in 2016 (and rebranded/restructured around 2020), Inmovidu operates a managed marketplace that connects students, mentors, and companies [1] [2] [4]. The company offers its products under the brand "1Stop.ai" and aims to empower 50,000 undergraduate students with practical project experience [1]. 

A core pillar of its current offering is acting as an authorized service provider for Microsoft Certification licenses across India, covering Artificial Intelligence, Data Fundamentals, Cyber Security, and Cloud Computing [6]. Historically, the company also provided online interactive classes for hobbies like arts, music, and dance [4].

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership
The company is led by a trio of founders with backgrounds in corporate strategy and product development:
* **Mridula Chhetri (Co-Founder & CEO):** A former IBM executive who worked in outsourcing and acquisition deals [7]. She is the primary face of the company and has founded multiple entities under the Movidu umbrella [8] [4].
* **Sidharta Viswanath (Co-Founder & CMO):** Leads marketing initiatives [4].
* **Seema Naresh (Co-Founder & CPO):** Serves as the Chief Product Officer [4] [9].

## 5. Recent News, Milestones & Public Signals (2024-2026)
Recent public signals reveal a stark contrast between the company's corporate messaging and its operational reality:
* **Corporate Optimism (Jan 2026):** The company published a LinkedIn update welcoming 2026, citing a focus on "innovation, impact, and creating meaningful value" [2].
* **Employee Backlash (2024-2025):** AmbitionBox reviews from 2024 and 2025 expose severe internal crises. Multiple reviews report that the company engages in "questionable practices regarding college placements" [5]. 

## 6. Business Model, Traction & Key Metrics
Inmovidu operates a B2B2C revenue model. On the B2B side, it partners with educational institutions to distribute Microsoft certifications, requiring an upfront deposit of ₹20,000 from partners [6]. On the B2C side, it monetizes students seeking internships and training [1]. 

Despite claiming 201-500 employees [1] [2], traction metrics are concerning. The company recruits freshers by offering attractive ₹7 LPA packages (₹3L fixed, ₹4L variable), but mandates a 15-day training period where students must achieve 7 to 15 sales conversions [5]. Students failing to hit these aggressive targets are placed on Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs) or terminated without pay, leading to accusations of deceptive hiring [5].

## 7. Strategic Recommendations
To survive and scale, Inmovidu must immediately address its operational liabilities:
1. **Overhaul Hiring Ethics:** The aggressive, conversion-based intern hiring model is destroying the brand's reputation [5]. The company must implement transparent, base-pay guarantees for freshers.
2. **Consolidate Brand Identity:** Operating simultaneously as Inmovidu, Movidu, and 1Stop.ai creates market confusion [1] [2]. Consolidating under a single domain will improve B2B sales efficiency.
3. **Leverage the Microsoft Partnership:** The company should pivot away from controversial B2C student sales and focus entirely on B2B corporate licensing and "Exams from Home" remote proctoring, which offers a cleaner, higher-margin revenue stream [6].
