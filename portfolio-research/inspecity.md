# InspeCity Deep-Tech Playbook: How an IIT-Bombay Startup Is Positioning India to Capture the $14B In-Space Servicing Market

## Executive Summary
InspeCity is an Indian spacetech startup founded in 2022 that is building a vertically integrated platform for In-Space Servicing, Assembly, and Manufacturing (ISAM) [1] [2]. Backed by over $8.6 million in recent 2025 funding, the company is developing technologies to extend satellite lifespans and remove orbital debris [1] [2]. With a target to launch a demonstration mission in 2027, InspeCity is positioning itself as a cost-effective, Make-in-India alternative in a global ISAM market projected to reach $14 billion by 2030 [1] [3]. 

## 1. Company Overview & Value Proposition
Incubated at IIT Bombay, InspeCity focuses on the sustainability of outer space by developing tools that support satellite systems, communication, and navigation [1] [4]. The company's core value proposition lies in its ability to provide a fully vertically integrated platform for satellite life-extension, encompassing propulsion, robotics, and Rendezvous, Proximity Operations, and Docking (RPOD) technologies under one roof [1]. By restoring functionality to failing satellites and deorbiting defunct ones, InspeCity helps operators maximize their return on initial investment and comply with end-of-life regulations [5].

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership
The company is led by a highly technical founding team with deep roots in academia and aerospace engineering:
* **Prof. Arindrajit Chowdhury (CEO & Co-Founder):** A professor at the Department of Mechanical Engineering at IIT Bombay, bringing extensive academic and research credibility to the company's propulsion and deep-tech initiatives [6] [3].
* **Dr. Tausif Shaikh (CTO & Co-Founder):** An IIT Bombay alumnus who leads the technological development of InspeCity's robotics and avionics hardware [4] [7].

## 4. Technology Portfolio & Development Roadmap
InspeCity is developing a proprietary suite of technologies designed to work in unison for orbital servicing [5]. The company's roadmap is divided into phases, with Phase 1 (2023-2025) focused on development and Phase 2 (2026-2028) targeting commercialization [9].

| Product Name | Description | Primary Function |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| **GITA** | Advanced propulsion system | High-efficiency micro-propulsion for orbital maneuvers [1] [10] |
| **RAMA** | Space-grade robotic arm | Ground-controlled manipulation for Orbital Transfer Vehicles [11] [5] |
| **CHAKSU** | Precision sensing suite | Intelligent object identification using LIDAR and EO sensors [11] [5] |
| **VEDA** | Life-extension platform | Central vessel that transports life-extension systems to target satellites [10] [5] |

## 5. Market Landscape & Competitive Position
The global ISAM market is projected to reach approximately $14 billion by 2030 and surpass $14.3 billion by 2031 [10] [3]. Currently, the market is nascent; InspeCity's CEO noted that Northrop Grumman is the only player to have executed a successful life-extension mission to date [3]. InspeCity differentiates itself through its vertically integrated architecture and its reliance on India's innovation-driven frugality, which is expected to yield significantly lower development and build costs compared to Western competitors [1] [3].

## 6. Recent News & Milestones (2024-2026)
InspeCity has maintained strong momentum through strategic partnerships and government defense contracts:
* **May 2023:** Signed the 250th contract under the Innovations for Defence Excellence (iDEX) initiative for micropropulsion systems [12].
* **Nov 2024:** Won two iDEX Prime X Challenges for its space-grade robotic arm and intelligent object identification system [11].
* **Dec 2024:** Signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Japanese startup Orbital Lasers to collaborate on laser-mounted satellite development and laser-based detumbling for debris removal [10].
* **May 2025:** Raised $5.6 million in Seed funding to space-qualify technologies within 12 months [1].
* **Jul 2025:** Jointly published a white paper with Orbital Lasers on integrated space laser and robotics operations [9].

## 7. Business Model, Traction & Information Gaps
**Business Model:** InspeCity operates a B2B and B2G model, planning to generate revenue by selling propulsion systems globally and offering VEDA as a comprehensive life-extension and de-orbiting service [3]. 
**Traction:** The company has secured critical early-stage validation through multiple iDEX defense contracts and international MoUs [1] [10]. InspeCity is currently preparing for a major demonstration mission scheduled for 2027, where it will fly a satellite to space to test its propulsion systems in orbit [3].
**Information Gaps & Risks:** Public information regarding InspeCity's current Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR), specific pricing models for its VEDA services, and exact customer pipeline metrics remains undisclosed. Furthermore, the company's ultra-lean team size (listed as 2-10 employees on LinkedIn) poses a potential scaling risk as it attempts to meet aggressive 2027 flight-qualification deadlines [2].
