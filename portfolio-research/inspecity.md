InspeCity is building the infrastructure for a sustainable orbital economy. Founded in 2022 by IIT Bombay alumni, the company positions itself as a "spacetech" firm rather than a software compliance vendor.

### 1.1 Value Proposition
InspeCity addresses the "use-and-throw" culture of the satellite industry. Currently, when a satellite runs out of fuel or malfunctions, it becomes expensive space junk. InspeCity's **VEDA** platform aims to dock with these satellites to refuel them or adjust their orbit, effectively extending their revenue-generating life. Conversely, for defunct satellites, they offer de-orbiting services to mitigate collision risks [5] [7].

## 4. Technology Stack and Approach

InspeCity's approach relies on **In-space Servicing, Assembly, and Manufacturing (ISAM)** technologies. Their stack is hardware-heavy and requires high-precision autonomous software.

### 4.1 Core Technologies
* **RPOD (Rendezvous, Proximity Operations, and Docking):** The critical ability for one spacecraft to autonomously approach and latch onto another moving object in space. This is the "holy grail" of satellite servicing [4].
* **Robotics:** Development of robotic arms (like the **RIG** system) to manipulate target satellites, perform repairs, or attach propulsion modules [5].
* **Propulsion:** Proprietary propulsion systems designed for the VEDA vehicle to maneuver effectively between orbits [3].
* **Sensors & Navigation:** Usage of LIDAR and Electro-Optical (EO) sensors for navigation. In November 2024, the company announced plans to develop space-grade robotic arms and sensor suites specifically for these missions [4].

### 4.2 Strategic Partnerships
* **Orbital Lasers (Japan):** In December 2024, InspeCity signed an MoU with this Japanese startup. By July 2025, they jointly published a white paper on integrating space lasers with robotics, suggesting a technological fusion where lasers might aid in debris detection or removal alongside InspeCity's robotic arms [4].

## 7. Risks and Red Flags

While the technology is promising, several risks exist:

* **Name/Brand Confusion:** The user's own query highlights a major issue. "Inspecity" sounds like a generic inspection software firm. This confusion could hamper brand recall in a specialized market.
* **Technological Execution:** Autonomous docking is extremely difficult. A single failure could result in a collision, creating *more* debris and potentially ending the company.
* **Capital Intensity:** With ~$7M raised, InspeCity is undercapitalized compared to Astroscale ($384M). They will need significantly more capital to launch demonstration missions.
* **Revenue Lag:** The current revenue (~$70k) is negligible. The company is burning cash on R&D and will rely on investor patience and government grants for the foreseeable future [4].
