# Jumbl India – Untapped Potential in the Paid-Internship & Referral Marketplace

## Executive Summary
Jumbl (legally registered as Kounselo Education Facilities Private Limited) is an early-stage Indian startup operating a dual-sided marketplace for paid internships and tech job referrals [1] [2]. Founded in 2024, the company differentiates itself by strictly listing verified, paid opportunities and utilizing AI to screen candidate profiles [3] [1]. Despite bootstrapping to over 20,000 users and 10,000+ Android app downloads, Jumbl remains unfunded with negligible reported revenue [3] [1] [4]. The platform shows strong product-market fit among Indian college students, but faces operational friction and intense competition from well-funded global referral networks [3] [1].

## 1. Company Snapshot – What Jumbl Does
Jumbl operates a cross-platform service (web and mobile app) designed to connect Indian college students and early-career professionals with growing startups [3] [4]. The platform serves two primary functions:
* **Paid Internship Discovery:** Jumbl strictly prohibits unpaid roles, offering verified stipends for positions in tech, design, marketing, and the founder's office [5] [3]. 
* **Job Referral Marketplace:** The platform allows job seekers to request referrals directly from employees at various tech companies in three steps, eliminating the need for cold outreach [1] [2]. 

The core value proposition for startups is faster hiring without the administrative hassle; Jumbl handles sourcing, AI-based profile screening, and shortlisting to improve interview success rates [5] [1].

Jumbl has actively engaged with the Indian startup and academic ecosystem to build traction:
* **Ecosystem Validation (Late 2024/Early 2025):** CEO Vansh Kathuria showcased Jumbl at the Indian School of Business (ISB) Solstice'25 fest and pitched the platform to Rohit Bansal (Co-founder of Snapdeal and Titan Capital), who validated the vision of streamlining employee referrals [6].

## 6. Business Model & Traction
Jumbl operates a B2B and B2C model, accessible via web and mobile applications [1] [2]. 

* **Traction Metrics:** The platform has achieved over **10,000 downloads** on the Google Play Store with a 4.0-star rating across 111 reviews [3]. CTO Praneel Bora notes the platform has scaled to over **20,000 users** [4].
* **Revenue Model:** While the exact pricing structure is an information gap, the platform likely monetizes by charging startups a fee for successful intern placements or offering premium referral-request tiers, given its B2B/B2C sales approach [2].
