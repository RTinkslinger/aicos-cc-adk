# Kaamwork — Building Sustainable Offshore Teams in India: Opportunity, Traction & Gaps (2024‑2026)

## Executive Summary

Kaamwork is an India-focused Employer of Record (EOR) and talent platform that enables global companies to build offshore teams without establishing a local legal entity [1]. The company claims to offer up to 80% savings on overhead costs compared to managing an owned entity in-country [1]. While operating in the rapidly expanding Indian Global Capability Center (GCC) market, Kaamwork's public financial footprint remains limited to a $500K seed round from 2018 [2]. The company differentiates itself through a hybrid AI-matching and "human touch" model [3], alongside a unique H-1B relocation service [4]. However, significant information gaps regarding current revenue and exact headcount require careful due diligence.

## 1. Company Overview – What Kaamwork Does

Kaamwork operates an AI-enabled EOR platform designed to help global companies build sustainable full-time equivalent (FTE) teams in India [2] [1]. The platform allows clients to specify business objectives or skills in plain English, which the AI then translates into work modules to match with curated talent [5]. By handling payroll, compliance, and HR activities, Kaamwork allows clients to bypass the complex process of setting up a local legal entity [5] [1]. 

## 2. Founding Team & Governance

Founded in 2018 [2], Kaamwork is led by a mix of US and India-based executives. 

* **CEO & Founder:** Nilesh Parwani, an ex-finance professional for whom Kaamwork is a second venture following his first startup, Printbell [3] [5].
* **Co-Founders:** Abhay Mishra, Byron Fuller, and Prateek Mishra [6] [5].
* **Key Executives:** Sunny Parwani (Business Development) [7] and Deepali Gupta [1].

## 5. Competitive Positioning & Differentiators

Kaamwork faces competition from heavily funded global EOR platforms like Deel and Multiplier [5]. 

| Competitor | Core Offering | Kaamwork's Unique Differentiation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Deel / Multiplier | Global EOR and Payroll across 100+ countries | Deep India focus with localized "human HR touch" [3] [5] |
| Traditional Staffing | Outsourced project work | Dedicated FTE model where talent works on one engagement at a time [5] |
| US Immigration Counsel | Visa processing | Turnkey "H-1B to India" relocation service maintaining US roles [4] |

*Takeaway:* Kaamwork's dedicated H-1B relocation service allows US employers to retain talent facing visa issues by relocating them to India while Kaamwork handles local compliance and payroll [4].

## 6. Traction & Customer Portfolio

Kaamwork has secured notable enterprise clients, proving its ability to source high-tier talent. The company cites successes with prominent brands like TripAdvisor, Amazon, and Google [1]. A specific case study highlights how Tripadvisor utilized Kaamwork to tap into a global talent pool, resulting in faster and more cost-effective hiring [12] [13]. 

## 7. Business Model & Revenue Signals

Kaamwork utilizes a subscription-based revenue model. Pricing is structured per resource per month, which is akin to full-time salaries but offers flexibility regarding the duration of the engagement [5]. The company does not publicly disclose its Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) or current revenue figures [2].

## 8. Recent News & Milestones (2024‑2026)

Recent public activity heavily emphasizes their specialized relocation services. In 2024-2025, Kaamwork actively promoted its H-1B alternative approach, positioning itself as the only India-focused EOR that allows H-1B employees to relocate to India and stay in their current roles fully compliant [4] [14]. 

## 9. Risk Assessment & Information Gaps

Several critical information gaps must be explicitly flagged:
* **Headcount Discrepancies:** PitchBook reports 22 employees [2], LinkedIn shows 21 [1], while The Company Check reports 104 employees as of March 2024 [15].
* **Financial Opacity:** There is zero public visibility into the company's revenue, profitability, or funding post-2018 [2].
* **Employee Sentiment:** Some Glassdoor reviews from individuals claiming to work on the TripAdvisor account cite poor management and lack of career growth [16] [17].

## 10. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Conduct Deep Financial Due Diligence:** Given the lack of funding data since 2018 [2], any potential partner or investor must request current ARR, unit economics, and cap table details.
2. **Validate Cost Savings:** Request empirical data to substantiate the claimed 80% overhead savings [1].
3. **Leverage the H-1B Niche:** The H-1B relocation service is a strong wedge offering [4]. Kaamwork should aggressively market this to US tech firms facing immigration bottlenecks.
