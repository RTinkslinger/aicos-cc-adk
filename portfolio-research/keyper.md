# Keyper — UAE's Rent-Now-Pay-Later PropTech Power-Play (2022-2026)

## Executive Summary
Keyper is a Dubai-based proptech startup that has rapidly scaled to become a dominant force in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) real estate market. *Note: While the research request targets Indian startups, Keyper is headquartered in the UAE and operates within the Middle Eastern market context.* The company has raised over $40 million to digitize property management and pioneer a "Rent-Now-Pay-Later" (RNPL) model [1]. By securing strategic corporate investments from major regional players like Property Finder and Ellington Properties, Keyper is well-positioned to capitalize on the UAE's shift toward monthly rental payments [2] [3].

## 1. Company Overview & Value Proposition
Founded in 2022, Keyper provides an end-to-end digital real estate investment and property management platform [4] [1]. The company solves a major friction point in the UAE rental market—where annual rent is traditionally paid in one to four upfront cheques—by allowing tenants to pay in 12 monthly installments via credit or debit card [5] [6]. 

Simultaneously, Keyper offers an "Upfront Rent Service" that allows landlords to receive their annual rent in a single upfront cash-out [1] [7]. For property investors, the platform provides real-time portfolio monitoring, data-driven valuation insights, and on-demand property management services [7].

## 2. Founding Team
Keyper's leadership brings deep financial and investment expertise, reducing reliance on external financial advisors:
* **Omar Abu Innab (Co-Founder & CEO):** Brings 20 years of experience as an investment researcher and real estate investor, previously holding roles at the Government Investments Management Company (GIMC) [8] [9].
* **Walid Shihabi (Co-Founder & CSO):** An investment banking veteran with over 15 years of experience, including serving as the CEO of SHUAA Securities and Chief Commercial Officer at Urban Real Estate [10] [11].

## 3. Funding History
Keyper has secured over $40 million in cumulative capital through a hybrid equity-debt model, utilizing Shariah-compliant financing to fund its upfront rent cash-outs [1].

## 4. Market Position & Competition
The UAE rental market is undergoing a structural shift toward monthly payments, supported by Dubai's D33 digital transformation agenda [6]. Keyper competes directly with other RNPL platforms like Rently and Ezy [13]. 

Keyper differentiates itself through its integrated property management software, its Shariah-compliant landlord cash-out capabilities, and its exclusive integrations with top-tier developers and listing portals [7] [2]. 

## 5. Recent News & Milestones (2024-2026)
* **Property Finder Integration (Nov 2025):** Keyper announced a strategic partnership and investment from Property Finder. Keyper's RNPL functionality will be integrated directly into the Property Finder platform, going live in the first half of 2026 [6] [3].
* **Ellington Properties Partnership (Jul 2025):** Luxury developer Ellington Properties made a strategic investment in Keyper to offer its buyers real-time portfolio insights and digital rent collection [2].
* **Industry Recognition:** CEO Omar Abu Innab was recognized as PropTech of the Year at the 2025 Property Finder Awards, and the company was named a Future 100 winner by the UAE government [14] [2].

## 6. Business Model, Traction & Information Gaps
Keyper monetizes through transaction fees on rent payments, property management service fees, and spreads on its upfront rent cash-out financing [7] [13]. 

**Key Traction Metrics:**
* **User Base:** Over 100,000 app downloads [2].
* **Transaction Volume:** Powering AED 100 million in annual rental payments as of mid-2025 [2].
* **Portfolio:** Onboarded over 2,100 residential units valued at >$1.22 billion, trusted by 800+ landlords (as of late 2023) [4].

**Information Gaps:** 
While user adoption metrics are publicized, there is limited public information regarding Keyper's exact revenue figures, customer acquisition costs (CAC), churn rates, and profitability margins. The exact equity stakes taken by Property Finder and Ellington Properties also remain undisclosed.
