# Material Depot: Scaling India's Omnichannel Home-Materials Marketplace

## Executive Summary
Material Depot is a rapidly growing Indian startup transforming the fragmented $14 billion home interior materials market through a design-centric, omnichannel approach [1]. By bridging the gap between digital discovery and physical material selection, the company empowers both end-consumers and industry professionals. Bolstered by a recent $10 million Series A funding round in February 2026, Material Depot is aggressively expanding its physical footprint from its current base in Bengaluru to new markets like Hyderabad [2] [1]. The company's unique positioning—focusing strictly on material curation rather than full-stack interior design—provides a highly scalable model in a low-trust, supplier-led industry [1].

## Product and Service Offering
Material Depot operates as a comprehensive digital-first, one-stop shop for construction, interior design, and home decor materials [3]. 

### Core Catalogue and AI Discovery
The platform's core catalogue includes tiles, decorative laminates, wallpapers, wall panels, quartz, and wooden flooring [1]. To differentiate itself, Material Depot functions as a SaaS-enabled marketplace, offering a proprietary "Discovery Engine and AI Design Tools" [4]. This technology allows users to navigate global design trends, visualize materials in their spaces, and make assisted decisions before committing to purchases. 

### Experiential Centres
Complementing its digital platform, Material Depot utilizes physical experience centres where customers can touch, feel, and compare materials [1]. This omnichannel strategy addresses the high-stakes nature of home building, ensuring customers have physical confidence in their digital selections.

## Founding Team
Material Depot was founded by Manish Reddy and Sarthak Agrawal, officially launching and participating in the Y Combinator Winter 2022 batch [5]. 
* **Manish Reddy:** Brings strategic and industry-specific expertise, having previously worked at Boston Consulting Group (BCG) in India and the USA, and possessing deep ties to his family's 30-year-old electrical contracting business [5].
* **Sarthak Agrawal:** An alumnus of IIT-Bombay, Agrawal drives the technological and product vision of the platform [5] [6]. 
The company currently maintains a team of approximately 50 employees [5].

## Business Model
Material Depot operates a B2B2C omnichannel marketplace model. Unlike larger competitors such as Livspace or HomeLane, which provide end-to-end design and build services, Material Depot focuses purely on the curation, pricing, and supply of materials [1]. 

The customer base is strategically split:
* **End Consumers (60-70%):** Homeowners who are increasingly design-aware due to platforms like Pinterest and Instagram, seeking direct control over their material finishes [1].
* **Professionals (30-40%):** Architects, interior designers, and contractors who utilize the platform for reliable sourcing and client presentations [1].

## Market Position
Material Depot targets an estimated $14 billion interior materials market in India [1]. The market has historically been highly fragmented, supplier-led, and characterized by low consumer trust [1]. By anchoring its category in design intelligence and supply chain control, Material Depot is carving out a distinct moat [1]. It currently serves around 15,000 customers, with aggressive targets to scale this to over 50,000 [1].

## Recent News
In February 2026, Material Depot made headlines by securing its $10 million Series A funding [1]. The company announced that the fresh capital will be deployed to strengthen its technology stack—specifically across supply chain management, inventory planning, and assisted in-store selling [1]. Furthermore, the company revealed plans to scale its offline presence dramatically, aiming to expand from its three existing experience centres in Bengaluru to over 30 centres across multiple cities, including Hyderabad, over the next 12 to 18 months [2] [1].
