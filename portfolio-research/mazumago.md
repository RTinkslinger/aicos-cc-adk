# From MazumaGo to Truss: Verticalizing Fintech to Solve the Construction Payment Crisis

## Executive Summary
MazumaGo has undergone a radical transformation from a general Canadian B2B payment processor into **Truss**, a specialized US-based neobank for the construction industry [1] [2]. By identifying that construction businesses face an average 83-day payment delay due to archaic paper check reliance, the company pivoted to offer instant, no-limit digital payments [2]. To bypass restrictive Canadian banking regulations, the founders sold their Canadian operations to Baseline Payments in 2022 and relocated to the US [1]. Today, Truss operates as a vertical-specific financial layer backed by Thread Bank, offering a blueprint for how fintechs can dominate niche industries [2]. 

## I. Corporate Evolution and Product Proposition
Founded in Victoria, British Columbia in 2018, the company originally launched as DivDot before rebranding to MazumaGo to facilitate flat-fee, no-limit business payments [3] [1] [4]. However, CEO Matthew Smith found the Canadian financial system "extremely challenging and restrictive" [1]. In November 2022, Vancouver's Baseline Payments acquired MazumaGo's Canadian operations, allowing the founding team to focus entirely on the more open US market [1]. 

On March 13, 2023, the US entity officially rebranded as **Truss** [2]. Truss provides a comprehensive banking and payment platform built exclusively for construction [2]. The platform allows contractors to collect payments online via ACH and cards, pay subcontractors in minutes, and issue unlimited corporate charge cards [2]. Through a Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS) partnership with Thread Bank, Truss provides users with up to $3,000,000 in FDIC insurance coverage and eliminates account fees and minimums [2].

## III. Market Position and Competitive Landscape
Truss targets a massive inefficiency in the construction sector, where contractors wait an average of 83 days to get paid [2]. By replacing hand-delivered checks and hidden wire fees with instant digital rails, Truss differentiates itself from traditional horizontal banks [2]. 

| Feature | Traditional Banks | Truss (formerly MazumaGo) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Transfer Limits** | Often capped daily/monthly | No-limit payments [8] |
| **Payment Speed** | 3-5 Business Days | Instant access for vendors/clients [2] |
| **Industry Focus** | Horizontal/General | 100% Construction [2] |
| **Fee Structure** | Hidden/Wire Fees | Free ACH and card payments [2] |

*Takeaway: Truss wins by removing the specific friction points (transfer limits and speed) that plague high-value construction projects.*

### Indian Market Context
While Truss operates in North America, its trajectory offers a vital strategic blueprint for Indian fintechs. India's booming real estate and infrastructure sectors suffer from similar fragmented supply chains and delayed subcontractor payouts. Indian startups like Walrus (a YC S21 peer targeting youth banking) [7] or enterprise-focused neobanks could replicate the Truss model by building vertical-specific ledgers that integrate directly with Indian construction workflows and the UPI framework.

## IV. Business Model and Recent Traction (2024-2026)
Truss monetizes primarily through interchange fees generated by the Truss Visa debit and charge cards, while offering core ACH payment collection for free to drive user acquisition [2]. The company has also introduced "Truss Card Rewards" to incentivize high-volume contractor spending [2]. Early marketing efforts by agency One Net yielded a 400% increase in conversion rates and a 338% increase in click-through rates [3].
