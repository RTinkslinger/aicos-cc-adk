# Measure AI (Singapore) – Gas-Sensing Startup Turning Clean-Tech Grants into Market-Ready Solutions

## Executive Summary

Measure AI is a high-potential deep-tech startup that has rapidly translated academic research into a versatile gas-sensing platform. While the initial research request targeted Indian startups, Measure AI is based in Singapore, operating as a spin-off from the National University of Singapore (NUS) [1]. The company has developed a proprietary sensor chip capable of detecting and quantifying hundreds of gases simultaneously in real time [2] [1]. Backed by early-stage ecosystem grants and equity investments, Measure AI has secured significant validation in the maritime and clean-tech sectors, including winning the PIER71 Smart Port Challenge [3]. For stakeholders looking at the Indian market, Measure AI represents a prime candidate for cross-border technology licensing or joint ventures in industrial safety and port emissions monitoring.

## Company Overview

Incorporated in March 2023, Measure AI operates as a privately held entity (Pte Ltd) headquartered in Singapore [2] [1]. The company currently maintains a lean operational footprint with an estimated 2 to 10 employees [2]. It is officially categorized under the Measuring and Control Instrument Manufacturing industry, with a strong thematic focus on Maritime Green Technologies and CleanTech [2] [1]. 

## Founding Team & Expertise

The company's technological foundation is driven by its founder, Dr. Gokula Krishnan Ramachandran [2]. 
* **Academic Pedigree:** Dr. Ramachandran holds a PhD from the National University of Singapore and a Bachelor's degree in Biotechnology from Tamil Nadu Agricultural University [4] [5].
* **Intellectual Property:** He brings deep technical expertise to the venture, holding 2 patents and having authored 8 peer-reviewed publications [5].
* **Role:** He serves as the Founder, Data Scientist, and Entrepreneur, guiding both the product development and the commercialization strategy [2].

## Product & Technology

Measure AI's core differentiation lies in its novel, low-cost, and highly sensitive real-time gas sensors [1]. 
* **Multi-Gas Detection:** Unlike traditional sensors that are highly specific to a single gas, Measure AI utilizes a patented method where sensors are non-specific. The platform identifies and quantifies a vast spectrum of gases and gas mixtures based on unique changes in the electrical properties of a sensor array upon exposure [1].
* **Hardware Efficiency:** This allows the system to detect hundreds of volatile compounds simultaneously using a single sensor chip [2] [1].
* **Key Applications:** The technology is currently being applied to flavour and fragrance quality assessment, hazardous gas detection, indoor air quality monitoring, and fitness/safety systems [2].

## Market Landscape & Position

Measure AI is positioning itself at the intersection of industrial safety, environmental monitoring, and quality control. 
* **Maritime & Clean-Tech:** The company is actively targeting the maritime sector, focusing on the safe adoption of low-emission marine fuels and green port technologies [2] [1].
* **Food & Fragrance:** They have successfully market-validated their solution for quality assessment with flavour and fragrance manufacturers [1].
* **Healthcare:** The sensors are also positioned for real-time monitoring of critical hospital environments [1].

## Business Model

While specific pricing tiers are not publicly disclosed, Measure AI operates on a B2B commercialization model. The company secures Letters of Intent (LOIs) through targeted pilot programs. For example, they have secured an LOI for the grading of Manuka Honey based on Methyl Glyoxal (MGO) levels [1]. The business model relies on deploying hardware sensor arrays to enterprise clients (such as port authorities, hospitals, and fragrance labs) and providing the accompanying data analytics required to interpret the complex gas mixtures [2] [1].

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Bridge the Geographic Gap:** Since the user interest lies in Indian startups, Measure AI should explore strategic partnerships with Indian port authorities or manufacturing hubs (e.g., Gujarat or Tamil Nadu) to pilot their hazardous gas detection systems, leveraging the founder's educational ties to Tamil Nadu [4].
2. **Accelerate Commercialization:** Transition from ecosystem awards (Slingshot, Shell) to disclosed commercial contracts. Converting the existing LOIs (like the Manuka honey grading) into recurring revenue is critical for the next funding cycle [2] [1].
3. **Expand Commercial Leadership:** With a highly technical founder and a small team (2-10 employees), Measure AI should prioritize hiring dedicated business development leadership to scale pilot programs across its diverse target verticals (maritime, healthcare, and food/fragrance) [2].
