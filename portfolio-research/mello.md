# Mello – India’s Vernacular AI Companion Play-to-Earn the Emotional-Support Market

## Executive Summary
Mello, the flagship product of Bengaluru-based startup Companion Labs, is an AI-powered companion application designed specifically for India’s vernacular internet users [1]. Founded by former executives from Flipkart and CRED, the platform allows users to interact with culturally localized AI bots for emotional support, entertainment, and daily conversation [1]. Having recently secured a $2.5 million seed round led by Peak XV’s Surge in February 2026, Mello is rapidly positioning itself as a pioneer in India's nascent AI-native entertainment category, targeting the massive demographic of Tier II, III, and IV cities [1]. 

## Product and Service
Mello operates as a personal world of virtual AI friends, offering users a judgment-free space for conversation, life advice, and entertainment [2]. The application features 12 unique AI bots, each with distinct Indian personas such as a "Comedian," "Tech Expert," "Astrologer," and "Boyfriend" [2]. 

A key differentiator for Mello is its deep vernacular integration. The bots are capable of conversing in seven Indian languages: English, Tamil, Telugu, Gujarati, Punjabi, Marathi, and Bengali [1]. The platform emphasizes privacy, ensuring that all chats remain on the user's device with end-to-end encryption [2]. 

## Founding Team
Companion Labs, the parent company of Mello, was founded in early 2025 by a team with strong consumer tech pedigrees [1] [3]. 
* **Ajit Pol:** Co-founder and former executive at Flipkart and Lead Scholar [1]. 
* **Akshay Jhanwar:** Co-founder and former executive at the fintech unicorn CRED [1]. 

Their combined experience in scaling consumer internet products in India provides a strong foundation for Mello's go-to-market strategy, though the team is currently actively hiring to bolster its core AI, engineering, and content capabilities [4].

## Market Position
Mello is carving out a niche at the intersection of generative AI and digital entertainment. While global players like OpenAI dominate the general-purpose LLM market, Mello specifically targets Gen Z users in India's Tier II-IV cities who are seeking emotional connection and companionship in their native languages [1]. A recent survey highlighted that 83% of Gen Z respondents believe they could develop meaningful connections with chatbots, validating Mello's core thesis [1]. 

*Note: Mello shares its brand name with another Bengaluru-based startup (mello.fun, an event discovery platform founded in 2022), which may necessitate future trademark navigation [5] [6].*

## Business Model
Mello operates on a freemium consumer application model. While the app is free to download on the Google Play Store, it monetizes through in-app purchases and subscriptions [2]. Users can likely access basic conversational features for free, with premium personas, advanced scenarios, or extended chat limits gated behind subscription tiers.
