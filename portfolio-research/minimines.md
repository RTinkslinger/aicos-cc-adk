# MiniMines Cleantech: India's Fast-Growing Lithium-Ion Recycling Powerhouse – Funding, Expansion & Market Outlook (2024-26)

## Executive Summary

MiniMines Cleantech Solutions is rapidly scaling from a specialized clean-technology startup into a foundational player in India's critical minerals ecosystem. Founded in 2020 and headquartered in Bengaluru, the company leverages its proprietary Hybrid-Hydrometallurgy (HHM)™ process to recycle end-of-life lithium-ion batteries into battery-grade materials [1] [2]. Backed by substantial venture capital and government grants, MiniMines is currently executing an aggressive expansion strategy, highlighted by a recent ₹350 crore Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the Karnataka government to build a Giga Critical Minerals Refining Complex [3] [4]. With a target to reach ₹1,500 crore in annual revenue by 2028, the company is positioned to capture a significant share of India's domestic lithium demand [3].

## Product & Technology

### Proprietary Hybrid-Hydrometallurgy (HHM)™ Process
At the core of MiniMines' operations is its patented Hybrid-Hydrometallurgy™ (HHM) process, a zero-discharge, sustainable technology designed to extract battery-grade salts of lithium, cobalt, nickel, and manganese from spent batteries and manufacturing scrap [1] [5]. The process is agnostic to various lithium-ion battery chemistries and form factors, ensuring high separation efficiency with recovery rates of up to 99 percent [5]. 

### High-Purity Output and R&D Expansion
The HHM process yields metal salts that are re-crystallized to 99.9% purity, making them suitable for high-specification sectors like battery manufacturing and pharmaceuticals [3]. Beyond lithium-ion batteries, MiniMines is expanding its R&D footprint to include the HydroMag REE Loop technology for recovering rare earth elements (REEs) from permanent magnets, as well as extracting platinum group metals (PGMs) from catalytic converters [3] [4].

## Founding Team & Talent

MiniMines is led by Co-Founder and CEO Anupam Kumar, alongside Co-Founder and CTO Arvind Bhardwaj [3]. Under their leadership, the company has grown to a team of 56 employees as of early 2026, following a major hiring initiative announced for the 2024-2025 period to support its rapid operational scaling [2].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

MiniMines is ranked 4th among 18 active competitors in the Indian market, which includes players like Attero [2]. A key competitive differentiator is its regulatory standing: MiniMines is the first CPCB-certified R4 battery recycler in South India [4]. While many smaller competitors only crush batteries to extract intermediate 'black mass', MiniMines performs the full refining cycle to produce end-use ready materials [3]. By 2028, the company aims to expand its capacity to 45,000 metric tons per annum, which CEO Anupam Kumar estimates could solve 25 to 30% of India's domestic lithium demand [3].

MiniMines operates a diversified B2B business model centered on the circular economy:

| Revenue Stream | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| Battery Grade Materials Supply | Selling 99.9% pure recovered lithium, cobalt, and nickel back into the supply chain for cell manufacturing [8] [3]. |
