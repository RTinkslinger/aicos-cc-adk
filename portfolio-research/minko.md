# Minko – India’s Niche B2B BNPL Play: Funding Trail, Market Fit & Post-Acquisition Outlook (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary
Minko (Minko Platforms Private Limited) emerged as a targeted financial technology solution designed to digitize payments and provide embedded supply chain financing for India's offline retailers and FMCG distributors [1] [2]. Despite successfully identifying a massive $250 billion unmet credit gap in the Indian offline retail sector [3] [4], the company struggled to scale against heavily funded competitors. This operational strain culminated in a 91% headcount reduction by mid-2023 [2], followed by the undisclosed acquisition of its core supply chain financing product in March 2024 [5] [6]. 

## 1 Company Snapshot & Value Proposition

Headquartered in Sanquelim, Goa, Minko operates as a point-of-sale and B2B Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) financing provider [7] [2]. The company's core value proposition centers on moving "retailer-to-distributor" payments online—a segment traditionally dominated by cash and cheques [4].

*Takeaway: Minko's financial footprint remains highly localized and modest, with FY24 annual revenues of just ₹72.8L highlighting a struggle to monetize its product suite effectively before its 2024 asset sale.*

## 2 Founding Team & Leadership

Minko was founded in 2017 by Sanket Shendure (CEO), Sanmati Shendure, and Rohit Pratap Singh [2]. The founding team brought significant domain expertise to the table, with Sanket and Sanmati hailing from a family of entrepreneurs possessing over 15 years of experience in FMCG product distribution [4]. This background directly informed Minko's focus on the friction points between FMCG distributors and kirana store owners.

## 5 Business Model & Traction

Minko's business model relies on providing short-term credit lines to retailers, generating revenue through interest margins and B2B payment stack integration [3] [4]. 
* **Customer Base:** By late 2021, Minko claimed to have onboarded over 10,000 retailers and distributors [3].
* **Geographic Focus:** Operations were heavily concentrated in Goa, Maharashtra, and Bihar, with stated ambitions to expand to other states [3] [8]. 
* **Information Gap:** Current active user metrics post-2022 are unavailable, and the exact revenue breakdown between its credit, invoice, and QR products remains undisclosed.

## 6 Recent News & Milestones (2024-2026)

The 2023-2024 period marked a definitive structural pivot and functional exit for Minko's core operations:
* **Headcount Collapse:** By July 2023, Minko's employee count had plummeted by 91% year-over-year to just 4 employees, signaling severe operational distress or preparation for an asset sale [2].
* **Product Acquisition (March 2024):** Minko's flagship Supply Chain Financing Product was officially acquired by a "prominent fintech company operating in a similar domain in India" [5] [6]. The financial terms and the acquiring entity remain undisclosed due to a strict Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) [5].
* **Founder Departure (June 2024):** Following the product acquisition, Co-Founder and CEO Sanket Shendure officially transitioned to Niyogin Fintech Limited, taking on the role of Chief Product and Growth Officer to spearhead their embedded lending and co-lending business [9] [10]. 

**Strategic Recommendation:** For market analysts and potential partners, Minko should currently be viewed as a legacy shell entity. The true value of its merchant network and proprietary B2B payment stack has already been absorbed by the undisclosed 2024 acquirer. Competitors should monitor Niyogin Fintech's upcoming product rollouts, as Shendure's transition will likely inject Minko's FMCG supply-chain playbook directly into Niyogin's ecosystem.
