# MomsMade: India's New-Age Haldiram

## Executive Summary

MomsMade (legally registered as Singhashani Nextgen Private Limited) is a Bengaluru-based food and beverage startup founded in 2024 that aims to become India's "New-Age Haldiram" [1] [2] [3]. Specialising in traditional North Indian snacks like Thekua and Namak Pare, the company leverages a clean-label, preservative-free approach to capture the growing health-conscious consumer market [4] [3]. Having recently secured a $244K seed funding round led by prominent venture capital firms and angel investors, MomsMade is rapidly scaling its direct-to-consumer (D2C) operations across India [5] [3]. 

## Product and Service

MomsMade manufactures and delivers authentic, homemade-style traditional Indian snacks [2] [3]. The company's flagship products include variations of Thekua and Namak Pare, crafted with premium ingredients such as whole wheat flour (aata) and jaggery [3]. 

A core differentiator for the brand is its strict adherence to clean-label manufacturing. The company explicitly markets its products as being 100% free from preservatives, Dalda, Maida, and Palm Oil [4]. By offering combo packs and nationwide delivery, MomsMade successfully bridges the gap between traditional regional flavours and modern, health-conscious snacking preferences [4] [3].

## Founding Team

The startup was founded in 2024 by Sameer Kumar and Veena Kumari [1]. Operating out of Bengaluru, Karnataka, the founders have maintained significant control over the company's direction. As of the latest cap table data from June 2025, the founders hold a commanding 61.91% equity stake in the business, reflecting a net worth of approximately INR 6.26 Crore [5]. The core team remains lean, with an estimated company size of 2-10 employees [2].

## Business Model

MomsMade operates primarily as a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) e-commerce brand. The company manufactures its snacks and sells them directly through its proprietary website (momsmade.shop) and social media channels [4] [3]. By cutting out traditional FMCG middlemen, the company can maintain strict quality control over its preservative-free claims while capturing higher profit margins. Customer acquisition is heavily driven by organic social media marketing, particularly through Instagram reels showcasing the authentic, homemade nature of their production [6] [4].

## Recent News

Recent reports indicate that the company is in advanced talks with Fireside Ventures to secure at least Rs 40 crore in a new funding round [7]. If successful, this capital injection will likely be used to scale manufacturing capabilities, expand the product portfolio beyond Thekua and Namak Pare, and establish a stronger omnichannel presence to truly compete with legacy giants like Haldiram's.
