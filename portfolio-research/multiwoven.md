# Multiwoven — India’s Open-Source Reverse-ETL Playbook After Its AI Squared Acquisition

## Executive Summary

Multiwoven is a Bengaluru-based technology startup that developed an open-source Reverse ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) platform designed to synchronize customer data from data warehouses directly into business applications [1]. Founded in 2023 by a team of seasoned Indian product engineers, the company quickly positioned itself as a cost-effective, open-source alternative to proprietary data integration tools [2] [1]. In a rapid strategic exit, Multiwoven was acquired by Washington, D.C.-based AI Squared in May 2024, less than a year after its inception and following a $1.1 million pre-seed funding round [3] [4]. This acquisition highlights a growing trend of US enterprise AI firms acquiring Indian data infrastructure startups to accelerate their data movement and AI activation capabilities [3] [1].

## Product and Service

Multiwoven operates as an open-source Reverse ETL and data activation platform [2]. The core problem it solves is breaking down data silos by moving organized data from central data warehouses into the operational SaaS platforms used by sales, marketing, and advertising teams [5] [3]. 

Initially conceived as a product notification platform, the team pivoted after realizing that data silos were a universal pain point across all company departments [5]. The platform is licensed under the AGPLv3 License and can be deployed locally using Docker Compose [2]. It features a rapidly growing ecosystem of connectors, supporting destinations such as Klaviyo, Braze, Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Slack, Google Sheets, and Airtable [2]. 

## Founding Team

The founding team brings deep experience in managing population-scale data from prominent Indian and global tech companies, including Affle, InMobi, Razorpay, and Truecaller [1]. Following the acquisition by AI Squared, the founders transitioned into key global leadership roles:

* **Sujoy Golan:** Co-founder and CEO of Multiwoven, who previously worked at InMobi, transitioned to the role of Chief Product Officer at AI Squared [3] [1].
* **Nagendra Dhanakeerthi:** Co-founder and CTO, with prior experience as a Senior Engineer at Razorpay, assumed the role of Chief Technology Officer at AI Squared [1] [6].
* **Subin T P:** Co-founder who previously owned product integration in a $100M+ acquisition and worked at Truecaller, took on the role of Senior VP of Engineering at AI Squared [4] [1].

## Market Position

Multiwoven positioned itself as a direct open-source alternative to heavily funded, proprietary reverse ETL competitors like Hightouch and Census [2]. By offering a free-to-use, community-driven model, Multiwoven appealed to data teams—engineers, analysts, and operations professionals—who required secure, self-hosted data segmentation and synchronization without prohibitive enterprise licensing costs [9] [10]. 

**Competitive Landscape Matrix**

*Takeaway:* Multiwoven's open-source strategy allowed it to bypass the traditional enterprise sales cycle, driving grassroots adoption among developers who needed immediate, low-cost data activation.

## Business Model

Prior to the acquisition, Multiwoven's business model centered on building a robust open-source community to drive adoption, with the platform remaining free to use [3] [10]. Post-acquisition, the business model shifted toward enterprise integration. AI Squared is incorporating Multiwoven's reverse ETL capabilities into its existing enterprise offering to help organizations efficiently integrate AI insights into business applications [3]. AI Squared has committed to keeping the core Multiwoven technology open-source and free, utilizing it as a foundational layer to introduce advanced, monetizable capabilities for activating AI and machine learning data [3] [9].

## Recent News

The most significant recent event for Multiwoven was its acquisition by AI Squared, officially announced on May 9, 2024 [3]. The acquisition occurred less than two weeks after AI Squared secured a nearly $14 million Series A funding round led by ANSA Capital and NEA [3]. AI Squared's CEO, Benjamin Harvey, emphasized that the acquisition was driven by a recognition of the critical role the open-source community plays in fueling innovation, noting that the combined entity will continue to lead in open-source rETL while bringing critical data-movement functionality to enterprise customers [3] [9].
