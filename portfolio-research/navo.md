# Navo – AI-Powered B2B Fashion Wholesale for Tier-2 & Tier-3 India

## Executive Summary

Navo (formerly known as BizUp) is an AI-enabled B2B fashion wholesale marketplace that is digitizing the apparel supply chain for Tier-2 and Tier-3 Indian retailers [1] [2]. Founded in 2020 and headquartered in New Delhi, the company leverages a proprietary artificial intelligence engine to predict fashion trends by analyzing social media content, allowing small-town retailers to source trendy apparel with unprecedented speed [1] [2]. Backed by a recent Rs 8 crore seed round led by IndiaQuotient, Navo has rapidly scaled its network to 50,000 retailers across 250 districts, achieving a strong 50% repeat purchase rate within 30 days [2]. By combining AI-driven trend spotting with a zero-risk 30-day free exchange policy, Navo is well-positioned to capture a significant share of India's $100 billion fashion wholesale market [2] [3].

## Company Overview & Founding Team

Navo, the registered brand name of Soaring Owl Technologies Private Limited, was founded in 2020 to bridge the gap between viral fashion trends and local retail availability in "Bharat" (non-metro India) [1] [3]. The company is led by co-founders Suparn Goel, an alumnus of IIM Ahmedabad, and Anshul Singhal, an alumnus of the Indian School of Business (ISB) [2] [4] [5]. Their combined expertise in digitally native operations and consulting has driven Navo's product-first approach to solving traditional supply chain bottlenecks [4]. The company currently operates as a private entity with approximately 25 employees [1].

## Product & Technology

Navo's core differentiator is its AI-driven trend prediction engine. The platform analyzes over 10,000 YouTube and Instagram videos, alongside more than 500,000 images each month, to identify emerging fashion trends before they reach the mass market [2]. 

Key product features include:
* **Rapid Catalog Expansion:** The platform curates and adds over 2,000 new designs weekly (approximately 5,000 per month) across categories like sarees, suits, jeans, and t-shirts [2] [3].
* **Interactive Buying Experience:** To mimic the familiarity of offline wholesale markets, Navo integrates live video shopping, reels-based discovery, and agent-assisted calls [2] [3].
* **Risk-Free Purchasing:** Navo offers a 100% sale guarantee with a 30-day, no-questions-asked free exchange policy, significantly lowering the inventory risk for small retailers [3].

## Funding History & Recent News

In September 2025, Navo successfully raised Rs 8 crore (recorded as a $1.51M later-stage VC deal in some databases) in a seed funding round [1] [2]. The round was led by IndiaQuotient, with participation from Antler, DeVC, Bharat Founders Fund, OTP Venture, SAB Holdings, and several angel investors [1] [2]. 

Recent news highlights the company's strategic rebrand from BizUp to Navo, aligning with its mission to bring "newness" to the market [6] [3]. The fresh capital is earmarked for scaling retailer acquisition, enhancing the AI trend engine, and optimizing supply chain efficiencies [2].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

The Indian fashion wholesale sector is an Rs 8 lakh crore ($100B+) market ripe for digital disruption [2]. Traditional retailers in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities often wait 3-4 months for new designs to reach their stores, putting them at a disadvantage against fast-fashion giants [3]. Navo compresses this timeline to 7-10 days [2].

## Business Model & Strategic Recommendations

Navo operates a B2B marketplace model, connecting manufacturers directly with retailers and cutting out unnecessary middlemen markups [1]. While exact take-rates are undisclosed, the platform monetizes through wholesale margins and transaction facilitation, offering Cash on Delivery (COD) and doorstep delivery [3]. 

**Strategic Recommendations:**
1. **Scale Logistics Infrastructure:** To maintain the 7-10 day delivery promise and support the 30-day free exchange policy, Navo must invest heavily in reverse logistics and regional micro-fulfillment centers.
2. **Monitor Retailer Churn:** While the 50% repeat purchase rate is strong, the company should implement loyalty tiers to lock in high-volume buyers and defend against well-capitalized competitors entering the B2B space.
3. **Expand AI Capabilities:** Continue refining the AI engine to predict hyper-local trends, allowing for targeted inventory recommendations based on specific district-level preferences.
