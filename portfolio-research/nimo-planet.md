# Nimo Planet – India’s Spatial-Computing Play to Redefine Enterprise Workflows

## Executive Summary

Nimo Planet is an Indian-US spatial computing startup focused on delivering pocket-sized, multi-screen virtual workspaces for the enterprise sector [1] [2]. Founded in 2016 and operating out of Kochi and Milpitas, the company differentiates itself from consumer-focused VR giants by targeting hybrid workforce productivity [1] [3]. With a proprietary operating system (Nimo OS), compact computing hardware (Nimo Core), and AR glasses (Nimo Glass), the company has secured early enterprise pilots with major firms like KPMG Consulting [3] [2]. Backed by approximately $7 million in funding from a mix of venture capital and strategic angel investors, Nimo Planet is currently scaling its manufacturing capabilities through partnerships to meet enterprise demand [4] [3].

## Product & Technology

Nimo Planet’s hardware and software ecosystem is designed to replace traditional laptops with a highly portable, multi-monitor virtual setup [2]. The system comprises three main components: Nimo OS, Nimo Core, and Nimo Glass [3]. 

### Core Hardware Specs & Differentiators

The Nimo Core is a highly compact compute device measuring just 63mm in width, 43mm in length, and 23mm in thickness [5]. It utilizes a custom rendering system that reduces CPU and memory usage, minimizing heat generation and optimizing battery life for multiple hours of continuous use [5]. The device includes a trackpad and an Air Mouse for intuitive navigation [5]. 

### Software Stack & Patents

Built on the Android Open Source Project (AOSP) and Linux, Nimo OS supports Mac and Windows applications via USB-C or Remote Desktop [2]. The company has secured a U.S. utility patent for its Nimo OS Spatial Workspace and Multi-Window Architecture, alongside an India design patent for the Nimo Glass [2] [5]. 

## Founding Team & Leadership

Nimo Planet was founded in 2016 by Rohildev Nattukallingal (CEO) and Suneesh Thuluthiyil [1]. Nattukallingal conceptualized the spatial computing system after finding the usability and portability of existing smart devices limiting for modern professionals [2]. The leadership team has also expanded its strategic advisory board, bringing on Ritesh Malik, founder of Innov8, as a board member to guide enterprise growth [2].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

Unlike Meta's Quest 3, which focuses heavily on gaming and the metaverse, or Apple's Vision Pro, which is primarily designed for indoor, stationary use, Nimo Planet is strictly positioned as a B2B productivity tool for hybrid and traveling workers [3].

*Takeaway: By avoiding direct competition in the consumer entertainment space, Nimo Planet captures a niche but highly lucrative enterprise segment that prioritizes portability and multi-screen workflow integration.*

## Business Model & Recent News

Nimo Planet operates a B2B business model, targeting enterprise deployments rather than direct-to-consumer retail [3]. The company has successfully delivered its Nimo OS to 15 enterprises, including a notable deployment with KPMG Consulting and other firms in the Middle East, US, and Europe [3]. 

Recent developments highlight the company's push toward mass production. Following a strong showcase at CES 2024, Nimo Planet announced a partnership with US-based AR glass manufacturer Rokid Global [3]. As of late 2025, CEO Rohildev Nattukallingal stated the immediate goal is to ship 3,000 to 5,000 units to early customers within six to eight months, supported by ongoing fundraising efforts to scale mass production [3]. Furthermore, the company is launching a developer program to encourage the creation of custom enterprise applications on Nimo OS [3].
