# Obvious Finance (India) – Cash-Management Playbook for Founders & the Road to Scale

## Executive Summary
Obvious Finance (operating via tryobvious.com and Obvious Technology) is an emerging Indian fintech startup that provides a founder-centric cash management platform blending traditional financial analytics with Web3 infrastructure. Positioned as a tool to help founders master their burn rate and runway, the company recently reached a major milestone by becoming part of the PhonePe group. Backed by a strong technical founding team and niche pre-seed investors, Obvious Finance is uniquely positioned to scale its hybrid fiat-crypto financial dashboard to millions of SMEs, provided it navigates India's complex regulatory landscape effectively.

## Product and Service Capabilities
Obvious Finance markets its core offering with the promise: "Your cash, explained" [1]. The platform is designed to help startup founders streamline cash management and master their burn, runway, expenses, and money flow [1]. 

Beyond traditional fiat cash flow management, the company has built robust Web3 capabilities. Its Obvious Smart Contract Wallet allows users to manage cryptocurrencies across various blockchains, featuring the ability to pay gas fees in any token, one-click swap and bridge functionality, and free ENS subdomains [2] [3]. The product suite also includes "TokenGPT," an AI-powered software that predicts cryptocurrency token price movements using on-chain data [3].

## Market Position
Obvious Finance operates at the intersection of SME financial operations and decentralized finance. In the traditional cash management space, it faces indirect competition from established runway-management tools like Runway, which initially built prototypes for small startups [5], and Relay, which focuses on bringing cash flow clarity to SMBs [6]. However, Obvious differentiates itself by offering a unified on-chain and off-chain portfolio with liquidity routing, aiming to make crypto truly interoperable for everyone [3]. 

## Recent News and Strategic Milestones
The most significant recent development for Obvious Finance is its acquisition. In a major strategic shift, the company announced: "We have news: Obvious is now a part of the PhonePe group" [7]. The leadership noted they had thought long and hard about the decision and are "super excited about this new future" [7]. This integration with India's largest fintech startup provides Obvious with an unprecedented distribution channel to scale its cash management tools.

## Business Model
While specific pricing tiers for the SaaS dashboard are not publicly detailed, the company's business model relies on a hybrid approach:
1. **SaaS/Dashboard Access:** Providing founders with early access to premium cash flow and runway analytics [1].
2. **Web3 Transaction Fees:** Generating revenue through its multi-chain mobile wallets and cross-chain bridge, which finds the fastest and cheapest routes through decentralized exchanges [3].
3. **Enterprise Integration:** Following the PhonePe acquisition [7], Obvious is positioned to potentially offer white-labeled cash flow APIs or premium merchant services to PhonePe's massive SME user base.
