# OnCare 2026: How a Distributed Oncology Model Is Re-shaping Affordable Cancer Care in India

## Executive Summary
OnCare is a Gurugram-based healthcare startup rapidly scaling a capital-efficient, distributed oncology model across India [1] [2]. By building and operating cancer care departments within existing mid-sized, non-branded hospitals, OnCare avoids the massive capital expenditures associated with standalone facilities [1] [2]. Backed by a recent ₹27 crore ($3 million) Series A funding round in February 2026, the company is positioned to become a price leader in the Indian oncology market, offering treatments at 30-40% lower costs than corporate hospitals [1] [2]. With a proven founding team and strong early traction, OnCare is aggressively expanding into tier-II and tier-III cities to bridge the massive demand-supply gap in affordable cancer care [1] [3].

## Product & Service Offering
OnCare provides an end-to-end oncology care suite, including medical, surgical, and radiation oncology services, as well as diagnostics and psychological counseling [4] [3]. 

### Disruptive Pricing Advantage
The core value proposition of OnCare is its affordability. By leveraging its asset-light model, the company passes significant cost savings directly to patients:
* **Chemotherapy:** Priced at approximately ₹24,000 to ₹25,000 per session, which is about 40% lower than the ₹40,000 typically charged at large corporate hospitals [1] [2].
* **Surgeries:** Cancer surgeries are offered at a 30% to 40% discount compared to corporate peers [1] [2].

## Founding Team
Founded in 2023, OnCare is led by Amar Sneh and Deepak Kumar [1] [3]. Both founders bring deep operational expertise from their previous tenures at Pristyn Care, a platform known for successfully scaling surgical care via partner hospitals [1]. This background significantly de-risks OnCare's execution strategy, as the founders possess a proven playbook for standardizing care and managing revenue-share partnerships across fragmented hospital networks [1].

## Business Model
OnCare operates a B2B2C distributed oncology platform [3]. Instead of building expensive, standalone cancer hospitals, it partners with mid-sized, non-branded hospitals to set up and manage their oncology departments [1] [2]. 

### Pooled Procurement Economics
A critical driver of OnCare's margin and pricing strategy is pooled drug procurement. Mid-sized hospitals typically lack bargaining power when purchasing high-cost oncology medicines [2]. By aggregating demand across its network of centers, OnCare can negotiate bulk discounts, mimicking the success of India's National Cancer Grid, which achieved median savings of 82% on maximum retail prices [2].

## Market Position
OnCare is carving out a distinct niche in the highly fragmented Indian cancer care market. While specialized oncology services traditionally cluster in large urban corporate hospitals, OnCare brings specialist services closer to home for patients in smaller towns [2].

## Recent News & Expansion Strategy
As of early 2026, OnCare operates four operational centers across the Delhi-NCR region [1] [3]. The company has already demonstrated significant traction, interacting with approximately 30,000 patients, conducting 3,000 consultations, and completing over 4,000 procedures for roughly 1,000 treated patients [1].

Following its February 2026 Series A raise, OnCare announced aggressive expansion plans. The company intends to enter the Bangalore market and open new centers in tertiary cities across Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and West Bengal over the next year [1]. This eastern belt expansion strategically targets regions with a massive demand-supply gap, where patients historically had to travel to metropolitan hubs like Delhi for affordable treatment [1].
