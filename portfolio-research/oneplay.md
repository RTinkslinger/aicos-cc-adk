# OnePlay Deep-Dive: India's Decentralised Cloud-Gaming Playbook (2024-26)

## Executive Summary

OnePlay is an emerging Indian cloud-gaming startup that leverages a decentralised server model to offer hardware-agnostic gaming to price-sensitive consumers. With over 50,000 registered users and 100,000 downloads [1], the company has proven early product-market fit. Backed by a $1.8 million seed round [1] and strategic investments from industry veterans [2], OnePlay is well-positioned to capture a slice of India's projected $7.5 billion gaming market [2]. However, the company must navigate infrastructure bottlenecks and scale its paying subscriber base to secure future Series A funding.

## 1. Company Overview: Democratising AAA Gaming

Founded in 2019 and based in Mumbai, OnePlay is an open-source cloud gaming platform that allows users to play high-end PC games without expensive hardware [3] [2]. 

### 1.1 Core Product Suite
OnePlay streams games directly to users' devices, including Android, Windows, Mac, and Samsung Smart TVs [4]. Users must own the games on digital storefronts like Steam or Epic Games [1]. Additionally, the company offers "OneSpace," a cloud PC subscription providing 256 GB of storage for gaming, creative work, and general computing [4].

### 1.2 Decentralised Infrastructure
Unlike traditional centralised data centres, OnePlay utilises a decentralised network of distributed servers [5]. This approach reduces direct infrastructure investments and aims to lower latency by connecting users to the closest available server [5].

## 2. Founding Team & Governance

OnePlay is led by a young, technically focused founding team with recent backing from prominent esports figures.

### 2.1 Key Executives
* **Harshit Jain (Co-Founder & CEO):** Transitioned from the OTT and AI space to capitalise on the gaming boom [5] [6].
* **Abhik Saha (Co-Founder & CTO):** A tech prodigy who began his engineering career at age 12, building antivirus software before co-founding OnePlay [1] [6].

### 2.2 Strategic Advisors
In November 2024, Akshat Rathee, co-founder of NODWIN Gaming, joined OnePlay as an investor and advisor to help the company penetrate domestic and international markets [2].

## 6. Business Model & Traction

OnePlay operates a subscription-based revenue model with flexible pricing tiers.

### 6.1 Revenue Streams and Pricing
Users can access the platform for as low as Rs 39 per hour or opt for a flat monthly plan at Rs 499 [5]. The OneSpace cloud PC is also offered as a monthly subscription [4]. 

### 6.2 User Metrics and Infrastructure
As of late 2023, the platform reported over 50,000 registered players and 100,000 downloads across key Indian cities like Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru [1]. To support this, OnePlay operates approximately 67 active servers across the country [5]. The founders have also teased a future token-based economy to reward server hosts [5].

## 7. Risks & Strategic Recommendations

**Information Gaps & Risks:**
OnePlay requires a minimum internet speed of 15Mbps for 720p gaming and 25Mbps for 1080p [4]. Inconsistent broadband in India remains a significant hurdle. Furthermore, the company has not publicly disclosed its revenue, EBITDA, or customer acquisition costs, making financial valuation difficult.

**Strategic Recommendations:**
1. **Forge ISP Partnerships:** Partner with major telecom operators (like Airtel or Jio) to offer zero-rated data bundles for OnePlay traffic, mitigating the broadband barrier.
2. **Delay Token Rollout:** Postpone the planned cryptocurrency token launch [5] until regulatory clarity is achieved in India; focus instead on traditional loyalty points.
3. **Prepare Financial Data Rooms:** To secure a Series A round, OnePlay must transition from reporting "registered users" to showcasing sticky Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and low churn rates.
