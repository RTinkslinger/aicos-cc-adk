# OpenIAM – Identity-First Security Playbook & APAC Growth Blueprint (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary
OpenIAM is a bootstrapped, profitable cybersecurity firm providing a converged Identity and Access Management (IAM) platform. While headquartered in the United States, the company is aggressively expanding its footprint in the Asia-Pacific (APAC) region, specifically targeting India's regulated sectors through its local entity, OpenIAM Pvt Ltd, and "Make in India" certifications. Despite a strong product offering and global customer base, conflicting public data regarding its employee count and revenue requires careful due diligence from potential partners or investors.

## 1. Company Overview & Value Proposition
Founded in 2008 and headquartered in Cortlandt Manor, New York, OpenIAM delivers a comprehensive, open-source-based identity platform [1] [2]. 

The company differentiates itself by offering a "converged" platform that consolidates traditionally fragmented capabilities into a single solution [2] [3]. Its core offerings include:
* **Workforce and Customer Identity (CIAM):** Securing both internal employees and external customers [1] [3].
* **Identity Governance and Administration (IGA):** Automated provisioning and compliance controls [1].
* **Access Management:** Web access management, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), Passwordless Authentication, and Privileged Access Management (PAM) [1] [2].

By utilizing an Identity-First Security strategy and Zero-Trust Architecture (ZTA), OpenIAM allows deployments across on-premises, cloud-native, or IDaaS environments [1].

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership
OpenIAM was built by a technical founding team with deep enterprise experience, though its current executive structure has notable gaps in public documentation.
* **Suneet Shah (Co-Founder & CTO):** A former engineer at IBM and Novell, Shah leads product strategy and technology [4] [5].
* **Arun Shah (Co-Founder):** Holds an electrical engineering background and co-founded the platform [4] [6].
* **Ameet Shah (Chief Marketing Officer):** Drives global marketing and go-to-market strategies [4] [7].
* **Veneet Sharma (VP of Sales, APAC):** Appointed in February 2026, Sharma brings 27+ years of experience from Thales and Cisco to lead the company's Asian expansion [3] [8].

*Information Gap:* Public records do not explicitly name a current Chief Executive Officer (CEO), though Suneet Shah has been referenced as a former CEO in past roles [9].

## 3. Funding History & Financial Health

## 6. Business Model & Traction
OpenIAM generates predictable revenue through an annual subscription model. Organizations purchase the "Enterprise Edition" via Gold or Platinum tiers, which dictate Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and support levels [15].

**Customer Sentiment:** On Gartner Peer Insights, OpenIAM holds a 3.5/5 rating. While 40% of users give it 5 stars for being cost-effective and easy to configure compared to Oracle or IBM, 60% give it 2 stars, citing poor post-implementation support, slow bug resolution, and limited documentation [13].

## 7. India & APAC Context
OpenIAM is aggressively targeting India and the broader APAC region, focusing on regulated mid-sized and large enterprises [3]. 
* **Local Infrastructure:** The company has established **OpenIAM Pvt Ltd** in India, featuring a cross-functional team spanning engineering, sales, marketing, and customer support [3] [8].
* **Regulatory Alignment:** To capture public-sector and highly regulated financial clients, OpenIAM has secured **"Make in India" certification** [3] [8]. This positions the company perfectly to capitalize on India's stringent data localization and digital identity mandates.

## 8. Information Gaps & Risks
While OpenIAM presents a strong product narrative, several critical data discrepancies exist in public records:
* **Employee Count:** Tracxn reports just 3 employees (as of Dec 2022) [2]. LinkedIn and Crunchbase report 51-200 employees [1] [4]. RocketReach estimates 25 employees [17]. 
* **Revenue:** RocketReach estimates OpenIAM's revenue at $7 million, but as a private, bootstrapped company, this figure is unverified [17].
* **Operational Risk:** Consistent Gartner reviews highlighting poor vendor support and lack of training resources in regions like India pose a churn risk for enterprise clients [13]. 

**Strategic Recommendation:** Any enterprise buyer or regional partner in India must negotiate strict, financially backed SLAs for local support and demand audited financials to verify the company's operational scale before committing to multi-year contracts.
