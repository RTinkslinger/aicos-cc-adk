# Orange Slice AI: How a Spreadsheet-Based Lead-Enrichment Engine Is Disrupting B2B Sales

## Executive Summary
Orange Slice is a Y Combinator-backed (S25) B2B SaaS startup that transforms a standard spreadsheet into an AI-powered lead enrichment engine [1]. By allowing sales teams to use natural language prompts to deploy AI agents that scrape the web for high-intent buying signals, the company eliminates the need for static lead lists [1] [2]. Fresh off a **$5.3 million seed round in February 2026**, the company is rapidly scaling its user base, boasting over 5,000 sales and RevOps teams [3] [4]. While the founders have Indian roots, the company currently operates with a global/U.S. focus, presenting a potential expansion gap for the Indian SaaS market.

## 1. Company Snapshot: What Orange Slice Does
Orange Slice provides an "agentic sales enrichment spreadsheet" where every column is powered by TypeScript written automatically by AI [1]. Users describe their ideal customer in plain English (e.g., "find 50 Series A fintech companies in NYC that just hired a Head of Sales"), and the platform's AI agents execute the research [3]. 

The value proposition centers on timing rather than effort. Instead of relying on static databases, Orange Slice builds custom scraping infrastructure to scan company websites, news articles, job postings, social media, and niche sources like court dockets for legal tech or building permits for proptech [5] [2]. This allows sales teams to target prospects who are actively in-market to buy.

## 2. Founding Team
The company was founded by two 23-year-old University of Michigan graduates who met on a college Bollywood dance team [4]. 
* **Vihaar Nandigala (Co-Founder & CEO):** Previously sold a ghost kitchen startup, KitchenKonnect, at age 19 and worked as a summer analyst at J.P. Morgan [4] [1]. He also has ties to India, having spent time teaching underprivileged children in Hyderabad [6].
* **Kishan Sripada (Co-Founder & CTO):** Previously bootstrapped FORMI, a choreography software used by Broadway and NBA teams (scaling to 100k+ users), and worked as a front-end engineering intern at Ramp [4] [1].

## 4. Market Position and Differentiation
The B2B data enrichment market is highly competitive, traditionally dominated by static database providers. Orange Slice differentiates itself through **real-time signal scoring** and its **spreadsheet-native interface** [1] [2]. Rather than forcing users to learn a new workflow builder, the platform integrates directly into a familiar spreadsheet format where AI handles the complex data pipelines [1]. 

## 5. Business Model and Traction
Orange Slice operates on a freemium, tiered SaaS subscription model based on monthly credit usage for enrichments, AI calls, and web scraping [8]. 

### Pricing Tiers
| Tier | Monthly Cost | Monthly Credits | Target Audience |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | 2,000 (one-time) | Trial users (includes 10 free messages) |
| Starter | $20 | 10,000 | Individuals getting started |
| Growth | $100 | 60,000 | Regular prospecting (adds priority support) |
| Scale | $400 | 300,000 | Power users (adds custom integrations) |

*Takeaway:* This pricing structure allows for easy bottom-up adoption by individual sales reps before expanding into enterprise-wide contracts [8]. The company reports being trusted by over 5,000 sales and RevOps teams [3].
