# Paisabazaar 2024-2026: India's Leading Digital Credit Marketplace

## Executive Summary
Paisabazaar, a subsidiary of PB Fintech, is India's dominant digital consumer credit marketplace. Holding a 53.7% market share in digital loan disbursals as of Fiscal 2021 [1], the platform has successfully transitioned from a pure B2C aggregator to a diversified credit ecosystem. Recent strategic shifts include a major leadership overhaul in March 2025 and the rapid scaling of its B2B2C platform, PB Connect, which now drives the majority of its loan disbursals [2] [3]. While the company boasts a massive user base of over 47.7 million credit score customers [4], it faces margin compression in its core credit business, necessitating a strategic pivot toward higher-margin initiatives and deeper integration with its parent company's insurance offerings.

## Company Overview & Value Proposition
Founded in 2014 in Gurugram, Haryana, Paisabazaar operates as an algorithm-based digital lending platform that enables consumers to compare, choose, and apply for personal credit products [5] [6]. The platform partners with over 65 large banks, NBFCs, and fintech lenders to offer a wide array of products, including personal loans, business loans, credit cards, home loans, and loans against property [7] [6]. Its core value proposition lies in providing unbiased advice, ease of comparison, and end-to-end assistance from application to disbursal [7]. Additionally, Paisabazaar offers free credit scores, acting as a powerful customer acquisition funnel [1].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape
Paisabazaar is the undisputed market leader in India's digital credit aggregation space. In Fiscal 2021, it held a 53.7% market share based on loan disbursals [1]. The platform leverages its massive top-of-funnel reach—over 47.7 million credit score customers as of September 2024—to cross-sell credit products [4]. While it competes with other aggregators like BankBazaar and CreditMantri, Paisabazaar's integration with Policybazaar under the PB Fintech umbrella provides a unique cross-selling moat. In March 2022, it also joined the FACE Consortium alongside players like Orange Retail Finance and Phocket to promote responsible lending [5].

## Business Model, Revenue Streams & Traction
Paisabazaar generates revenue primarily through commissions and take-rates from its lending partners upon successful loan disbursals or credit card issuances. 

| Metric | FY25 Performance | Strategic Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Take Rate** | 4.1% | The core Paisabazaar business generated INR 5.1bn in revenue in FY25, though revenue declined 12% year-over-year [2]. |
| **PB Connect Take Rate** | ~0.9% | The new B2B2C platform drove ~70% of total Q4 FY25 disbursals, generating an estimated INR 737mn in FY25 revenue [2]. |
| **Total Disbursals** | INR 76.5bn (Q4 FY25) | Disbursals surged 116% YoY in Q4 FY25, heavily driven by the PB Connect platform [2]. |
| **User Base** | 47.7 Million | Cumulative credit score customers as of September 2024 [4]. |

The company is actively expanding its secured lending portfolio (home loans, loans against property) and plans to introduce savings products like fixed deposits to better assess customer risk profiles [2].

## Risks, Information Gaps & Future Outlook
While Paisabazaar's disbursal volumes are growing rapidly, its core revenue dipped 12% in FY25, indicating margin compression as the lower-margin PB Connect platform (0.9% take rate) cannibalizes the higher-margin core business (4.1% take rate) [2]. 

**Information Gaps:** Publicly available financial disclosures lack granular data on Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), product-specific loan default rates, and the standalone unit economics of the PB Connect platform. These metrics are critical for assessing the long-term viability of its unsecured lending portfolio.

Looking ahead, Paisabazaar's ability to leverage its new account aggregator and payment aggregator licenses will be crucial in improving underwriting precision and creating a stickier, closed-loop financial ecosystem [10] [11].
