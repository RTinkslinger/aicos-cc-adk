# PowerEdge Deep-Dive: From Bangalore-Born Fraud-Detection SaaS to a Strategic Exit to Signzy

## Executive Summary

PowerEdge (registered as PowerEdge Analytics and Services Private Limited) is a Bengaluru-based financial technology startup that rapidly scaled its infrastructure solutions for digital credit before achieving a strategic exit [1] [2]. Founded between 2022 and 2023, the company built a robust suite of products encompassing fraud detection, debt recovery, and credit card deployment [1] [2]. Demonstrating strong product-market fit, PowerEdge reached ₹11.2Cr in annual revenue by March 2025 and expanded its workforce to 136 employees by May 2025 [1]. Following a ₹8.54 crore ($1.56M) funding round backed by DeVC and White Venture Capital in May 2025, the startup was acquired by digital banking infrastructure provider Signzy in November 2025 [2] [3]. 

## Product & Service Overview

PowerEdge operates as a B2B SaaS provider, offering a vertically-integrated technology stack designed to address core challenges in the digital credit ecosystem [1] [4]. The platform is regulatory-ready and compliant with the RBI Sachet Portal [4]. Its primary offerings include:

* **AntiFraud Ring:** A superior fraud detection network designed to safeguard financial institutions from organized fraud syndicates [4]. The system utilizes a sharp-shooting model with precise match rates across traditional, digital, and biometric parameters [4]. Notably, it helped Uni cards stop 15 basis points (bps) of risk-approved applications made through identity fraud [4].
* **Power Collect:** An end-to-end debt recovery solution that tackles delinquency across the entire collection lifecycle [1] [4]. It is supported by an in-house field collections team active across more than 130 cities and 10,000+ pincodes [4].
* **Credit Card Suite:** A platform enabling banks and financial institutions to efficiently launch, deploy, and manage co-branded credit card offerings while streamlining operational workflows and compliance [2] [5].

The company was built by a team of industry veterans with deep expertise in engineering, risk management, and business leadership [4].

## Recent News & Strategic Moves

The defining strategic event for PowerEdge was its acquisition by Signzy on November 12, 2025 [2] [6]. Prior to this, the company had successfully scaled its operations, reporting an annual revenue of ₹11.2Cr as of March 31, 2025, and growing its team to 136 employees by May 2025 [1]. The integration into Signzy allows the parent company to bolster its digital banking and identity verification infrastructure with PowerEdge's specialized anti-fraud and credit card deployment capabilities [2] [6].

## Business Model & Revenue Streams

PowerEdge operates a B2B SaaS and tech-enabled services business model targeting financial institutions, neo-banks, and digital lenders [2] [4]. Its revenue streams are driven by API integrations for its AntiFraud Ring, platform licensing for its Credit Card Suite, and performance/service fees tied to its Power Collect debt recovery operations [2] [4]. By offering a vertically-integrated stack that reduces false positives and manages end-to-end collections, PowerEdge effectively lowers the customer acquisition and risk costs for its enterprise clients [4].
