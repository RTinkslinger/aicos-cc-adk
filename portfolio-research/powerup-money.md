# PowerUp Money: Fast-Tracking India's Mutual-Fund Advisory Market

## Executive Summary
PowerUp Money has rapidly emerged as a formidable player in India's wealth-tech sector, transitioning from a Uni Cards spin-off to a well-capitalized, independent advisory platform. With approximately $19 million raised across two major rounds in 2025 [1], the company is capitalizing on the growing demand for unbiased, zero-commission mutual fund advice. By securing a SEBI Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) license and adopting a subscription-based revenue model, PowerUp Money differentiates itself from traditional commission-driven distributors. The platform has already amassed 500,000 users and tracks over ₹65,000 crore in assets [2] [3], signaling strong product-market fit. 

## 1. Company Overview - Why PowerUp Matters
Based in Bengaluru, PowerUp Money is a digital wealth management and mutual fund advisory startup [4] [2]. The company addresses a critical gap in the Indian market: while millions of retail investors have started Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs), many lack the guidance to manage, review, and rebalance their portfolios effectively [5]. PowerUp operates as a zero-commission platform, offering research-led, unbiased investment advice rather than earning commissions from fund manufacturers [2] [3]. 

Its product suite includes:
* **Power Mutual Funds:** A free portfolio tracking and evaluation tool [2] [5].
* **PowerUp Elite:** A premium subscription offering personalized advisory, portfolio reviews, and asset allocation [2].
* **Power FD:** A tool providing access to high-interest fixed deposits from RBI-regulated banks and NBFCs [5].
* **Power Age:** A financial planning tool for long-term goal tracking [5].

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership DNA
PowerUp Money was founded in 2024 by Prateek Jindal, who previously co-founded the credit card fintech Uni Cards [2] [1]. The startup was originally incubated within Uni Cards before being formally spun off as an independent entity following its seed fundraise [5]. While Uni Cards retains a shareholding, the operations and teams have been fully separated, allowing Jindal and his team to focus exclusively on digital wealth management [5].

## 3. Funding History & Capital Deployment
Since its inception, PowerUp Money has raised approximately $19 million [1]. The investor base remains largely consistent with its former parent company, Uni Cards, demonstrating strong ongoing investor confidence [5].

| Funding Round | Date | Amount Raised | Lead Investor | Key Participants |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Seed | June 2025 | $7.1 Million | Accel, Blume Ventures, Kae Capital | 8i Ventures, DeVC, Angel Investors [5] |
| Series A | December 2025 | $12 Million | Peak XV Partners | Accel, Blume Ventures, Kae Capital, 8i Ventures, DevC [2] |

*Key Takeaway:* The rapid succession of funding—closing a Series A just six months after the seed round—highlights aggressive scaling. Peak XV contributed $7 million to the Series A, with existing investors adding $5 million [1]. The fresh capital is earmarked for strengthening research capabilities, scaling the "Elite" offering, launching a new managed advisory product called "PowerUp Infinite," and investing in financial literacy [2].

## 4. Market Landscape - The Indian Wealth-Tech Boom
The Indian mutual fund industry is experiencing record inflows, driven heavily by retail SIPs [1]. However, access to high-quality, institutional-grade advice has not kept pace, leaving many investors reliant on informal guidance or short-term performance metrics [3]. PowerUp Money targets users with a wealth bracket of ₹20 lakh to ₹2 crore, aiming to democratize the kind of advisory services traditionally reserved for high-net-worth individuals [1].

## 5. Competitive Positioning & Differentiation
While platforms like Groww and Zerodha Coin dominate mutual fund distribution, PowerUp Money focuses strictly on the advisory layer. 

| Feature | PowerUp Money | Traditional Distributors (e.g., Groww, Coin) | Traditional Wealth Managers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core Model | Advisory-First (Zero Commission) [3] | Execution/Distribution-First | Advisory/Distribution |
| Regulatory Status | SEBI Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) [2] | Stock Broker / Mutual Fund Distributor | Varies (Often Distributor) |
| Revenue Stream | Subscription Fees (₹999/year) [2] | Cross-selling, Broking fees | High AUM-based fees / Commissions |
| Target Audience | Mass Affluent (₹20L - ₹2Cr) [1] | Retail / Beginners | High Net Worth Individuals (HNIs) |

*Key Takeaway:* By holding a SEBI RIA license (INA000019798), PowerUp ensures its recommendations are legally aligned with the investor's long-term wealth creation goals, avoiding the conflict of interest inherent in commission-based models [6] [2].

## 6. Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)
* **April 2024:** PowerUp Money officially launches its DIY app [5].
* **June 2025:** Secures $7.1 million in Seed funding and formally spins off from Uni Cards [5].
* **December 2025:** Closes a $12 million Series A led by Peak XV Partners [2].
* **Traction Milestone:** Within eight months of launch, the platform onboarded over 500,000 users and tracked more than ₹65,000 crore in assets [2].

## 7. Business Model, Traction & Unit Economics
PowerUp Money monetizes through its flagship subscription product, **PowerUp Elite**, which charges an annual fee of ₹999 [2]. 
* **Customer Base:** As of late 2025, the platform had 500,000 total users and over 25,000 paid Elite members [2] [1].
* **Assets Under Management (AUM):** The company tracks ₹65,000 crore overall, with ₹12,500 crore in assets held specifically by paid subscribers [2] [1].
* **Future Targets:** The company aims to onboard 10 million users and 1 million paid subscribers over the next three years [1]. It is also preparing to launch **PowerUp Infinite**, a fully managed, one-on-one advisory product [2].

## 8. Regulatory & Risk Assessment
Operating as a SEBI-registered RIA provides a significant trust moat, especially as Indian regulators crack down on unregistered "finfluencers" [2]. However, the reliance on human relationship managers and a centralized team of portfolio managers for paid users [1] presents a scaling risk. If user growth outpaces the hiring of qualified advisors, service quality could degrade.
