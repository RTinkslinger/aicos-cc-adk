# Praan ‑ India's Filter-Less Air-Purification Pioneer: Technology, Funding & Market Playbook (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary
Praan is a deep-tech startup redefining the air purification landscape through its proprietary filterless technology and carbon capture capabilities [1] [2]. By eliminating the need for recurring filter replacements, Praan drastically reduces the lifecycle operational expenditure (OPEX) of air purification for both industrial and consumer markets [2]. Backed by strategic industrial investors and experiencing rapid commercial traction—including a 5x manufacturing capacity increase in late 2025—the company is well-positioned to capitalize on India's booming air purifier market, which is projected to reach $1.16 billion by 2030 [3] [4]. 

## Company Overview and Value Proposition
Founded in 2017 and dual-headquartered in Mumbai and San Francisco, Praan develops advanced air purification technologies for commercial, industrial, and residential applications [5] [6]. The company's core value proposition lies in its filterless architecture, which captures particulate matter and carbon dioxide with near-zero maintenance costs [1] [2]. 

Praan's product portfolio includes:
* **MK One and MK Two**: Industrial-grade systems designed for large spaces like factories, airports, and schools. These systems use computational fluid dynamics to capture polluted air, separate microscopic particulate matter into a collection chamber, and release clean air [5] [2].
* **HIVE**: A compact, aircraft-grade aluminum purifier designed for homes and offices. It features an advanced indoor environmental sensing system and medical-grade filtration [5].
* **EDITH**: An AI-based air pollution solving engine that provides a holistic approach to clean air management [5].

## Founding Team and Leadership
Praan was founded by Angad Daryani in 2017 during his sophomore year at the Georgia Institute of Technology [7]. Daryani, an inventor and social entrepreneur with a background in electrical engineering, serves as the CEO [8]. The leadership and advisory team includes notable figures such as Surya Panditi (CEO of EnelX) and Naresh Shahani, who serves as an advisor and India Director [9] [10]. The company has faced talent acquisition challenges in India, with Daryani publicly noting the difficulty of raising capital locally to hire senior engineers [11] [6].

## Market Position and Competitive Landscape
The Indian air purifier market is experiencing massive tailwinds, valued at $157.2 million in 2022 and projected to reach $1.169 billion by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 28.5% [4] [15]. Praan differentiates itself through its deep-tech, filterless approach, contrasting sharply with competitors who rely on recurring filter sales.

## Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)
Praan has accelerated its commercial and operational momentum significantly between 2024 and early 2026:
* **June 2024**: Secured strategic capital from major industrial families (Kirloskar, Thyssenkrupp) to enhance production capacity and scale industrial tech sales [5].
* **May 2025**: Showcased the HIVE system at the Smart Home Expo in Mumbai, positioning it as a foundational layer for modern home automation [3].
* **Late 2025**: Quintupled manufacturing capacity by December 2025 to meet surging demand, achieving a 31x repeat order rate [3].
* **November 2025**: Commenced deliveries to early enterprise and home customers in the San Francisco Bay Area [3].
* **December 2025**: Announced a major partnership with Blue Tokai Coffee Roasters to deploy HIVE systems across its cafes, aiming to deliver clean air to 1.5 million customers [3] [16].

## Business Model and Traction
Praan operates a bifurcated business model depending on the geography. In India, the company relies on hardware sales coupled with recurring maintenance services (1 to 4 times a year) [6]. The HIVE product currently sells for an average of INR 60,000, though the company aims to reduce this to INR 30,000 as it scales [6]. In the United States, Praan treats clean air as a utility, charging customers a monthly subscription fee rather than an upfront hardware cost [6].

Traction has accelerated rapidly. By late 2025, Praan was selling approximately 150 units per month—matching its entire sales volume for the previous year—and had produced and shipped roughly 1,000 devices [6]. The company's industrial systems have been deployed by major corporations including Tata Steel, Pidilite, Nestle, and Berger Paints [6].
