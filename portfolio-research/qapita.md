# Qapita 2024-2026: Redefining Equity Management for Indian Startups

## Executive Summary
Qapita is a Singapore-headquartered, India-focused fintech platform that has rapidly become a dominant force in equity and cap table management for startups. Having raised $65.5 million to date, including a recent $26.5 million Series B led by Charles Schwab in late 2025, the company is expanding its footprint into the US while solidifying its stronghold in India and Southeast Asia. By combining cap table management, ESOP administration, and fund administration into a single platform tailored for complex Indian regulatory frameworks, Qapita offers a compelling alternative to global players like Carta.

## 1. Company Overview & Value Proposition
Qapita operates a comprehensive digital equity management platform designed to help private companies manage capitalization tables, Employee Stock Ownership Plans (ESOPs), and liquidity [1]. Its core value proposition lies in its deep localization for the Indian market. The platform natively handles complex Indian compliance layers, including SEBI-mandated disclosures, RBI cross-border reporting, and tax deferral tracking [2]. By allowing founders to run real-time "what-if" dilution scenarios and fundraise modeling, Qapita enables data-backed negotiations and prevents costly equity allocation mistakes [2]. 

## 2. Founding Team
The company is led by a seasoned team with deep financial and technological expertise:
* **Ravi Ravulaparthi (CEO & Co-founder):** A former investment banker and IIM graduate with extensive experience in corporate finance [3] [4].
* **Lakshman Gupta Kanamarlapudi (COO & Co-founder):** A Chartered Accountant who brought two decades of corporate experience to the startup [5].
* **Vamsee Mohan Kamabathula (CTO & Co-founder):** Leads the technological architecture of the platform [1].

## 4. Market Position & Competition
The global equity management software market is projected to reach $3.56 billion by 2034 [10]. In India, Qapita competes with global giant Carta, as well as regional players like Trica Equity, Pulley, EquityList, and Ledgy [11] [12]. Qapita differentiates itself through its 2022 acquisition of ESOP Direct, which instantly gave it management over $12 billion in ESOPs and a massive localized data advantage [13]. Furthermore, Qapita offers a three-pronged solution: equity management for companies, fund administration for VCs, and secondary liquidity offerings [9].

## 5. Recent News & Milestones (2024-2026)
* **October 2025:** Raised $26.5 million in Series B funding to collaborate with Charles Schwab on "Schwab Private Issuer Equity Services," expanding its reach to US-based private companies [9].
* **2024-2025 Market Insights:** Qapita has positioned itself as a thought leader, publishing data showing that 78% of Indian startups implemented ESOPs in 2024, up from 59% in 2021 [14]. They also actively track SEBI regulatory shifts, such as the March 2025 proposed amendments allowing founders to retain ESOPs [15].

## 6. Business Model, Traction & Information Gaps
Qapita operates on a B2B SaaS subscription model, offering tiered pricing for cap table and ESOP management, alongside consulting and fund administration services [16] [17]. The platform serves over 1,200 companies across India and Southeast Asia [13], boasting a customer base that includes prominent Indian startups like Zingbus, Mailmodo, and Boat [18]. 

**Information Gaps:** While Qapita claims transparent pricing, specific subscription costs (ARR/MRR) and exact revenue figures are not publicly disclosed [16].
