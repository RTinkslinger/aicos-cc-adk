# Rakshan AI — A Stealth-Mode AI-Governance Playbook and Its Implications for the Indian Startup Landscape

## Executive Summary
Rakshan AI is an unfunded, San Francisco-based startup operating in stealth mode within the rapidly expanding AI governance and compliance sector [1]. Although the research brief requested a focus on Indian startups, Rakshan AI is headquartered in the United States and currently shows no verified operational footprint in India [1]. The company is building a platform to help large enterprises govern, secure, and comply with emerging global AI regulations [1] [2]. For Indian stakeholders, Rakshan AI serves as a critical case study: it highlights a massive, well-funded global market for AI compliance tools where Indian-headquartered solutions are currently absent, presenting a distinct first-mover opportunity for local founders and investors.

## Company Overview — What Rakshan AI Does
Rakshan AI operates as an AI-based governance management platform designed to help businesses navigate the complex landscape of AI regulations [1]. The platform addresses critical enterprise challenges, including the management of shadow AI assets, AI-specific security threats, and the auditability of AI models [1]. 

Its core capabilities provide a unified solution for:
* Discovering AI assets such as models, datasets, and APIs [1].
* Automating compliance with global frameworks like the EU AI Act and the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF) [1].
* Detecting runtime security threats and enabling continuous monitoring with audit-ready workflows [1].

Currently operating in stealth mode, the company's public presence is limited to a landing page stating they are "building something extraordinary with large enterprises" and offering a "Schedule Demo" call-to-action [2].
