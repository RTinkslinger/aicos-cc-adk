# Realfast.ai – AI-Native Salesforce & IT-Services Playbook for the Indian Market

## Executive Summary

Realfast.ai is an early-stage, AI-native IT services startup operating out of Bengaluru, India, and Singapore [1] [2]. Founded to disrupt the $1.2 trillion IT services market, the company leverages hyper-scalable AI assistants to accelerate software development, customization, and deployment [1] [2]. By embedding AI agents directly into Salesforce and enterprise service workflows, Realfast aims to double revenue potential and cut delivery times in half for IT companies [2]. Backed by top-tier venture capital, the company represents a critical shift from traditional human-only IT services to hybrid human-AI delivery models, offering a compelling margin-expansion playbook for the Indian IT sector.

## Product & Service Offering: The Vayu Platform

Realfast’s core product is the "Vayu" platform, which integrates deeply with the Salesforce ecosystem to empower AI agents with developer tools [2] [3]. Rather than replacing human workers, Vayu augments them across the entire service lifecycle, including requirement gathering, design, development, testing, and deployment [2]. 

The platform has demonstrated significant operational efficiencies in early pilot programs with mid-sized Indian IT companies [2]. 

| Performance Metric | Traditional IT Services | Realfast (Vayu) Implementation | Impact |
|-------------------|-------------------------|--------------------------------|--------|
| Unit Testing Tasks | Manual execution | Handles >700 tasks autonomously | Mimics developer workflows [2] |
| Sprint Cycle Time | 10 days | 8 days | 50% reduction in unit-test writing time [2] |
| Gross Margins | ~35% | ~60% (Targeted) | Significant profitability uplift [2] |

**Strategic Takeaway:** IT service providers should evaluate Vayu to compress delivery timelines. The proven reduction of sprint cycles from 10 to 8 days offers an immediate competitive advantage in project bidding [2].

## Founding Team & Leadership: Strategy Meets Execution

Realfast’s leadership combines decacorn-scale engineering experience with top-tier management consulting and venture capital expertise [4] [5]. 

* **Sidu Ponnappa (CEO & Co-founder):** Former MD of Gojek India and ex-CIO of Gojek Singapore. He previously co-founded C42 Engineering, which Gojek acquired in 2015 [6] [2].
* **Aakash Dharmadhikari (CPTO & Co-founder):** Co-founder of C42 Engineering alongside Ponnappa, bringing deep technical architecture experience [7] [2].
* **Steve Sule (CFO & Co-founder):** Former partner at Singapore-based VC firm Edith Grove Capital, providing robust financial and fundraising strategy [2].

After operating in stealth mode for its first year, Realfast successfully secured institutional backing to scale its operations and expand the Vayu model into new domains like ERP and IT Service Management (ITSM) [2].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape: Disrupting a $1.2T Market

The Indian AI sector is projected to reach $17 billion by 2027, growing at 25-35% annually [2]. Realfast operates in a highly competitive niche focused on enterprise productivity, competing alongside startups like Alltius, Ema, and Revrag.ai [2]. 

However, Realfast differentiates itself through its specialized focus on Salesforce and Agentforce implementations [9]. While competitors often build generalized enterprise AI workflows, Realfast acts as an "Iron Man suit for the IT guys," specifically targeting the pain points of IT service delivery and SaaS profit margins [2] [3].

## Business Model & Revenue Strategy: Margin Expansion

Realfast operates as an AI-native implementation partner, targeting mid-sized IT companies in India through pilot programs [10] [2]. The business model hinges on transforming the fundamental unit economics of IT services. By utilizing AI agents, Salesforce partners can implement solutions at double the speed, effectively shifting traditional IT service gross margins from 35% to a targeted 60% [2].
