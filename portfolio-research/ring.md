# RING (formerly Kissht): India's Fast-Growing BNPL-to-Consumer-Loan Play – Funding, Performance & IPO Outlook (2024-26)

## Executive Summary
RING (formerly Kissht) is a prominent Indian digital lending platform operated by OnEMI Technology Solutions Limited [1]. Originally founded in 2015 as Kissht, the company rebranded to RING in 2021 to better resonate with a younger, convenience-oriented demographic [2] [3]. As of early 2026, RING has established itself as a major player in the Indian consumer credit market, boasting an Asset Under Management (AUM) of over ₹4,200 crore in FY25 [4]. The company recently secured SEBI clearance for a ₹1,000 crore Initial Public Offering (IPO), marking a significant milestone in its growth trajectory [5].

## Company Overview
RING operates as a financial technology platform providing instant, seamless credit solutions at digital points of sale, both online and offline [2]. The company's core value proposition centers on offering accessible credit to underserved segments, specifically targeting individuals earning between ₹20,000 and ₹70,000 per month, including gig workers, self-employed professionals, and call center employees [2]. 

RING's product suite includes:
* **Personal Loans:** "RING Power Loans" offering up to ₹5 Lakhs within minutes, with repayment plans ranging from 3 to 36 months and interest rates starting at 3.8% per month [6].
* **Business Loans:** Instant loans up to ₹5 Lakhs tailored for small business growth [7].
* **Secured Loans:** Loan Against Property (LAP) offering up to ₹15 Lakhs, introduced to diversify the portfolio and capture higher-ticket, longer-tenure segments [4] [7].

## Founding Team & Leadership
The leadership team brings extensive experience in financial services, consulting, and technology:
* **Ranvir Singh (Founder, CEO & MD):** An IIT Bombay and IIM Bangalore alumnus with over 15 years of experience. He previously served as an Associate Partner at McKinsey & Company, advising banks and financial institutions across India and Southeast Asia [8] [3].
* **Krishnan Vishwanathan (Founder & Executive Director):** An IIT Delhi and Yale University MBA graduate. He also worked at McKinsey & Company, specializing in retail financial services, before co-founding Kissht and RING [3].
* **Karan Mehta (Founder & CTO):** Leads the technological vision, focusing on making credit affordable and practical through advanced tech [3].
* **Sonali Jindal (Founder & COO):** Brings deep expertise in credit risk assessment and retail banking analytics, having previously worked with HDFC Bank and Deutsche Bank [3].

## Financial Performance & Traction
RING has demonstrated explosive AUM growth, though recent regulatory shifts and market dynamics have pressured top-line revenue and profitability. The platform serves over 9.16 million customers and boasts 53.23 million registered users [1]. Notably, the platform maintains an impressive repeat borrower rate of 80-90% monthly [2].

| Metric | FY23 | FY24 | FY25 |
|--------|------|------|------|
| **Revenue from Operations** | ₹984.46 Crore | ₹1,674.45 Crore (+70.09%) | ₹1,337.47 Crore (-20.12%) |
| **Net Profit** | N/A | ₹197.29 Crore | ₹160.62 Crore (-18.6%) |
| **Assets Under Management (AUM)** | N/A | ₹2,700 Crore | ₹4,200 Crore (+55%) |

*Takeaway:* While AUM grew by a robust 55% in FY25, revenue and net profit contracted. This divergence is largely attributed to the company discontinuing short-term products in response to RBI scrutiny, pivoting instead toward longer-tenured and secured loans [4] [13].

## Market Landscape & Competitive Position
The Indian digital lending space is highly competitive and capital-intensive. RING competes with established players like KreditBee, CASHe, Fibe, Stashfin, and MoneyTap [4]. 

| Competitor | Target Segment | Key Differentiation |
|------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **RING** | ₹20k-70k income, gig workers | 80-90% repeat rate, strong omnichannel presence (3,500+ offline POS) [2] [11]. |
| **KreditBee** | Young professionals | Aggressive digital acquisition and broad unsecured loan portfolio [4]. |
| **CASHe** | Salaried millennials | Mobile-first UX and proprietary social behavior-based credit scoring [4]. |

*Takeaway:* RING's distinct advantage lies in its exceptionally high repeat usage and its strategic pivot toward secured lending (LAP), which insulates it from the intense unit economic pressures facing pure-play unsecured digital lenders [2] [4].

## Recent News and Milestones (2024-2026)
* **April 2024:** Raised ₹100 crore in venture debt from Trifecta Capital to expand its loan book [12].
* **August 2025:** Parent company OnEMI Technology Solutions filed a Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) with SEBI for a ₹1,000 crore IPO, comprising a fresh issue and an offer for sale [1].
* **January 2026:** Secured SEBI clearance to proceed with the public listing [5].

## Business Model & Strategic Outlook
RING generates revenue primarily through interest income (starting at 3.8% per month) and processing fees (e.g., 4.13% including GST) [6]. The company utilizes proprietary self-learning algorithms that assess credit profiles based on over 2,000 digital footprints [11]. 

**Strategic Recommendations:**
1. **Accelerate Secured Lending:** With 50% of its unsecured loan customers being small business owners eligible for LAP, RING must aggressively cross-sell secured products to stabilize margins and reduce regulatory risk [4].
2. **Optimize IPO Timing:** Following SEBI clearance in January 2026, RING should capitalize on its ₹4,200 crore AUM milestone to anchor its valuation narrative during the upcoming roadshow [4] [5].
3. **Manage Compliance Costs:** As the RBI tightens oversight on unsecured digital lending, maintaining robust capital buffers and stringent underwriting standards will be critical to sustaining the projected 30-50% annual growth rate [4].
