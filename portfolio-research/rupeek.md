# Rupeek 2024-26: Gold-Loan Fintech Scaling, Profitability-Driven Funding and the Road Ahead

## Executive Summary

Rupeek has established itself as a disruptive force in India's gold-loan market by replacing traditional brick-and-mortar branches with a digital, doorstep-valuation model. As of early 2026, the company has successfully turned its core lending business profitable and is actively seeking a $50 million funding round at a premium valuation [1]. Operating primarily as a business correspondent for major banks, Rupeek has scaled its Assets Under Management (AUM) to ₹2,514 crore as of September 2024 [1]. However, it faces intense competition from legacy non-banking financial companies (NBFCs) and emerging fintechs in a market projected to cross ₹4 trillion by FY27 [2]. 

## Company Overview & Value Proposition

Founded in 2015, Bengaluru-based Rupeek is an online gold-loan platform that allows customers to pledge their gold ornaments for instant credit [1] [3]. 

The company's primary value proposition is its "loan-at-your-doorstep" model. Instead of requiring borrowers to visit a physical branch, Rupeek sends loan officers to the customer's residence or office to value the gold and process the loan within 30 minutes [4] [5]. To ensure security and reduce overhead, Rupeek utilizes a hybrid-branch model where the pledged gold is securely stored in the vaults of its partner banks [6] [5]. This asset-light approach significantly reduces operating costs compared to traditional NBFCs, allowing Rupeek to pass on the savings to consumers through lower interest rates [6].

## Founding Team & Leadership

Rupeek was founded by Sumit Maniyar, who serves as the Chief Executive Officer [7] [1] [3]. Maniyar recognized that traditional NBFCs were not optimized to deliver quick turnaround times and higher loan-to-value ratios for small business owners, prompting the creation of Rupeek's tech-enabled model [5]. 

The leadership team also includes Ashish Bansal, who serves as the Senior Vice President of Lender Partnerships, driving the company's critical co-lending and bank integration strategies [8].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

The Indian gold-loan sector is experiencing massive tailwinds, with total outstanding gold loans reaching ₹3.59 trillion in November 2025, up from ₹1.59 trillion in November 2024 [12]. CRISIL projects the AUM of gold-loan NBFCs to cross ₹4 trillion by FY27 [2].

## Business Model & Traction Metrics

Rupeek operates primarily as a business correspondent and co-lending partner for traditional financial institutions. Its lending partners include Federal Bank, Indian Bank, ICICI, and South Indian Bank [1] [17] [3] [8]. 

The company generates revenue by sourcing customers for these banks, while also maintaining a smaller, in-house loan book through its own NBFC, Rupeek Capital Private Limited (RCPL) [1] [18]. 

* **AUM Growth:** As of September 2024, Rupeek's total AUM reached ₹2,514 crore. Of this, ₹1,934 crore was off-book (partner lenders), highlighting the dominance of its asset-light correspondent model [1]. This is a notable increase from December 2023, when total AUM stood at ₹1,659.3 crore [18].
* **Geographic Reach:** The company operates in over 63 cities across 14 states in India [18].
* **Profitability:** By early 2026, Rupeek achieved profitability in its core lending business, a critical milestone that is driving its current $50 million fundraising efforts [1].

## Recent News & Milestones (2024-2026)

* **February 2026:** Rupeek initiated talks to raise $50 million to capitalize its in-house NBFC and expand operations, leveraging its newly achieved core business profitability [1] [11].
* **2025 Regulatory Shifts:** The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) released draft directions in April 2025 aimed at harmonizing the regulatory framework for gold-backed loans, which could impact LTV ratios and compliance costs for NBFCs [19].
* **2024 Financial Performance:** Despite achieving contribution margin break-even across its operational cities, Rupeek's consolidated net loss for the first nine months of fiscal 2024 was ₹116.1 crore, primarily driven by the high employee costs (57.4% of total expenses) required to maintain its tech and field staff [18].

## Information Gaps & Strategic Recommendations

While Rupeek's top-line AUM and funding targets are public, there are significant information gaps regarding its exact revenue split (origination fees vs. interest spread), specific Non-Performing Asset (NPA) ratios, and the full composition of its executive leadership team. 

**Recommendations:**
1. **Capitalize the Captive NBFC:** Rupeek should utilize the targeted $50 million raise to expand the balance sheet of Rupeek Capital. While the off-book model is capital efficient, growing the own-book portfolio will capture higher interest margins [1] [18].
2. **Deepen Bank Integrations:** To defend against legacy NBFCs, Rupeek must expand its co-lending roster beyond its current partners, targeting regional banks to penetrate Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities where gold ownership is highest.
