# Seeds Fincap: Tech-Enabled MSME Lending at Scale

## Executive Summary
Seeds Fincap Private Limited is a fast-growing, Gurugram-based Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) dedicated to bridging India's ₹30 trillion MSME credit gap [1] [2]. Operational since April 2021, the company leverages a technology-first, cash-flow-based underwriting model to provide tailored credit solutions to underserved micro, small, and medium enterprises [3] [2]. With over ₹500 crore in Assets Under Management (AUM), a network of approximately 120 branches across eight states, and a recent ₹50 crore Pre-Series B funding round in August 2025, Seeds Fincap has established itself as a highly efficient player in the Indian fintech lending space, maintaining strong asset quality with non-performing assets (NPAs) consistently below 2% [4] [2].

## Company Overview & Founding Team
Incorporated in 2019 and officially launching operations in 2021, Seeds Fincap was founded by Subhash Chandra Acharya and Avishek Sarkar [3] [5]. Both founders bring over 15 years of deep industry experience working across NBFCs and rating agencies, providing them with a strong foundation in risk management, branch network expansion, and MSME portfolio creation [5]. Their stated mission is to serve one million MSMEs in India over the current decade, focusing heavily on Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities where traditional banking penetration remains low [1] [5].

## Product & Service Offerings
Seeds Fincap offers a diversified portfolio of credit products tailored to the specific needs of small business owners, with ticket sizes ranging from ₹50,000 to ₹10 lakh [2]. The core offerings include:
* **Pragati:** An unsecured loan up to ₹40,000 designed for micro-enterprises operating for at least three years [1].
* **Samvardhan:** An unsecured loan up to ₹2 lakh for small businesses, featuring 2 to 3-year tenures [1].
* **Samridhi:** A secured loan ranging from ₹2 lakh to ₹5 lakh, backed by property, with tenures of 2 to 5 years [1].
* **Additional Services:** The company also provides Consumer Durable Loans and Supply Chain Finance [3]. 

## Business Model & Technology
The company operates a fully tech-enabled sourcing-to-collections model [2]. Rather than relying solely on traditional collateral or credit scores, Seeds Fincap utilizes cash-flow-based underwriting powered by artificial intelligence, machine learning, and big data [6] [2]. This relationship-based lending philosophy allows the company to accurately assess the creditworthiness of businesses that sit between the informal and formal economies [6]. Furthermore, the company has established strategic co-lending and debt partnerships with major financial institutions, including Tata Capital, AU Small Finance Bank, and IDFC Bank, to optimize its cost of capital [3].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape
Seeds Fincap has rapidly scaled its geographic footprint, currently operating around 120 branches across eight high-growth states: Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and Gujarat [3] [2]. The company has successfully disbursed over ₹1,000 crore to more than 50,000 MSMEs [2]. Its robust risk management framework has earned it a 'CRISIL BBB-/Stable' rating upgrade, distinguishing it from competitors in a sector often plagued by high default rates [3] [2].

## Recent News & Strategic Outlook
In August 2025, Seeds Fincap announced it had closed FY25 profitably and secured ₹50 crore in a Pre-Series B funding round [4] [2]. This capital injection is earmarked for aggressive geographic expansion, with plans to add 40 new branches by the end of the year, particularly targeting underserved regions in Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, and Bihar [4]. Looking ahead, the company plans to increase its secured lending portfolio while continuing to invest heavily in its technology and risk infrastructure, positioning itself as a dominant force in India's inclusive finance ecosystem [4] [2].
