# SolarSquare 2024-2026: How India's Fast-Growing Rooftop-Solar Brand is Scaling, Funding, and Facing Policy-Driven Risks

## Executive Summary
SolarSquare has rapidly emerged as India's leading residential rooftop solar brand, capitalizing on the country's massive push for renewable energy adoption. By transitioning from a B2B model to a direct-to-consumer (D2C) residential focus, the company has scaled to power over 20,000 homes [1]. Backed by nearly $56 million in venture funding from top-tier investors like Lightspeed and Elevation Capital, SolarSquare leverages a unique "IKEA-style" modular installation process to outpace traditional competitors [2] [3]. As of early 2026, the company is heavily integrated with the Indian government's PM Surya Ghar subsidy scheme, positioning itself to capture a significant share of the 70 million solar-eligible homes nationwide [4].

## Company Snapshot: Turnkey Residential Solar Solutions
Originally founded as a B2B commercial solar provider, SolarSquare pivoted to the consumer residential segment in 2020 [3]. The company provides a full-stack, turnkey rooftop solar solution that includes system design, third-party component procurement, installation, and financing assistance [3]. 

Its primary value proposition is speed and standardization. SolarSquare utilizes an in-house designed, modular setup that reduces typical project installation times from 3-5 days to a maximum of eight hours [3]. Furthermore, the company assists customers in navigating India's complex state and central government subsidies, ensuring they can capitalize on net-metering policies [1] [3].

## Founding Team & Leadership
SolarSquare was founded in 2015 by IIT Bombay alumni Neeraj Jain and Shreya Mishra, alongside former Panasonic executive Nikhil Nahar [3] [5]. Shreya Mishra currently serves as the CEO and has been instrumental in driving the company's D2C brand narrative and navigating the regulatory landscape of Indian solar subsidies [1] [4].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape
The Indian rooftop solar market is highly fragmented but growing rapidly, driven by the government's target to solarize millions of homes. While only about 1% of Indian homes currently have solar, the potential market is estimated at 70 million homes [1] [4]. 

| Competitor | Market Focus | Key Differentiators |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SolarSquare** | Residential D2C | 8-hour modular installs, strong VC backing, tech-first consumer brand [1] [3] |
| **Tata Power Solar** | Enterprise & Residential | Legacy brand trust, India's #1 EPC company for 9 consecutive years [8] [9] |
| **ZunRoof** | Residential | IoT-enabled solar solutions, early mover in residential tech [3] [10] |
| **Cleantech Solar** | Commercial & Industrial | Heavy focus on large-scale corporate PPAs across Southeast Asia and India [10] |

*Takeaway: SolarSquare differentiates itself from legacy players like Tata Power by focusing obsessively on the consumer experience, rapid installation, and dedicated residential financing [3].*

## Recent Milestones & Traction (2024-2026)
SolarSquare has achieved significant scale over the past two years:
* **December 2024:** Secured a $40 million Series B to expand operations from 20 to 50 cities and double its 800-person headcount [2].
* **September 2025:** Crossed the 250 MW milestone in rooftop solar installations, comprising 100 MW in the residential sector and 150 MW in commercial and industrial [11].
* **March 2026:** CEO Shreya Mishra highlighted that the PM Surya Ghar scheme has turbocharged adoption, helping solarize 3 to 3.8 million homes nationally since its launch, providing a massive tailwind for SolarSquare's continued expansion [4].

## Business Model & Financial Metrics
SolarSquare generates revenue by charging a lump sum fee for solar systems, which typically range from 3 kW to 50 kW, though 95% of demand is for sub-10 kW systems [3]. The company claims that each home going solar saves an average of Rs 48,000 annually on electricity bills [6].

Financially, the company has shown aggressive growth. SolarSquare reported revenue of Rs 107 crore in FY23, which grew to Rs 170 crore in FY24, with management projecting revenue to double in FY25 [2]. 

## Risks & Information Gaps
While SolarSquare's growth trajectory is clear, several risks and information gaps remain:
* **Policy Dependence:** The company's growth is heavily tied to the PM Surya Ghar scheme and state-level net-metering policies. Any rollback or delay in subsidy disbursements could severely impact consumer demand [1] [4].
* **Information Gaps:** Publicly available data lacks detailed unit economics, specific customer acquisition costs (CAC), and exact profitability margins. Furthermore, while the company plans to expand to 50 cities, the specific geographic breakdown of its current 250 MW installed capacity is not fully disclosed [2] [11].
