# Soulside AI – India-rooted Behavioral-Health AI Platform: Funding, Product Edge & Market Playbook

## Executive Summary
Soulside AI is a behavioral health technology startup that leverages artificial intelligence to automate clinical documentation, specializing in complex group therapy sessions. With a strong remote engineering presence in India and backing from prominent global and Indian-focused investors like Peak XV, the company recently secured a Seed round in June 2025 to scale its operations. By reducing documentation time by 83% and offering patent-pending speaker recognition, Soulside is positioned to alleviate clinician burnout and improve margins for mental health providers globally and within the Indian healthcare ecosystem.

## Product and Service
Soulside provides a comprehensive AI platform built specifically for behavioral health [1]. Its flagship feature is an AI-Scribe that fully automates both individual and group session notes [1]. Unlike generic AI scribes, Soulside utilizes patent-pending speaker recognition technology that can accurately identify up to 15 group participants and generate individual notes in under 60 seconds [1]. 

The platform dramatically improves clinical efficiency, transforming standard intake documentation from 55 minutes down to just 8 minutes, which increases intake throughput by 40% [1]. Furthermore, the system is HIPAA-compliant, features zero audio storage for maximum security, and offers one-click transfer to major Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems [1].

## Founding Team
The company was co-founded by Surabhi Bhandari (CEO) and Anurag Verma (CTO) [3] [4]. Bhandari brings extensive experience in healthcare technology, having previously worked at McKinsey & Company, Lexington Medical, and as an investor at Sequoia Capital (now Peak XV) [4] [2]. She holds an engineering degree and an MBA from Harvard Business School [2]. While the company is headquartered in San Francisco, it maintains a strong operational and talent footprint in India, actively hiring remote engineers to build out its platform [3].

## Funding History
Soulside has rapidly attracted capital from high-profile investors. In June 2025, the company announced a Seed funding round backed by Counterpart Ventures as part of their $132M Fund III [5]. Additional backers include GreyMatter Capital, OneMind, Sarah Smith Fund, and Kaya Ventures [6]. Prior to this, Soulside secured a $100,000 investment by joining the One Mind Accelerator's 2024 cohort in March 2024 [7]. In February 2024, the startup was selected for Peak XV's Spark 03 program, receiving a $100,000 equity-free grant and over $1M in cloud and technology perks [8].

## Market Position
The behavioral health AI market is becoming increasingly saturated with platforms like Ellipsis Health and Function Health [9] [7]. However, Soulside differentiates itself by focusing heavily on the complexities of group therapy and intermediate levels of care (such as maternal and postpartum support groups) [2] [7]. Its ability to guarantee 100% staff adoption within 8 weeks and its specialized focus on complex behavioral health workflows provide a strong competitive moat [1].

## Recent News
In mid-2025, Soulside was highlighted as a key Seed investment in Counterpart Ventures' newly announced $132M Fund III, emphasizing its role in bringing generative conversational-AI to group-based mental health [5]. The company has also been recognized as a UCSF Rosenman Healthtech Innovator 2024 and ranked in the Global Top 10 for the Confluent Data Streaming Challenge [3].

## Business Model
Soulside operates on a B2B SaaS business model tailored for behavioral health treatment facilities, outpatient clinics, and community mental health centers. The platform integrates documentation automation, real-time chart auditing for compliance, and Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) processes designed to extract coding insights and reduce claim denials [1]. By saving clinicians an average of 83% of time spent on documentation with zero after-hours work, Soulside delivers a clear ROI to healthcare organizations looking to navigate margin crunches and optimize resource allocation [1].
