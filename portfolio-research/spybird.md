# Spybird Interactive: Navigating India's Card-Battle Game Niche at Seed-Stage Scale

## Executive Summary

Spybird Interactive is an emerging Indian game development studio focused on creating high-quality, culturally resonant card-battling mobile games. Founded in 2024, the company has quickly brought its flagship title, *Moksha*, to market, leveraging the rich mythology of the Mahabharata to differentiate itself in a crowded mobile gaming landscape [1] [2]. Backed by a $279K seed round from notable venture capital firms including Eximius Ventures and DeVC, Spybird is attempting to bridge the gap between accessible mobile gaming and deep, strategic card mechanics [1]. While early traction is promising with over 10,000 downloads, the company faces significant competition and must scale its user base and monetization strategies to secure Series A funding and long-term viability.

## Company Overview

Spybird Interactive Studio Private Limited (CIN: U58203MH2024PTC417360) was incorporated on January 16, 2024, as a private company limited by shares [3]. While the company's registered corporate office is located in Dadar East, Mumbai [1] [3], its core product development and operations are heavily rooted in Pune [4]. 

### Founders & Leadership

The studio's leadership brings a strong blend of international gaming pedigree and specialized technical expertise. Co-founder Neale Hemrajani, who grew up in Mumbai, previously served as a founding member of California-based Annapurna Interactive, a leading indie game publisher known for global hits [4]. He is joined by co-founder Abhishek Kamdar, whose background includes professional experience in sound and gaming, supported by education from the SAE Institute UK [5]. 

## Product Portfolio

Spybird's strategy revolves around building a portfolio of thematic card-battling games, moving away from hyper-casual trends to focus on strategic depth.

### Moksha – Flagship Card-Battle Game

*Moksha* is Spybird's debut mobile game, currently available on the Google Play Store where it has amassed over 10,000 downloads [2]. The game is a fast-paced strategic card battler inspired by the Indian epic, the Mahabharata [2]. 
* **Gameplay Mechanics:** Matches are designed for mobile accessibility, taking approximately two minutes to complete [2]. Players use cards representing legendary figures like Arjuna, Karna, and Bhishma to conquer sacred locations [2].
* **Strategic Depth:** The game emphasizes tactics, bluffing, and timing over brute force, requiring players to predict opponent moves across six battlefield locations [2].

### Upcoming Titles

Spybird's website indicates a pipeline of future releases, teasing upcoming mobile titles named *Yorker* and *Ego*, slated for release in the near future [6]. 

## Business Model & Monetization

While specific revenue figures are not publicly disclosed, Spybird operates within the Entertainment Software and Gaming verticals [1]. As a mobile card-battler, *Moksha* is free to download [2], indicating a freemium business model. This typically relies on in-app purchases (IAPs) for card packs, cosmetic upgrades, and battle passes, though the company must educate the Indian market on card game mechanics to drive conversion [4].

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Accelerate User Acquisition:** With 10K+ downloads [2], Spybird must deploy targeted performance marketing to scale *Moksha*'s player base, which is critical for matchmaking liquidity in PvP card games.
2. **Clarify Brand Positioning:** Consolidate the company's geographic narrative. While registered in Mumbai [3], highlighting the Pune development hub [4] can help attract local gaming talent.
3. **Prepare for Series A:** The $279K seed capital [1] provides a limited runway. Spybird should focus on demonstrating strong Day-1 and Day-7 retention metrics for *Moksha* to secure a larger Series A round to fund the development of *Yorker* and *Ego* [6].
