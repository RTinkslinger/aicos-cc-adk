# Stance Health – Building a Tech-First Musculoskeletal Care Platform in India (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary
Stance Health is an emerging Indian health-tech startup redefining musculoskeletal (MSK) care through an omnichannel approach. Founded in 2024, the Bengaluru-based company integrates physical physiotherapy clinics with a proprietary digital platform to offer structured, technology-driven rehabilitation [1] [2]. Backed by a recent $1 million pre-seed round led by General Catalyst, Stance Health has demonstrated strong early traction, conducting over 12,000 sessions in its first eight months with patient retention rates significantly higher than industry averages [1] [3]. 

## Company Overview & Value Proposition
Stance Health operates as a full-stack MSK health platform that bridges the gap between clinical diagnosis, physiotherapy, and long-term wellness [4] [1]. The company's value proposition lies in its precision-driven rehabilitation framework, which utilizes AI-based motion sensors, objective strength assessments, and structured treatment pathways [5]. 

Patients undergo a structured three-phase recovery model—Reassess, Restore, Reignite—designed to ensure measurable outcomes and prevent recurring issues [1] [5]. By blending offline touchpoints at its physical centers with online engagement, Stance Health provides a holistic care model that contrasts with the highly fragmented and traditional orthopedic ecosystem in India [1] [5].

## Business Model, Traction & Recent Milestones
Stance Health generates revenue through its physical clinic sessions and integrated digital recovery programs. The company launched its first 2,000-square-foot flagship center in HSR Layout, Bengaluru [1] [17] [18]. 

**Key Traction Metrics (2024-2025):**
* Conducted over **12,000 sessions** within the first eight months of launching its pilot center [1] [3].
* Achieved patient retention rates up to **three times higher** than the industry average [3].
* Plans to expand to approximately **six physical centers** by December 2025 and enter a new city by 2026 [1].

The recent $1 million funding will be utilized to scale this model, enhance the proprietary technology platform, and expand the multidisciplinary team [1] [5].
