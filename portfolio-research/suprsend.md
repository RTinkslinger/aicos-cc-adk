# SuprSend — India’s Emerging Notification‑Infrastructure Play (2022‑2026)

## Executive Summary

SuprSend is an early-stage enterprise technology startup providing a centralized notification infrastructure stack for developers. Founded in 2022, the company offers a unified API that allows businesses to manage and scale product notifications across multiple channels, including email, SMS, push, Slack, and in-app inboxes [1] [2]. Despite operating with a lean team and having raised only a single $1.0 million seed round in September 2022 [1] [3], SuprSend has demonstrated strong platform traction, with workflow processing growing at 150% month-over-month and over 100 companies registered on the platform [1]. With reported revenues of $321K as of March 2025 [3] and the impending shutdown of legacy competitors like Twilio Notify [4], SuprSend is strategically positioned to capture migration demand, provided it can overcome its current talent capacity bottlenecks and secure enterprise-tier clients.

## Company Overview & Founding Team

SuprSend operates with a dual geographic footprint: it is headquartered in San Francisco, United States, while maintaining its registered legal entity (SuprSend Technologies Private Limited) in Bengaluru, India [3]. The company was incorporated on July 28, 2022 [3]. 

The startup was launched by co-founders Nikita Navral (an IIT Delhi alumna) and Gaurav Verma (an IIT Bombay alumnus) [1]. The founders built SuprSend after experiencing first-hand the crippling amount of engineering time required to build and maintain notification services in their previous roles [1]. As of July 2024, the company maintained a highly lean operation with an employee count of just two [3], though the team includes senior engineering talent such as Jonathan Hernandez [5].

## Product & Technology

SuprSend provides a central communication stack that enables developers to create, manage, and deliver notifications to end-users without writing extensive custom code [3]. The platform leverages AI to identify the most effective communication channels for user engagement [3]. 

The product roadmap demonstrates a steady cadence of enterprise-focused feature releases:

| Release Date | Feature Update | Strategic Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Aug 2024** | Bulk Preference APIs | Simplifies the migration and management of user preferences within the platform [6]. |
| **Aug 2025** | Message Logs & UI Revamp | Redesigned UI for seamless tracking of notification lifecycles, advanced filtering, and entity-level visibility [6]. |
| **Jan 2026** | Tenancy Social Links Update | Added support for TikTok in tenant social links and updated Twitter to "X" for better visual consistency [6]. |

## Business Model & Financials

SuprSend operates on a B2B Horizontal SaaS model, targeting enterprise tech and developer infrastructure [7]. The company generates revenue by providing its API and infrastructure to product teams. As of March 31, 2025, SuprSend reported a revenue of $321K [3].

The company's funding history is currently limited to a single early-stage round:
* **Date:** September 14, 2022 [1] [7]
* **Stage:** Seed [7]
* **Amount:** $1.00 Million [1] [7]
* **Investors:** Led by BoldCap, with participation from Titan Capital, FortyTwo.VC, All In Capital, and marquee angel investors [1]. 

The founders stated that these funds were earmarked for hiring talent, driving platform innovation, and marketing to developer communities [1].

## Market Landscape & Competitive Position

The notification infrastructure market is highly competitive, with SuprSend ranking 5th among 52 active competitors tracked by Tracxn [3]. A significant market tailwind for SuprSend is the scheduled end-of-life for Twilio Notify on December 31, 2025, which is forcing companies to migrate to alternative multi-channel messaging providers [4].

| Competitor | Founded | Total Funding | Key Focus / Differentiator |
|---|---|---|---|

To capitalize on its 150% MoM workflow growth [1], SuprSend must leverage its AI capabilities and unified API to win enterprise contracts and position itself as the premier migration destination for displaced Twilio Notify customers.
