# Terafac: India's AI‑Vision Welding Retrofit Bet — What to Watch in 2026

## Executive Summary

Terafac is a Chandigarh-based deep-tech startup positioning itself as a "Software-as-a-Skill" provider for industrial automation. Founded in 2021, the company has developed an AI-Vision platform designed to retrofit standard industrial robots, converting them into intelligent, self-adjusting cells capable of handling high-variation manufacturing tasks [1] [2]. Its beachhead product, **WeldT**, targets the welding automation market, aiming to solve the limitations of traditional pre-programmed robots that struggle with part variability [2].

As of early 2026, Terafac is in the seed stage, having raised approximately $750K–$800K from a syndicate of Indian investors including Inuka Capital, Bharat Founders Fund, and Innovation Mission Punjab [3] [2]. The company has set ambitious targets, aiming for 80 deployments across 20 customers and ₹8 crore (approx. $1M) in Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) by FY26 [2].

**Strategic Outlook:**
* **Opportunity:** Terafac addresses a critical gap in Indian manufacturing—the need for flexible automation in high-mix, low-volume environments (e.g., auto components, agri-machinery). Its retrofit model lowers the barrier to entry for SMEs compared to buying new adaptive robot systems.
* **Differentiation:** Unlike traditional seam tracking (e.g., ABB WeldGuide) which relies on electrical impedance (Thru-the-Arc), Terafac uses computer vision to adjust parameters in real-time [2] [4]. This theoretically allows for better handling of fit-up variations, though it must prove superior reliability against mature incumbents.
* **Risks:** The company operates with a very lean team (estimated ~5 employees) relative to its deployment targets [5]. Execution risk is high, particularly in scaling physical deployments and integration across different robot brands (ABB, KUKA, Fanuc).
* **Recommendation:** For investors or partners, the key validation metric is **First-Pass Yield (FPY)** improvement on actual production lines. Due diligence should focus on verifying the "80 deployments" pipeline and stress-testing the vision system's robustness in harsh welding environments (smoke, spatter, lighting changes).

## Product & Technology — Vision-Guided Welding vs. Incumbents

Terafac's core thesis is that vision software can upgrade existing hardware to handle variability that currently requires human intervention.

### WeldT Capabilities and Promises
The flagship product, WeldT, is an AI-Vision platform that automates welding by converting standard robots into "intelligent, self-adjusting machines" [2].
* **Problem Solved:** Standard robots follow a blind path. If a part is slightly misaligned or the gap varies (common in metal fabrication), the weld fails.
* **Solution:** WeldT uses computer vision to detect the seam and orientation of the workpiece in real-time. It adjusts the robot's path and welding parameters to compensate for variations [2].
* **Expansion:** The company plans to extend this "Software-as-a-Skill" approach to other tasks like gluing, painting, and inspection [2].

### Benchmarking Against Incumbents (ABB WeldGuide)
Terafac competes not just with other startups, but with mature OEM solutions. A key benchmark is ABB's **WeldGuide IV**.

| Feature | ABB WeldGuide IV (Incumbent) | Terafac WeldT (Challenger) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technology** | Thru-the-Arc™ tracking (impedance-based) [4] | Computer Vision + AI [2] |
| **Sampling Rate** | 25 kHz (25,000 times/sec) [4] | *Not disclosed (likely frame-rate dependent)* |
| **Precision** | 0.01 mm correction increments [6] | *Not disclosed* |
| **Primary Use** | Heavy welding, cast components, blind spots [4] | High-variation parts, fit-up issues [2] |
| **Hardware Req** | Specialized sensor unit in robot cabinet [7] | Cameras/Vision sensors + Edge Compute |

**Strategic Insight:** ABB's solution is extremely fast (25 kHz) and robust against smoke/arc glare because it uses electrical feedback, not light. Terafac's vision approach must demonstrate it can see through the harsh welding environment (arc glare, smoke) and offer easier setup ("teaching") to win against such established tech.

## Risks & Red Flags

1. **Execution Bandwidth:** With ~5 employees [5] and a target of 80 deployments [2], the company is severely understaffed for the high-touch nature of industrial automation. Deploying hardware often requires on-site calibration, debugging, and training.
2. **Technological Robustness:** Computer vision in welding is notoriously difficult due to extreme lighting changes (arc flash) and smoke. If WeldT fails to maintain tracking during a weld, it causes expensive scrap. The "AI" must be more reliable than a skilled welder.
3. **Integration Complexity:** Retrofitting "standard industrial robots" [2] implies interfacing with closed controllers (e.g., ABB IRC5, Fanuc R-30iB). This is technically challenging and often void of warranty support from the robot OEM.
4. **Data Discrepancies:** Funding dates vary between sources (2024 vs 2025). While common in private markets, it complicates the assessment of runway. The ₹8 Cr ARR target for FY26 is aggressive for a seed-stage company.

## Strategic Recommendations

### For Investors
* **Verify the Pipeline:** Ask for evidence of the "20 customers" and "80 deployments." Are these paid pilots, LOIs, or full production contracts?
* **Technical Due Diligence:** Request a head-to-head comparison of WeldT vs. a standard laser seam tracker or Thru-Arc sensor. Measure **First-Pass Yield (FPY)** and **Setup Time** (time to program a new part).
* **Scalability Check:** How does the team plan to support 80 installations? If they don't have a strong System Integrator (SI) partner network, support costs could crush margins.

### For Potential Customers
* **Start with a Pilot:** Test WeldT on a high-mix, low-volume part where manual welding is currently a bottleneck.
* **Demand Interoperability:** Ensure the system works with your existing robot fleet (e.g., if you have Yaskawa, ensure WeldT supports it fully).
* **Assess Support:** Given the small team size, define clear SLAs for downtime support.

### For Terafac Management
* **Focus on Welding:** Resist the urge to expand to "gluing, painting, inspection" [2] until the welding product has unassailable product-market fit and referenceable case studies.
* **Build the SI Channel:** Partner with regional robot integrators who can handle the physical installation and first-line support, allowing the core team to focus on software.
