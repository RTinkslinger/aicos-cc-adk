# Tractor Factory: Unlocking the Indian Used-Tractor Market

## Executive Summary
Tractor Factory is an emerging Indian agritech startup operating an omnichannel marketplace for pre-owned tractors and farm equipment [1]. Headquartered in Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh, the company aims to democratize farm mechanization for small and mid-income farmers who are often priced out of the new equipment market [2] [1]. By offering a structured platform with warranties, financing options, and a buyback guarantee, Tractor Factory addresses the severe trust deficit in the unorganized used-tractor market [3] [1]. Backed by a recent ₹4 crore ($500K) pre-seed funding round led by All In Capital in January 2025, the company is aggressively scaling its technology and physical hub network to capture a growing share of India's booming agricultural machinery sector [4].

## Product and Service Portfolio
Tractor Factory operates a hybrid digital-physical (omnichannel) marketplace that facilitates the buying, selling, and financing of second-hand tractors [2] [5]. The platform is designed to remove the friction and uncertainty typically associated with broker-driven transactions [1]. 

Key features of their service include:
* **Omnichannel Discovery:** Farmers can browse over 40 fully inspected tractor models online and book test drives either at their homes or at designated Tractor Factory Hubs [3].
* **Trust and Quality Assurances:** Every "Tractor Factory Assured®" vehicle comes with a no-questions-asked 5-day money-back guarantee [3].
* **Post-Sale Protection:** The company provides a free 6-month to one-year comprehensive service warranty, alongside an Assured Resale Value (Buyback Guarantee) [3].

## Founding Team
The company was founded by Shailesh Tiwari, an IIT and IIM alumnus with over a decade of deep domain experience in the tractor and farm equipment industry [1]. His extensive background allows the company to navigate the complex logistics and valuation challenges inherent in the heavy agricultural machinery space. While PitchBook and LinkedIn list the company's founding year as 2022, recent press coverage highlights its formal market launch and incorporation activities scaling up in 2024 [2] [6] [1].

## Business Model and Market Position
Tractor Factory generates revenue through a transaction-based marketplace model, capturing value via commissions on tractor sales, financing spreads, and value-added services like warranties and repairs [3] [2]. The founder has publicly stated an ambitious target to generate ₹1,000 crores in revenue over the next three years [1].

The company's market position is highly strategic. With over 95% of Indian farmers falling into the middle-to-low income category, the inability to afford new tractors severely limits national agricultural productivity [1]. Tractor Factory competes with other digital platforms like TractorGuru and Khetigaadi, but differentiates itself through its heavy emphasis on physical inspection hubs, comprehensive warranties, and buyback guarantees [3] [5]. 

## Recent News and Macro Tailwinds
Tractor Factory's growth is supported by strong macroeconomic tailwinds in the Indian agricultural sector. As of early 2026, the domestic tractor industry is experiencing robust expansion. In January 2026, retail tractor sales reached 114,759 units, representing a 22.9% year-on-year growth [8]. This momentum continued into February 2026, with total industry volumes hitting 88,545 units, a 31% increase compared to February 2025 [9]. This surging demand for farm mechanization, combined with the company's recent capital infusion, positions Tractor Factory to rapidly scale its operations across rural India.
