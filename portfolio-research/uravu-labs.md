# Uravu Labs – Turning Thin Air into Scalable, Climate-Resilient Water for India and Beyond

## Executive Summary
Uravu Labs is a Bengaluru-based climate-tech startup pioneering a highly efficient, renewable-energy-powered Atmospheric Water Generation (AWG) system. By utilizing proprietary liquid desiccants and waste heat rather than energy-intensive refrigeration cycles, Uravu produces high-quality drinking water directly from the air [1] [2]. Having raised $6.07 million across multiple funding rounds [3], the company has successfully commercialized its "FromAir" technology, initially targeting the hospitality and beverage sectors before expanding into industrial applications like data center cooling [1] [4]. With a modular design capable of scaling to industrial capacities, Uravu Labs is uniquely positioned to address global water scarcity while helping large-scale enterprises achieve "water-positive" ESG mandates.

## Product & Technology
Uravu’s core technology, FromAir®, departs from traditional AWG systems that rely on electricity-heavy condensation coils. Instead, it uses proprietary liquid salts (desiccants) to absorb moisture from the air, which is then heated using renewable sources (solar, biomass) or industrial waste heat to release and collect pure water [5] [1] [2]. 

This approach yields significant operational advantages:
* **Energy Efficiency:** The system consumes 60–80% less electricity than equivalent chiller-based AWG systems [4].
* **Modular Scalability:** The technology is highly modular. A standard 150 kW module produces 3,000 liters per day (LPD), and modules can be stacked linearly for multi-megawatt deployments (e.g., 30 modules for 90,000 LPD) [4]. The company currently operates a flagship 4,000 LPD plant that runs 24/7 [5].
* **Data Center Integration:** Uravu's system can harness reject heat (30–65°C) from data center immersion cooling loops to drive water generation, effectively reducing chiller loads and enabling a "negative Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE)" [4].

## Founding Team
The company was founded in 2019 by a team of engineers driven by firsthand experiences with water scarcity. Swapnil Shrivastav (CEO) and Venkatesh RY (COO) met at NIT Calicut, where a severe 2016 drought rationed residents to just two buckets of water a day [6] [7] [8]. They were joined by Govinda Balaji (CPO) and Pardeep Garg (CTO) to commercialize the technology [9] [10].

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape
The AWG market includes several global and domestic players, but Uravu differentiates itself through its desiccant-based, heat-driven approach.

| Competitor | Core Technology | Target Market / Scale | Key Differentiator vs. Uravu |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------------|------------------------------|
| **Uravu Labs** | Liquid Desiccant + Waste Heat | Commercial, Industrial, Data Centers | 60-80% lower electricity use; utilizes waste heat [2] [4] |
| **Watergen** | Refrigerant-Coil (Condensation) | Global / Large-scale | Highly electricity-dependent [14] [2] |
| **Aeronero** | Atmospheric Water Generation | India / Mid-scale | Competes in the domestic AWG space [15] |
| **SOURCE Global**| Hydropanel (Solar-driven) | Off-grid / Remote | Relies strictly on solar panels, less modular for industrial waste heat [15] |

*Takeaway:* Uravu’s ability to integrate with data centers and utilize waste heat provides a distinct competitive moat against traditional, energy-intensive AWG competitors.

## Business Model
Uravu Labs operates a B2B model, primarily functioning as a "Water-as-a-Service" (WaaS) provider. 
* **Pricing:** The renewable water is priced competitively with mineral water, at approximately $0.078 per liter, with costs expected to decrease as operations scale [16].
* **Customer Segments:** The company initially targeted the hospitality sector, securing 40 clients who use the water for customer drinking supplies [6]. They also provide sustainable packaged beverages for the F&B industry [5] [17].
* **Industrial Expansion:** Uravu is actively expanding into on-site commercial and high-capacity industrial plants, offering performance-based cooling and water generation contracts for data centers [5] [4].

## Recent News & Strategic Outlook
In mid-2025, Uravu Labs successfully raised $1.2 million in a Pre-Series A round led by Japanese VC firm Enrission India Capital [12] [18]. This partnership is expected to accelerate global community and industrial deployments [1]. Furthermore, the company is planning its first international pilot project in Japan, which will serve as a critical reference point for overseas expansion [10]. Looking ahead to 2026, Uravu is heavily promoting its data center solutions, aiming to run pilot projects in India and Singapore to capture waste heat and drastically reduce freshwater consumption in the IT infrastructure sector [6] [4].
