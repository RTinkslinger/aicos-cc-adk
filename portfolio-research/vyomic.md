# VyomIC (formerly AeroDome): Building India's First Private LEO-PNT Constellation

## Executive Summary
VyomIC (formerly AeroDome) is a Bengaluru-based deep-tech startup developing India's first private Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite constellation for Positioning, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) services [1] [2]. Founded in 2023 by IIT Madras alumni, the company aims to provide a sovereign, spoofing-proof alternative to legacy Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSS) like GPS and NavIC [2] [3]. Having recently secured $1.6 million in pre-seed funding, VyomIC is strategically positioned to capitalize on the growing global demand for resilient, high-precision navigation infrastructure across defense, telecommunications, and autonomous mobility sectors [2] [3]. 

## Product and Service
VyomIC is engineering a constellation of LEO satellites designed to deliver highly accurate, secure, and sovereign PNT services [1]. Legacy GNSS systems broadcasting from over 20,000 kilometers away are increasingly vulnerable to jamming and spoofing [3]. By operating in Low Earth Orbit, VyomIC's technology fundamentally changes the operating envelope, offering significant technical advantages over traditional Medium Earth Orbit (MEO) and Geosynchronous Equatorial Orbit (GEO) systems [2] [3].

| Technical Capability | Performance Metric / Advantage | Strategic Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Signal Strength** | 100–1000x stronger than legacy MEO/GEO signals [3] | Significantly less vulnerable to jamming and interference [3] |
| **Positioning Accuracy** | Centimeter-level precision [1] [3] | Essential for autonomous vehicles, drone swarms, and battlefield robotics [3] |
| **Timing Precision** | Nanosecond-level timing [1] [3] | Critical for synchronizing telecom networks and real-time financial systems [2] [3] |
| **Security** | Spoofing-proof, encrypted signals [1] [3] | Ensures mission-critical reliability in contested or GNSS-denied zones [3] |

These capabilities provide faster convergence and lower spoofing coherence time, making the system highly resilient against electromagnetic spectrum warfare [2] [3].

## Founding Team
VyomIC was founded by Lokesh Kabdal, Vibhor Jain, and Anurag Patil, three alumni of the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras [4] [2]. The founders possess a strong execution track record in complex hardware engineering. They previously led Avishkar Hyperloop, India's premier student hyperloop initiative, where they secured $1.25 million in funding and built a 422-meter vacuum tube test facility [2] [3]. Following this, they commercialized massive swarm drone systems, operating a fleet of 60 drones, which exposed them firsthand to the critical limitations and vulnerabilities of existing GNSS infrastructure [2] [3].

## Funding History
The company is currently in its early stages of capitalization. In October 2025, VyomIC raised $1.6 million (₹14 Cr) in a pre-seed funding round [2] [5]. The round was led by Speciale Invest, a seed-stage venture capital firm focused on deep tech, alongside one other undisclosed investor [2] [5] [3]. This capital is earmarked for finalizing the PNT payload architecture, building the core engineering team, and laying the groundwork for a spaceborne technology demonstration mission [3].

## Market Position and Business Model
VyomIC operates at the intersection of space technology and critical national infrastructure. The global navigation landscape is facing a crisis, with over 430,000 spoofing and jamming incidents reported in 2024 alone—a 62% year-on-year spike [3]. Furthermore, India's indigenous NavIC program has achieved less than 50% satellite deployment and faces adoption hurdles due to its use of the S-band [3]. 

VyomIC's business model capitalizes on the industry shift from hardware sales to "Navigation-as-a-Service" [3]. The company employs a dual-use design strategy, optimizing its constellation for both civilian precision (finance, mobility, telecom) and strategic defense applications (encrypted signals, jamming resistance) [3]. By 2033, global GNSS revenues are expected to exceed €580 billion, with 80% of that value concentrated in software, services, and augmentation layers [3].

## Recent News
The company recently rebranded from AeroDome to VyomIC (combining "Vyom," Sanskrit for space, and "IC" for Indian Constellation) to better reflect its mission [2] [3]. In early 2026, VyomIC has been highly active in industry leadership. Team member Shivam Singh represented the company at the Broadcast Engineering Society (India) Expo 2026 in a panel on "Make in India, Make for the World," discussing the critical nature of precise timing for telecom networks [2]. Additionally, co-founder Vibhor Jain participated in a panel on resilient PNT at the GeoBuiz Summit 2026 in Denver, Colorado, alongside representatives from Iridium and Xairos, highlighting the global necessity for LEO-PNT solutions amid escalating GNSS degradation in regions like the Middle East [2].
