# Workatoms (Rilo): Disrupting the $200K Knowledge Worker Market with Autonomous AI Agents

## Executive Summary
Workatoms Inc., operating under the brand name Rilo, is a 2025-founded AI startup that transforms plain text descriptions into reliable, production-ready workflows [1] [2]. Headquartered in San Francisco with a strong operational footprint in Bangalore, the company builds autonomous AI agents that execute complex business tasks end-to-end [3] [4]. Unlike traditional AI copilots that merely suggest actions, Rilo delivers "finished work" across sales, finance, and HR operations [5] [6]. Backed by prominent investors like Peak XV and Day Zero Ventures, Workatoms is rapidly scaling its usage-based automation platform to eliminate manual operational drag for modern enterprises [5] [7].

## Product & Service: The "Finished Work" Philosophy
Rilo's core value proposition is delivering completed tasks rather than AI-generated suggestions [5]. Users assign tasks in plain English via Slack, and Rilo logs into existing web apps to execute the work without requiring new UIs, coding, or babysitting [5]. 

* **Browser Vision & Deterministic Workflows:** Rilo operates using browser vision, allowing it to drive applications like Salesforce and NetSuite without relying on brittle APIs [5]. It turns CRM logic, lead enrichment, and revenue reporting into deterministic, production-grade AI workflows [8].
* **The Compounding Knowledge Flywheel:** Every task makes Rilo faster. It records what it learns—such as menu navigation and required fields—so that a task taking 8 steps and 52 seconds on day one can be reduced to just 3 steps and 8.5 seconds by week 12 [5].

## Founding Team: IIT Bombay & LinkedIn Pedigree
Workatoms leverages a highly effective cross-border talent strategy, combining San Francisco's market access with India's elite engineering prowess [3] [4].

## Business Model: The $5/Hour Digital Employee
Workatoms disrupts traditional SaaS seat-based licensing with a transparent, usage-based pricing model [5]. Customers pay **$5 per agent-hour**—billed only for the time Rilo actively spends executing a task [5]. There are no platform fees or seat licenses, aligning costs directly with business value [5]. 

To support enterprise adoption, Rilo's architecture features fail-closed access, audit logging, and optional human approval gates, ensuring that misconfigurations result in blocked tasks rather than data leaks [5] [13].
