# WorkOnGrid (aka Grid): AI-Powered Utility Ops Platform – Growth Levers & Headwinds in India's Smart-Meter Wave (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary

* **Rapid Utility Digitisation:** India's smart-meter rollout is accelerating, with Grid now supporting over 9.6 million meters [1]. A recent milestone includes Adani's smart metering business crossing 1 crore (10 million) installations [2].
* **AI-First Product Suite:** Grid has evolved into a deep, multi-agent intelligence layer for utilities, featuring GridAI (a multilingual copilot) and advanced OCR for meter validation [3] [1].
* **Modest Funding vs. Broad Ambition:** Despite a comprehensive product suite, Grid has raised only $835K to date [4]. This presents a scaling risk against well-funded competitors in the broader workflow automation space.
* **Strategic Expansion:** A 2025 partnership with Innorix opens doors to the Southeast Asian market [5], while domestic showcases at the India AI Impact Summit (2025) and India Energy Week (2026) highlight its growing industry presence [1].

## 1. Company Snapshot – What WorkOnGrid Does

WorkOnGrid (legally registered as Pragyaam Data Technologies Private Limited) provides an AI-driven operational intelligence platform tailored for electricity, water, and gas utilities [3] [6]. Headquartered in Bengaluru with a registered office in Ranchi, India, the company digitises frontline operations and integrates OT/IT systems [4] [7].

### 1.1 Core Product Suite
The platform offers a no-code workforce and asset management system, a Smart Metering Operations Center (SMOC) for real-time SLA tracking, and a utility-grade data warehouse [7]. Its flagship AI engine, GridAI, provides multilingual copilot capabilities (English, Hindi, regional languages) and advanced image intelligence (OCR) to validate meter photos, detect fraud, and automate billing [3] [1].

## 2. Founding Team & Leadership

Grid was founded in 2017 by a four-person team blending data engineering and operational expertise [4].

## 7. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Accelerate Fundraising:** Grid must leverage its 9.6M+ meter traction to raise a $5M-$10M Series A round. This capital is critical to expand the engineering team and defend against well-funded AI workflow competitors.
2. **Double Down on GridAI:** The proprietary OCR and multilingual capabilities are Grid's strongest differentiators [3]. Marketing should heavily index on these features to win state-level discom contracts.
3. **Expand OEM Partnerships:** Formalising co-sell agreements with smart meter manufacturers (like Adani) will reduce customer acquisition costs and embed Grid's software directly into hardware rollouts [2].
