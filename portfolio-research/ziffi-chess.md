# Ziffi Chess: Fast-Chess Disruption in India – Funding Surge, User Traction & Monetisation Challenges

## Executive Summary
Ziffi Chess is an innovative Indian mobile gaming startup that has reimagined traditional chess for the micro-attention era through a unique two-minute, six-move format [1] [2]. Founded in 2023 by former Blinkit executives, the Gurgaon-based company recently secured a $5 million Series A round led by Tanglin Venture Partners, pushing its valuation to approximately INR 206 crore [1] [3]. Despite achieving over 1 million downloads on the Google Play Store, the company faces significant monetization hurdles following India's regulatory crackdown on real-money gaming, which forced a pivot from paid entry fees to a free-to-play model [1] [4] [3]. Ziffi Chess must now bridge the gap between its high user acquisition and its early-stage revenue generation to sustain its growth trajectory.

## Product and Service Innovation
Ziffi Chess differentiates itself by stripping away slow opening theories and dropping players directly into tactical battles. The platform features a unique 6-move format where every match finishes in just two minutes [4] [2]. 

Key gameplay mechanics include:
* **Randomized Starts:** All games begin from randomized, balanced middle-game positions [2].
* **Point System:** Players score points for every piece captured (e.g., Queen is 9 points, Rook is 5 points) [2].
* **Win Conditions:** Players win by either checkmating the opponent or scoring more points within the 6-move limit [2].
* **Arena Progression:** Users climb a competitive ladder from the "Pawn Pit" up to the "Grand Master’s Gate" [4].

As of early 2026, the app has surpassed 1 million downloads on Google Play, maintaining a 3.5-star rating across 4.38K reviews [4]. 

## Founding Team
Ziffi Chess was founded in 2023 by Akshat Bansal and Aditya Dubey [1] [2]. Both founders are alumni of the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IIT-B) [1] [3]. Prior to launching Ziffi Chess, Bansal and Dubey worked together as senior leaders driving the quick-commerce pivot at Blinkit [1] [2] [3]. Their background in rapid scaling and operational execution has been instrumental in Ziffi's fast product rollout and subsequent funding success. Following the Series A allotment, the two co-founders retain a combined 53.54% stake in the company [1] [3].

## Market Position and Recent News
Ziffi Chess occupies a distinct niche in the Indian mobile gaming landscape by targeting casual gamers who want skill-based competition without the time commitment of traditional chess [4] [2]. In September 2025, the company made headlines with its INR 44.2 crore Series A funding announcement [1] [3]. The capital injection is expected to fuel user acquisition and product development as the company seeks to solidify its first-mover advantage in the "micro-chess" category against broader platforms like Chess.com and local competitors.

## Business Model and Monetization Challenges
Ziffi Chess initially operated on a real-money gaming model, charging users an entry fee starting from INR 10 per match [1] [3]. However, following the Indian government's blanket ban and heavy taxation on real-money online gaming, the company was forced to pivot to a free-to-play model [1] [3]. 

Currently, matches can be played for free, and the platform hosts tournament modes featuring a modest prize pool of INR 350 [1] [3]. This regulatory shock heavily impacted early monetization. In its first year of operations (FY24), Ziffi Chess recorded an operating revenue of just INR 1.5 lakh against expenses of INR 87.65 lakh, resulting in a net loss of INR 86 lakh [1] [3]. To achieve profitability, Ziffi Chess will need to aggressively explore alternative revenue streams, such as in-app advertising, brand-sponsored tournaments, and virtual cosmetic economies.
