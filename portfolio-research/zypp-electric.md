# Zypp Electric: India's Fast-Moving EV-as-a-Service Player - Funding, Fleet, and the Road to Profitability (2024-26)

## Executive Summary

Zypp Electric has established itself as a dominant force in India's rapidly expanding electric vehicle (EV) last-mile delivery sector. Operating an asset-light, tech-enabled EV-as-a-service model, the company has scaled its fleet to over 22,000 vehicles as of late 2025 [1]. Despite widening net losses of Rs 107.5 crore in FY25, Zypp achieved a 50% year-over-year revenue jump to Rs 438 crore, driven by the booming quick-commerce and e-commerce logistics markets [2]. Backed by $76.5 million in total funding, including recent strategic investments from Japanese conglomerate ENEOS, Zypp is now aggressively expanding into Tier-2 Indian cities and eyeing Southeast Asian markets [3] [2] [4]. 

## Company Overview & Value Proposition

Founded in 2017, Gurugram-based Zypp Electric operates India's leading tech-enabled EV rental and delivery platform [5]. The company's core mission is to decarbonize last-mile logistics through an ecosystem of electric two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and battery-swapping infrastructure [5] [4]. 

Zypp's value proposition serves two primary stakeholders:
* **Enterprise Clients:** Zypp provides zero-emission logistics solutions to e-commerce, grocery, and food delivery giants, reducing delivery costs and carbon footprints [5]. 
* **Gig Workers:** The platform empowers delivery executives by eliminating fluctuating petrol costs through an accessible rental model, allowing pilot partners to earn up to Rs 30,000–Rs 40,000 monthly [5].

The entire operation is underpinned by FleetEase.ai, an AI-driven fleet management system that tracks batteries, optimizes routes, and manages a network of over 1,500 battery swap points across India [5] [1].

## Business Model, Financials & Traction

Zypp operates a dual-segment revenue model. Revenue from B2B delivery services (serving clients like Zomato, Swiggy, Zepto, and BigBasket) accounts for 74% of operating revenue, while gig-worker vehicle rentals make up the remaining 28% [2] [4]. 

The company has demonstrated explosive top-line growth, though capital efficiency remains a challenge due to high operational and rider expenses.

| Financial Metric | FY24 | FY25 | YoY Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Operating Revenue** | Rs 293 Crore | Rs 438 Crore | +50% [2] |
| **Total Expenses** | Rs 392 Crore | Rs 556 Crore | +42% [2] |
| **Net Loss** | Rs 89.5 Crore | Rs 107.5 Crore | Widened by 20% [2] |
| **EBITDA Margin** | -19.3% | -15.98% | Improved by 3.32% [2] [11] |

*Traction Metrics:* Zypp facilitates 5 million deliveries every month and has completed over 165 million emission-free deliveries cumulatively, saving 62 million kilograms of carbon emissions [5] [4]. 

## Market Position & Competitive Landscape

Zypp operates at the intersection of two massive growth curves: India's $15 billion intracity logistics market and the booming EV sector [12]. India's express logistics shipments are projected to grow at a 19-23% CAGR to reach 29 billion by FY 2030, driven by deep-tier e-commerce penetration [13]. Simultaneously, India's electric two-wheeler segment achieved record retail sales of 1.28 million units in 2025 [14].

**Competitive Differentiation:**
While competitors like Yulu focus heavily on consumer mobility and shared rentals, Zypp has carved out a dominant B2B niche. Yulu reported Rs 237.4 crore in FY25 revenue with a Rs 126 crore loss [2]. Zypp outpaces Yulu in pure revenue (Rs 438 crore) by deeply integrating its FleetEase.ai software with major quick-commerce platforms and expanding its fleet to include electric three-wheelers, which now contribute 10% of total revenue [2] [4].

## Recent Milestones & Strategic Outlook (2024-2026)

Zypp has aggressively expanded its footprint and operational capacity between 2024 and early 2026:

* **Tier-2 City Expansion (Jan 2026):** Zypp successfully launched operations in Hyderabad and Jaipur, deploying 1,000 EVs in each city within a single month. The company plans to scale its fleet to 15,000 units in these regions within the next financial year [1].
* **Mega-Partnerships:** Zypp is executing a massive association with Zomato to deploy 1 lakh e-scooters by 2024, aligning with Zomato's commitment to go completely electric by 2030 [15].
* **Long-Term Fleet Goals:** From a base of 22,000 EVs in late 2025, Zypp aims to expand its deployment to over 100,000 vehicles across 25 cities by 2030 [1].

*Strategic Recommendation:* To achieve its goal of full EBITDA profitability by FY26, Zypp must focus on reducing its cost-to-serve—specifically the Rs 355 crore spent on production and transportation in FY25 [2]. Monetizing its AI tech stack as a SaaS offering to third-party fleet operators could provide the high-margin revenue needed to offset the heavy capital expenditures of physical fleet expansion [11].
