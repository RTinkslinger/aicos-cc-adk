## Competitive Landscape

**Terafac's Niche:** Unlike Doosan or Agile which build robots/controllers, Terafac appears to be a **retrofit** play—making *existing* dumb robots smart. This is a capital-efficient wedge if they can solve the integration challenges across different robot brands.

## Risks & Red Flags

1. **Execution Bandwidth:** With ~5 employees [5] and a target of 80 deployments [2], the company is severely understaffed for the high-touch nature of industrial automation. Deploying hardware often requires on-site calibration, debugging, and training.
2. **Technological Robustness:** Computer vision in welding is notoriously difficult due to extreme lighting changes (arc flash) and smoke. If WeldT fails to maintain tracking during a weld, it causes expensive scrap. The "AI" must be more reliable than a skilled welder.
3. **Integration Complexity:** Retrofitting "standard industrial robots" [2] implies interfacing with closed controllers (e.g., ABB IRC5, Fanuc R-30iB). This is technically challenging and often void of warranty support from the robot OEM.
4. **Data Discrepancies:** Funding dates vary between sources (2024 vs 2025). While common in private markets, it complicates the assessment of runway. The ₹8 Cr ARR target for FY26 is aggressive for a seed-stage company.

## Strategic Recommendations

### For Investors
* **Verify the Pipeline:** Ask for evidence of the "20 customers" and "80 deployments." Are these paid pilots, LOIs, or full production contracts?
* **Technical Due Diligence:** Request a head-to-head comparison of WeldT vs. a standard laser seam tracker or Thru-Arc sensor. Measure **First-Pass Yield (FPY)** and **Setup Time** (time to program a new part).
* **Scalability Check:** How does the team plan to support 80 installations? If they don't have a strong System Integrator (SI) partner network, support costs could crush margins.

### For Potential Customers
* **Start with a Pilot:** Test WeldT on a high-mix, low-volume part where manual welding is currently a bottleneck.
* **Demand Interoperability:** Ensure the system works with your existing robot fleet (e.g., if you have Yaskawa, ensure WeldT supports it fully).
* **Assess Support:** Given the small team size, define clear SLAs for downtime support.

### For Terafac Management
* **Focus on Welding:** Resist the urge to expand to "gluing, painting, inspection" [2] until the welding product has unassailable product-market fit and referenceable case studies.
* **Build the SI Channel:** Partner with regional robot integrators who can handle the physical installation and first-line support, allowing the core team to focus on software.
